Our guiding principle
No single reviewer gets the final word.

Every reviewer brings something valuable, and every reviewer has blind spots. One creator might be excellent on driving dynamics but less rigorous on long-term reliability. Another might be a careful technical analyst who doesn't notice when a car's cabin feel is off. A third might be deeply attuned to what a family buyer needs, but less so for the enthusiast.

That's why we don't build verdicts from one voice. We deliberately bring together creators with different strengths, different audiences, and different ways of evaluating cars, so that each reviewer's perspective is balanced and checked by the others. The result is a verdict that is more complete, more neutral, and more reliable than any single review could be.

Where the jury agrees, we say so clearly. Where they disagree, we show you the split, and let you weigh it yourself.

Different strengths
Each jury member is selected for what they do best: dynamics, ownership data, EV range, budget value, real-world India conditions. No one reviewer covers all of it equally well.
Deliberate diversity of view
We intentionally include voices that sometimes disagree. Consensus is useful. Dissent is more useful. A jury that always agrees isn't really deliberating.
Weighted, not averaged
We don't just add up scores and divide. A reviewer's weight on a particular dimension is based on how closely that dimension matches their documented strength.
Splits shown, not hidden
When jury members genuinely disagree, we surface the split in the article. The reader sees where the jury landed and where it was divided, so the decision stays theirs.
A note on who is not on the jury

We exclude any review where the creator attended a manufacturer-funded press drive, received a vehicle on loan with conditions attached, or where commercial relationships with the brand are disclosed or suspected but undisclosed. If a creator publishes both independent and sponsored content, only their independent reviews are included in our scoring. The jury is only as trustworthy as its independence.

Our methodology

How the jury reaches its verdict

1
We identify the jury

For each car, we identify every independent creator who published a substantive review, excluding press-trip content, brand-commissioned pieces, and spec-sheet summaries.

2
We balance across many voices

Each review is read in full and scored across five dimensions. No single reviewer dominates the verdict: scores are weighted by each creator's documented strengths. Where creators disagree, the split is shown, not smoothed over.

3
The verdict is rendered

The aggregate findings become the verdict: a score, a Buy, Wait, or Skip recommendation, and the decisive factors a buyer should actually weigh. The jury speaks. You decide.
