Every verdict on The Car Jury is based on the analysis of 2–7 independent creators from this panel. No single reviewer. No house view.

PRINCIPLES

How the jury thinks

  • 01

    Independence is binary

    A channel with one sponsored review is a sponsored channel. Jury membership requires a clean track record — no manufacturer loans, no exclusive partnerships, no paid content. No exceptions.

  • 02

    Ownership over press launches

    The first week of driving tells you almost nothing about a car you'll own for seven years. Jury members who cover long-term usage, real-world fuel economy, and post-warranty reliability are weighted accordingly.

  • 03

    Dissent is part of the verdict

    Manufactured consensus is not a verdict. Three of seven creators disagreeing significantly means the verdict says so — it does not paper over the disagreement with a single number.

ELIGIBILITY

Who doesn't qualify

  • Media outlets
    Channels affiliated with print or broadcast media organisations are excluded. The jury is independent operators only.
  • Channels with manufacturer relationships
    Any channel that has accepted a car loan, exclusive content partnership, or OEM sponsorship is disqualified for 24 months from the date of the relationship.
  • Channels without a review track record
    A minimum 2-year track record of in-depth, real-world reviews is required. First-look and spec-sheet channels are excluded.
  • Single-car brand specialists
    Channels that only review one manufacturer's cars — regardless of independence — introduce structural bias that skews cross-model verdicts.

METHODOLOGY

How a verdict is made

  • 01

    We identify the jury

    Only independent creators with no active manufacturer relationships, a minimum 2-year track record, and documented real-world testing are selected. Paid content or sponsored reviews disqualify a channel entirely.

  • 02

    We balance across many voices

    A minimum of two creators must have reviewed a car before a verdict is published. For popular models, we draw from seven or more independent assessments. No single reviewer controls the outcome.

  • 03

    We render the verdict

    Each creator's assessment is weighted by depth of analysis, not follower count. We synthesise findings into a single scored verdict, noting dissent where it exists.
