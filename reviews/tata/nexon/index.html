Tata Nexon: The Jury's Verdict

BUY

A genuinely well-rounded compact SUV with class-leading safety, sharp design, and strong feature value, held back only by minor refinement and fit-finish niggles.

By The Car Jury Editorial Published 6 May 2026 Synthesis of 4 independent sources 1,876 words · 8 min read

The 2025 Tata Nexon builds on India's best-selling compact SUV with a heavily redesigned exterior, a tech-forward cabin, and a 5-star Global NCAP safety rating. With prices from Rs 8.10 lakh to around Rs 15 lakh ex-showroom, petrol and diesel powertrains, and an aggressive feature list, it remains one of the most compelling buys in the sub-4-metre SUV space.

What Works

  • Striking, contemporary design that stands out in the compact SUV segment
  • Class-leading safety credentials with 5-star Global NCAP rating and six airbags
  • Plush ride quality and confident high-speed manners
  • Loaded feature list including 360-degree camera, ventilated seats, JBL audio and connected car tech
  • Wide engine and gearbox choice including a refined 1.5L diesel and 7-speed DCT

Watch Out For

  • Three-cylinder petrol has noticeable turbo lag and inconsistent low-end response
  • Diesel engine noise filters into the cabin more than expected
  • Limited cubby storage up front and tight rear headroom for taller occupants
  • Touchscreen and capacitive HVAC panel quality could be better; minor camera lag reported

Design

The Nexon's facelift is the most dramatic visual overhaul the nameplate has received, and it works. Designed at Tata's Marin Lariche studio, the SUV now wears slim bi-functional LED headlamps, a sequential LED DRL strip running across the bonnet, a closed-off grille with the new Tata logo, and tri-arrow elements borrowed from the Nexon EV. At the rear, a connected LED light bar with X-shaped tail lamps and sequential indicators gives it a genuinely premium signature. Dimensions stay close to before at roughly 4 metres long with 208 mm of ground clearance and 16-inch diamond-cut alloys on the top variants. Colour options include Flame Red, Pristine White, Daytona Grey, Fearless Purple and Ocean Blue, with dual-tone roofs available. As MotorBeam observes, it finally sheds any resemblance to a grown-up Indica and looks unmistakably modern. The coupe-like rising shoulder line divides opinion, but most agree this is among the most attention-grabbing designs in the segment.

Interior & Features

Step inside and the Nexon feels like a generational leap rather than a facelift. A three-tone dashboard with leatherette inserts, a 10.25-inch floating Harman touchscreen, a 10.25-inch fully digital instrument cluster with a Land Rover-inspired layout, and a capacitive touch panel for HVAC headline the cabin upgrades. Namaste Car notes the new infotainment is the single biggest highlight inside, supporting wireless Android Auto and Apple CarPlay, 200+ voice commands in six languages, and a clear 360-degree camera feed (with minor real-time lag). Ventilated front seats, a wireless charger, an air purifier, an electric sunroof, an 8-speaker JBL system with subwoofer, and ambient lighting lift the ambience considerably. Storage up front is limited and the centre console lacks proper cupholders. Rear seat comfort has improved thanks to a more reclined backrest and a touch more headroom, though anyone above 5'8" may still find headroom and legroom tight. Boot space is a practical 382 litres with a full-size spare wheel.

Performance & Powertrain

The Nexon offers one of the widest powertrain menus in its class. The 1.2-litre three-cylinder turbo-petrol Revotron now produces 120 PS and 170 Nm, paired with a 5-speed MT, 6-speed MT, 6-speed AMT or 7-speed DCT (with shift-by-wire, wet clutch and active cooling). The 1.5-litre four-cylinder Revotorq diesel makes 113 PS and 260 Nm with 6-speed MT or AMT options. Three drive modes (Eco, City, Sport) noticeably alter throttle response, and the Eco mode is now usably tuned rather than lethargic. The petrol's mid-range between 2,500 and 5,500 rpm is genuinely punchy, but turbo lag below 2,000 rpm and a few flat spots make low-speed driving less smooth than ideal. The diesel is the more relaxed long-distance choice, though its clatter does seep into the cabin. Claimed efficiency stands at 17.4 kmpl for the petrol MT, 16 kmpl for the petrol AMT and around 22 kmpl for the diesel.

Ride Quality & Handling

Ride and handling remain the Nexon's strongest mechanical suit. The MacPherson strut front and semi-independent twist-beam rear setup, with coil springs and a stabiliser bar, soaks up broken urban surfaces with minimal fuss. At low speeds it feels mildly firm but never jarring, and as pace rises the suspension settles beautifully, making it a confident high-speed cruiser on Indian highways. Body control through corners is impressive for a compact SUV, with the Bridgestone Turanza T001 215/60 R16 tyres offering reassuring grip. The steering weights up nicely as speeds build and feels well calibrated for both city traffic and faster sweeping bends. Ground clearance of 208 mm is enough for most rural roads and speed breakers. Front disc and rear drum brakes deliver progressive bite, although the absence of rear discs is a missed opportunity at this price. The thick A-pillar can create a small blind spot at junctions, but otherwise outward visibility is good thanks to the high-set driving position.

Build Quality & Technology

Tata has clearly worked on perceived quality, and it shows in the soft-touch dashboard inserts, leatherette upholstery on top variants, and the solid feel of the new two-spoke illuminated steering wheel. That said, fit and finish still trails the segment's best in places: the capacitive HVAC panel feels less premium than it looks, and a few panel gaps could be tighter. On the safety and tech front, the Nexon is genuinely class-leading, with six airbags, ESP, hill-hold, TPMS, blind-view monitor, driver attention assist, ISOFIX mounts, front and rear parking sensors, and a 360-degree HD surround-view camera. The iRA 2.0 connected car suite adds remote engine start, geofencing, OTA updates, smartwatch integration and over-the-air diagnostics. The 5-star Global NCAP adult and child occupant rating remains a key trust marker for Indian families. After-sales service consistency, however, is an area where Tata still has work to do compared to Maruti or Hyundai.

Price & Value

Priced from roughly Rs 8.10 lakh to Rs 15 lakh ex-showroom, the Nexon undercuts most rivals while offering more equipment and a stronger safety story. Variants are now badged Smart, Pure, Creative and Fearless, with a 3-year/1.25-lakh-km warranty as standard. Against the Maruti Brezza it offers a far richer feature set and a Global NCAP 5-star rating; against the Tata Punch it brings more space, more power and far more tech for the step-up in price. The Hyundai Venue and Kia Sonet remain sharper on touchscreen polish and powertrain refinement, but neither matches the Nexon's safety credentials or design drama. Buyers who prioritise crash protection, ride comfort and standout looks will find few reasons to look elsewhere. Those who weigh long-term service experience and ultimate refinement above all may want to cross-shop carefully. Within Tata's own line-up, the upcoming Sierra and Harrier EV target very different segments, leaving the Nexon as the clear volume play.

What India's Reviewers Agree On

Consensus

  • Bold redesigned styling with sequential LED DRLs, connected tail lamps and X-factor lighting signatures looks distinctive in the segment
  • Cabin is a major upgrade with a 10.25-inch touchscreen, fully digital instrument cluster, ventilated seats, panoramic sunroof and JBL audio
  • Ride quality is mature and confident at both city and highway speeds, with strong body control and grip
  • 5-star Global NCAP rating, six airbags, ESP, TPMS, blind-view monitor and 360-degree camera make it one of the safest cars in its class
  • Strong value proposition with petrol, diesel, MT, AMT and 7-speed DCT options, plus a 3-year warranty

Points of Disagreement

  • Petrol engine refinement: some find the three-cylinder turbo unit responsive and fun in Sport mode, others flag turbo lag below 2000 rpm and flat spots in the power band
  • Touchscreen experience: praised for size and modernity by some, but rivals like the Kia Sonet are noted to offer a crisper, more responsive interface

TeamBHP's Take

TeamBHP

TeamBHP's community has long held the Nexon in high regard for its crashworthiness and ride quality, and owner threads on the facelift echo that, with most flagging the redesigned cabin and 360-degree camera as standout upgrades. Long-term owners continue to rate the diesel for highway efficiency, while the petrol DCT receives cautious praise tempered by typical dual-clutch concerns in stop-go traffic. After-sales service experience remains the most discussed pain point in owner forums.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I buy the Tata Nexon?
Yes, if you prioritise safety, ride comfort, distinctive design and feature value under Rs 15 lakh. It is one of the strongest all-rounders in the compact SUV segment, particularly for family buyers who want a 5-star Global NCAP rated car.
What is the Tata Nexon price in India?
The 2025 Tata Nexon is priced from Rs 8.10 lakh ex-showroom for the base Smart variant, with the top Fearless+ S petrol DCT going up to around Rs 15 lakh ex-showroom.
What are the main problems with the Tata Nexon?
Common observations include turbo lag and flat spots in the petrol's low-end, audible diesel engine noise inside the cabin, limited front cubby storage, tight rear headroom for taller occupants, and inconsistent after-sales service experience.
How is the Tata Nexon mileage?
Claimed efficiency is 17.4 kmpl for the petrol manual, 16 kmpl for the petrol AMT and around 22 kmpl for the 1.5-litre diesel. Real-world city figures of 10-12 kmpl for petrol and 16-18 kmpl for diesel are realistic.
Is Tata Nexon good for highway driving?
Yes. The suspension is tuned to settle nicely at speed, body control is strong, the steering weights up well and 208 mm of ground clearance handles rough patches. The diesel and petrol DCT are the most relaxed highway choices.
How does Tata Nexon compare to rivals?
Against the Maruti Brezza, it offers superior safety and a richer feature list. Versus the Hyundai Venue and Kia Sonet it leads on crash protection and design but trails slightly on touchscreen polish and powertrain refinement. Within Tata's range, the smaller Punch is the more affordable alternative.
What is the boot space of Tata Nexon?
The Nexon offers 382 litres of boot space along with a full-size spare wheel, a parcel tray, hooks rated for 3 kg and a 12-volt power outlet.
Is Tata Nexon safe?
Yes, the Nexon carries a 5-star Global NCAP rating for both adult and child occupant protection and comes with six airbags, ESP, hill-hold, TPMS, blind-view monitor, driver attention assist, ISOFIX mounts, and a 360-degree camera as standard on top variants.
What is the waiting period for Tata Nexon?
Waiting periods vary by city and variant but are typically between 2 to 8 weeks for popular trims like the Creative and Fearless. Buyers should confirm with their nearest Tata dealer for current timelines.
Which variant of Tata Nexon should I buy?
The Creative and Fearless variants offer the best balance of features and price, including the 10.25-inch touchscreen, digital cluster, sunroof, ventilated seats and 360-degree camera. The Fearless+ S adds JBL audio and full leatherette seats for buyers who want everything.
