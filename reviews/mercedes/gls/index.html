Mercedes-Benz GLS: The Jury's Verdict

BUY
8.0
Jury Score / 10

A genuinely complete luxury seven-seater for chauffeur-driven buyers, though the X7 drives better and undercuts it on price.

By The Car Jury Editorial Published 22 May 2026 Synthesis of 6 independent sources 1,334 words · 6 min read

The 2026 Mercedes-Benz GLS facelift arrives with a bigger grille, a refreshed cabin and the same proven inline-six petrol and diesel engines on a 48V mild-hybrid system. It remains the closest thing to an S-Class on stilts, with massive presence, lounge-grade second-row comfort and rock-solid build. The trade-off: low-speed ride is firmer than expected and the BMW X7 is the sharper, cheaper driver's choice.

What Works

  • Refined, torque-rich inline-six engines with 0-100 km/h in 6.1 seconds
  • Genuinely luxurious second row with massage, ventilation, recline and dual screens
  • Brilliant air suspension and insulation at highway speeds
  • Massive boot, 5-zone climate, 13-speaker Burmester, nine airbags
  • Strong brand value and S-Class-grade fit and finish

Watch Out For

  • Priced above the BMW X7 despite offering similar features
  • No Sport drive mode, no head-up display, no rear-wheel steering
  • Firm low-speed ride thanks to 21-inch wheels with low-profile tyres
  • Massive turning radius makes city driving and parking a chore

Design

The facelifted GLS doubles down on presence rather than reinvention. The grille is now significantly larger with the three-pointed star surrounded by radar hardware, the bumper gets a new design, and the AMG Line with Night Package adds blacked-out cladding, dark chrome and black 21-inch alloys for what MotorBeam aptly described as a mafia-style stance. At 5.2 metres long with a 3.1-metre wheelbase, it is longer than the BMW X7 and dwarfs the Audi Q7. The headlamps are largely carried over, the alloy design is unchanged (a missed opportunity), and the rear gets revised LED graphics with a light-show animation on unlock. The illuminated running boards, request sensors on door handles and Mercedes-star puddle lamps add theatre. It looks expensive, but the X7 still pulls more stares.

Interior & Features

Inside is where the GLS justifies its price. The dashboard mirrors the GLE but adds Maybach-inspired pinstripe detailing, ner-finish trim and a properly damped feel to every switch. Twin 12.3-inch screens up front, dual 11.6-inch rear entertainment displays and a detachable 7.4-inch Samsung tablet in the rear armrest cover every infotainment need. The second row is the highlight: ventilated, heated, massaging captain-style comfort with one-touch easy-entry, a chilled compartment for two bottles, and 64-colour ambient lighting that genuinely transforms the cabin at night. Five-zone climate, nine airbags and a 13-speaker 590W Burmester system are standard. The third row, as Faisal Khan notes, is a namesake seven-seater: fine for children, cramped for adults. Glaring omissions remain: no head-up display, no in-cabin fragrance, no rear-passenger climate touchpoint.

Performance & Powertrain

Both engines are 3.0-litre inline-sixes with a 48V mild-hybrid ISG adding 20hp and 200Nm. The GLS 450 petrol makes 375hp and 500Nm; the new GLS 450d replaces the 400d with 367hp and a massive 750Nm, Mercedes' most powerful diesel ever. Both do 0-100 km/h in 6.1 seconds with a 250 km/h top speed via a 9-speed torque converter. The diesel is the one to buy, V3Cars argues, for its effortless torque and real-world economy of 8-9 km/l in the city and up to 14 km/l on highways. Refinement is exceptional, with laminated glass keeping the cabin S-Class quiet. The big miss: there is no Sport mode, only Comfort, Eco and Off-Road. The GLS 63 AMG with its 630hp V8 is sadly not coming to India.

Ride Quality & Handling

The air suspension with adaptive damping is the GLS's defining trait, and it is a tale of two speeds. Above 60 km/h the car glides over expansion joints and broken patches with limousine composure, isolating occupants from everything the road throws at it. Below 40 km/h, the 275/45 R21 front and 315/40 R21 rear tyres transmit sharp impacts into the cabin, a problem the BMW X7 handles slightly better. Body roll is present but well-contained for a 2,500kg SUV. The steering is light and accurate but the 12.5-metre turning radius makes U-turns a multi-point affair, especially without rear-wheel steering. Handling is composed rather than engaging; this is a chauffeur's car first, a driver's car a distant second. Brakes are strong and progressive.

Build Quality & Technology

Build quality is genuinely a notch above the X7 and Q7 in the cabin. Metal switches, soft-touch surfaces everywhere, illuminated scuff plates, double-glazed laminated windows with infrared film, soft-close doors and beautifully damped action across every panel. The Burmester system, ambient lighting choreography and the way the easy-entry seat dances out of the way feel meticulously engineered. Feature highlights include nine airbags, 360-degree camera, transparent bonnet view, blind-spot assist, adaptive cruise, 500mm water-wading depth, off-road mode with detailed telemetry, wireless Apple CarPlay/Android Auto, 100W USB-C fast charging, and the Mercedes Me connected suite. Notable omissions for the price: no head-up display, no air-balance fragrance package (offered on the smaller GLE), and the touch-capacitive steering buttons are fiddly compared to the older physical layout.

Price & Value

At roughly Rs 1.73 crore on-road for the GLS 450d AMG Line with Night Package, the GLS sits above the facelifted BMW X7, which offers similar features, sharper handling and a better low-speed ride for less money. The Audi Q7, priced around Rs 80-90 lakh cheaper, is older and petrol-only. Within the Mercedes range, those needing a smaller footprint will find the GLC more city-friendly. Where the GLS earns its premium is brand cachet, second-row luxury and the sense of occasion that no rival quite matches at this size. As MotorOctane points out, it is not Maybach-level theatre, but it is the most complete chauffeur-driven seven-seater under Rs 2 crore. Highway-driven owners will love it; daily city commuters should think twice.

What India's Reviewers Agree On

Consensus

  • Inline-six petrol (375hp) and diesel (367hp) with 48V mild hybrid feel effortless despite the 2,500kg kerb weight
  • Air suspension delivers magic-carpet ride above 60 km/h but crashes through sharp bumps at low speeds
  • Second-row comfort, dual 11.6-inch screens and burmester audio make it a phenomenal chauffeur-driven car
  • Third row remains strictly for children, with tight headroom and poor under-thigh support for adults
  • Build quality, materials and switchgear are class-leading and noticeably plusher than the BMW X7

Points of Disagreement

  • Reviewers split on whether the facelift's bigger grille looks imposing or overdone
  • Low-speed ride quality: some find it acceptable for a luxury SUV, others call it firm and bump-sensitive

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I buy the Mercedes-Benz GLS?
Yes, if you will be chauffeur-driven on highways. The X7 is a better choice if you drive yourself in the city.
What is the Mercedes-Benz GLS price in India?
Ex-showroom prices start at around Rs 1.37 crore. The GLS 450d AMG Line with Night Package is roughly Rs 1.73 crore on-road Mumbai.
What are the main problems with the Mercedes-Benz GLS?
Firm low-speed ride on 21-inch wheels, no Sport mode, no head-up display, large turning radius and a premium over the BMW X7.
How is the Mercedes-Benz GLS mileage?
Expect 5-8 km/l in city traffic and 11-14 km/l on highways with the diesel. Petrol returns around 5-6 km/l in city driving.
Is Mercedes-Benz GLS good for highway driving?
Exceptional. Air suspension, laminated glass, 750Nm of diesel torque and a 90-litre tank make 700-800 km highway runs effortless.
How does Mercedes-Benz GLS compare to rivals?
More luxurious cabin than the BMW X7 but pricier and firmer at low speed. Better built than the older Audi Q7, which is petrol-only.
What is the boot space of Mercedes-Benz GLS?
355 litres with all three rows up, 890 litres with the third row folded, and 1,470 litres with both rear rows folded.
Is Mercedes-Benz GLS safe?
Yes. Nine airbags, 360-degree camera, blind-spot assist, adaptive cruise, ABS with brake assist, ESP and active bonnet are standard.
