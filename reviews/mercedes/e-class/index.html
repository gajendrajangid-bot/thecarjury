Mercedes-Benz E-Class: The Jury's Verdict

BUY
8.2
Jury Score / 10

Unmatched rear-seat comfort, a still-available diesel with 1,000 km range, and the segment's best chauffeur-driven experience justify the premium over the BMW 5 Series.

By The Car Jury Editorial Published 19 May 2026 Synthesis of 6 independent sources 1,323 words · 6 min read

The sixth-generation Mercedes-Benz E-Class LWB (V214) doubles down on what made it India's bestselling luxury sedan: limousine-grade rear-seat space, supreme ride comfort, and a 2.0L diesel that delivers nearly 1,000 km on a tank. Priced from Rs 78.5 lakh to Rs 92 lakh ex-showroom, it remains the default chauffeur-driven choice. Drivers seeking thrill should look at the BMW 5 Series instead.

What Works

  • Best-in-class rear-seat experience with boss mode, recline and rear tablet
  • Diesel delivers 15-16 km/l on highway and ~1,000 km tank range
  • Plush, well-damped ride even without air suspension
  • Excellent NVH, soft-close doors and a genuinely premium cabin
  • Strong safety kit: 8 airbags, Level 2 ADAS, 5-star Euro NCAP

Watch Out For

  • No ventilated seats, no rear-seat massage, no rear entertainment screen
  • Over-reliance on touch controls; sunroof and steering touch buttons are fiddly
  • Brake pedal feel and gearbox urgency disappoint enthusiast drivers
  • Priced Rs 7+ lakh above the BMW 5 Series for similar engines

Design

The V214 E-Class grows visibly longer and lower, with a wheelbase pushing 3.1 metres and an overall length close to 5.1 metres in LWB form. The grille is wider, the LED headlamps sharper, and an EQE-inspired profile gives a 0.23 drag coefficient. The Mercedes star pattern grille looks elegant on the E200/E220d, while the AMG-style bumper differentiates the E450. The 18-inch alloys are well-judged, though Faisal Khan flags the fake exhaust tips as an unnecessary cost-cut. The new tail-lamp signature with the three-pointed star embedded inside polarises opinion. The 'verde silver' anniversary shade and a subtle champagne gold are the most flattering colours. Overall, it looks like a smaller S-Class, exactly the visual halo Indian buyers want.

Interior & Features

Step inside and the Superscreen dominates: a 12.3-inch driver cluster, a 14.4-inch central touchscreen and a 12.3-inch passenger display, all running MBUX with AI and a selfie camera for video calls. Quality is excellent, with napa leather, real-feel wood and 64-colour ambient lighting that even shifts between red and blue with the AC temperature. Standard kit includes a four-zone climate, wireless charging, soft-close doors, a Burmester system and a panoramic sunroof plus rear moonroof. The omissions sting at this price: no ventilated seats, no massage, no rear entertainment screen, and a rear tablet for blinds and ambient lighting that does not quite replace dedicated buttons. As Gagan Choudhary notes, the lower-end E200 even drops electric steering adjust and the 360-degree camera.

Performance & Powertrain

Three engines are on offer in India. The E200 petrol makes 204 hp and 320 Nm; the E220d diesel that most buyers will choose makes 197 hp and 440 Nm; the E450 petrol jumps to a 3.0L six-cylinder with 381 hp and 500 Nm, claiming 0-100 km/h in 4.5 seconds. All run a 48V mild-hybrid system with a 20 hp/200 Nm ISG boost and a 9-speed torque converter. The diesel is the sweet spot: linear, refined and effortless for highway overtakes, though the old kick-in-the-back surge is gone post-BS6. MotorBeam's E450 test confirms the V6's pace, while the E200's 8.39-second 0-100 run is adequate rather than exciting. Paddle shifters exist but the gearbox refuses to hold gears, which annoys enthusiasts.

Ride Quality & Handling

Ride quality is where the E-Class earns its money. India gets steel coil springs with a selective damping system, not the air suspension or rear-wheel steering offered globally as the Refinement Package. Yet the calibration is so good that rear passengers waft over broken tarmac and expansion joints with minimal disturbance. The steering is light at city speeds and weights up nicely on highways, though there is noticeable body roll given the 1,900 kg kerb weight and soft setup. Ground clearance has been adjusted for Indian conditions, though there is no nose-lift function, so speed breakers still need care. As MotorOctane points out, anyone wanting genuine driver involvement should cross-shop the BMW 5 Series or 3 Series Gran Limousine.

Build Quality & Technology

Fit and finish are typical Mercedes: door pockets are lined, switchgear feels dense, and the flush door handles deploy with theatre. The car earned a five-star Euro NCAP rating and ships with eight airbags including knee and centre airbags, Level 2 ADAS, 360-degree camera, blind-spot assist, active brake assist and Pre-Safe. Safety kit is now consistent across variants, a welcome change. Niggles remain: Gagan reports the flush handles occasionally fail to retract, the central screen has hung mid-drive, and the manual rear sunshades feel out of place. The Burmester 17-speaker 730W audio is excellent up front but skips exciters in the rear, an odd choice for a chauffeur-driven car. India assembly continues at Chakan with a three-year warranty extendable to six.

Price & Value

Prices run from roughly Rs 78.5 lakh for the E200 to Rs 92 lakh for the E450, ex-showroom. The E220d at Rs 82.76 lakh commands a Rs 4.26 lakh premium over the petrol, justified by its torque, 15-16 km/l highway economy and near 1,000 km tank range on a 66-litre tank. Against the BMW 5 Series LWB, the E-Class costs around Rs 7.27 lakh more on a like-for-like petrol comparison and the 5 Series no longer offers diesel, leaving Mercedes with a near-monopoly on oil-burning luxury sedans. The Audi A6 is ageing and the Volvo S90 and Jaguar XF are gone. For chauffeur-driven buyers there is no better pick; self-drivers should weigh the 5 Series carefully.

What India's Reviewers Agree On

Consensus

  • Rear-seat comfort and space are the segment benchmark, with reclining seats, tablet controls and dual sunroofs
  • The E220d diesel offers effortless torque and a real-world range close to 1,000 km on a full tank
  • The Superscreen layout looks stunning but pushes too many functions into touch menus
  • Ride quality on steel springs is supple and refined for Indian roads
  • It comfortably outsells the BMW 5 Series despite costing more, because it is the better chauffeur car

Points of Disagreement

  • Faisal Khan calls the diesel engine refined and musical; Gagan Choudhary finds the car uninspiring and 'just a comfortable cute car' to drive
  • Reviewers split on the polarising tail-lamp Mercedes-logo motif and the fake exhaust tips

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I buy the Mercedes-Benz E-Class?
Yes, if you will be chauffeur-driven. It is the segment benchmark for rear-seat comfort, ride quality and brand presence in India.
What is the Mercedes-Benz E-Class price in India?
Roughly Rs 78.5 lakh for the E200 petrol, Rs 82.76 lakh for the E220d diesel, and Rs 92 lakh for the E450, ex-showroom.
What are the main problems with the Mercedes-Benz E-Class?
No ventilated seats, no rear massage or entertainment screen, over-reliance on touch controls, occasional screen hangs and flush door handle glitches.
How is the Mercedes-Benz E-Class mileage?
The E220d diesel returns 10-11 km/l in city and 15-16 km/l on the highway, giving a real-world range near 1,000 km on a 66-litre tank.
Is Mercedes-Benz E-Class good for highway driving?
Yes. The diesel's 440 Nm torque, supple steel-spring suspension, light-yet-weighted steering and 238 km/h top speed make highway cruising effortless.
How does Mercedes-Benz E-Class compare to rivals?
It costs about Rs 7.27 lakh more than the BMW 5 Series LWB but offers better rear comfort and a diesel option, which the 5 Series no longer has.
What is the boot space of Mercedes-Benz E-Class?
540 litres with a powered tailgate and a space-saver spare wheel housed below the boot floor.
Is Mercedes-Benz E-Class safe?
Yes. It scored a 5-star Euro NCAP rating with eight airbags including knee and centre airbags, Level 2 ADAS, 360-degree camera and Pre-Safe systems.
