BMW 5 Series LWB: The Jury's Verdict

BUY
8.0
Jury Score / 10

The new 5 Series LWB is a more spacious, better equipped and more comfortable executive sedan than before, with the i5 M60 adding genuinely supercar-rivalling EV performance.

By The Car Jury Editorial Published 19 May 2026 Synthesis of 6 independent sources 1,375 words · 6 min read

The eighth-generation BMW 5 Series arrives in India exclusively in long-wheelbase form, with a 530Li petrol, 520d diesel and the range-topping i5 M60 xDrive electric. It is bigger, plusher and more tech-laden than the car it replaces, while the i5 M60 delivers genuine sub-4-second acceleration for ₹1.28 crore on-road Mumbai.

What Works

  • Limousine-grade rear legroom thanks to the 3.1m+ wheelbase
  • i5 M60 launches harder than BMW's claimed 3.8 seconds
  • Bowers & Wilkins audio and theatrical ambient lighting
  • Slick iDrive 8.5 with crisp 360-degree cameras
  • Adaptive dampers, active anti-roll and rear-wheel steering mask the 2,400 kg weight

Watch Out For

  • Very low ground clearance, the i5's side skirts scrape easily
  • Too many functions, including AC airflow, buried in the touchscreen
  • No rear sunblinds, no soft-close doors, no height-adjustable seatbelts
  • i5's 81.2 kWh battery delivers only ~350-400 km real-world range

Design

Styling is the most polarising part of the new car. The shark-nose fascia, illuminated kidney grille, flush door handles and slim L-shaped tail-lamps borrow heavily from the 7 Series, while the LWB stretches length to roughly 5.2 metres on a 3.1m+ wheelbase. In darker shades with M60 detailing, including red M calipers, M mirrors and 20-inch wheels with 245/40 front and 275/35 rear rubber, the i5 looks genuinely imposing. Faisal Khan admits the i5 in M trim wins him over despite his dislike for the new 5 Series silhouette. The petrol 530Li on 18-inch wheels with 225/55 Pirelli P7s looks more conservative. The downside is consistent: ground clearance is alarmingly low, side skirts almost kiss the tarmac, and Indian speed breakers will be a constant worry.

Interior & Features

Inside is where the new car decisively beats the old. The curved display, housing a 12.3-inch cluster and 14.9-inch iDrive 8.5 touchscreen, dominates a clean, driver-angled dashboard with crystal-effect interaction bar, multi-colour ambient lighting and four-zone climate. Front seats are chunky, multi-way electric and ventilated; rear occupants get four-zone AC, USB-C, a tablet mount, pillows and the Travel & Comfort system. MotorBeam confirms rear legroom and cushioning are notably better than the G30, though under-thigh support could be longer. The Bowers & Wilkins 18-speaker, 655W system is excellent. Frustrations are real: AC airflow control is touch-based, glaring omissions include rear sunblinds (offered before), height-adjustable seatbelts and soft-close doors, and the i5's lower floor and intrusive battery pack erode some space versus the petrol LWB.

Performance & Powertrain

The headline act is the i5 M60 xDrive: dual motors making 601 hp and 795 Nm, 0-100 km/h claimed in 3.8 seconds. Real-world launches with launch control returned 3.55-3.57 seconds, comfortably beating BMW's number. Top speed is 230 km/h, and a 10-second boost paddle adds 10% extra shove. The 81.2 kWh usable battery (from the i4) delivers a WLTP-claimed 516 km but a realistic 350-400 km; 10-80% DC fast charging takes ~30 minutes at up to 205 kW. The 530i petrol uses the familiar 2.0-litre turbo four with ZF 8-speed auto and feels punchy and refined, while the 520d brings a 2.0-litre diesel. Namaste Car notes a 48V mild-hybrid system supports the ICE cars and the active anti-roll setup on the i5.

Ride Quality & Handling

Ride and handling are tuned firmly for Indian comfort priorities. The 530Li on taller-profile 18s rides slightly softer than the equivalent E-Class, with some body movement at highway speeds but excellent cushioning at the rear. The i5 M60, despite weighing 2,400 kg and riding on 20-inch low-profile rubber, hides its mass remarkably well thanks to adaptive dampers, 48V-assisted active anti-roll bars and rear-wheel steering that turns the rear axle up to 2.5 degrees. Steering is light at parking speeds and nicely weighted at pace. Arun Panwar's brief drive impressions echo the consensus: the chassis is unflustered, grip is enormous, and the car never feels its weight. The one persistent caveat across every reviewer is ground clearance: the i5 scrapes its underbody on routine speed breakers.

Build Quality & Technology

Fit and finish lift the cabin to genuine seven-figure standards. Doors thud shut, the leatherwork, stitching and crystal-effect controls feel expensive, and the iDrive rotary remains one of the best in the business. The 360-degree camera with 3D view, reversing assistant, Level 1 ADAS with well-calibrated lane keep, head-up display and gesture control work cleanly. MotoWagon highlights the polished steering feel and ergonomics. Niggles persist, though. Some plastics on the dashboard control panel feel cheap for the price, the multi-info display can lag, the 'Digital Art' and 'Relax' My Modes feel gimmicky, and BMW's signature parsimony shows up as missing soft-close doors, no rear sunshades and only 15 ambient colours when rivals offer 64. Seven to eight airbags and ISOFIX are standard.

Price & Value

The 530Li petrol and 520d diesel sit at roughly ₹86.7 lakh on-road Mumbai, lining them up directly against the Mercedes-Benz E-Class LWB and Audi A6, with the BMW 3 Series Gran Limousine a size below. The i5 M60 xDrive is the headline-grabber at ₹1.28 crore on-road, undercutting the Porsche Taycan and Audi e-tron GT by a significant margin while delivering comparable performance. Registration is ₹50,000 and insurance roughly ₹4.88 lakh. The catch is the ₹41 lakh premium over the petrol LWB for a CBU electric with a smaller battery and shorter wheelbase than the ICE car. For chauffeur-driven buyers the 530Li makes the strongest case; for enthusiasts with home charging, the i5 M60 is a genuine performance bargain.

What India's Reviewers Agree On

Consensus

  • LWB makes rear-seat space and comfort a clear step up over the outgoing G30
  • Cabin quality, ambient lighting and the 14.9-inch iDrive 8.5 screen feel genuinely premium
  • Ride is on the softer, comfort-biased side, suiting Indian chauffeur-driven use
  • i5 M60 xDrive's 601 hp twin-motor performance is mind-bending for the price
  • Ground clearance is a serious concern, especially on the i5 with low-profile 20-inch tyres

Points of Disagreement

  • Whether the new design improves on the G30: some find the i5 in M60 trim striking, others prefer the older car's cleaner styling
  • Whether the touch-heavy dashboard is acceptable at this price or a genuine usability downgrade

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I buy the BMW 5 Series LWB?
Yes, if you want a chauffeur-driven luxury sedan with strong rear comfort. Choose the 530Li for ICE or the i5 M60 if you have home charging.
What is the BMW 5 Series LWB price in India?
The 530Li petrol and 520d diesel are around ₹86.7 lakh on-road Mumbai. The i5 M60 xDrive electric costs ₹1.28 crore on-road Mumbai.
What are the main problems with the BMW 5 Series LWB?
Very low ground clearance, AC airflow buried in the touchscreen, no rear sunblinds or soft-close doors, and the i5's modest 350-400 km real-world range.
How is the BMW 5 Series LWB mileage?
The i5 M60 delivers roughly 350-400 km real-world range from its 81.2 kWh usable battery against a 516 km WLTP claim. ICE economy figures are pending Indian testing.
Is BMW 5 Series LWB good for highway driving?
Yes. Stable at 180 km/h, well-calibrated Level 1 ADAS with lane keep, comfortable seats and strong refinement make it an excellent high-speed cruiser.
How does BMW 5 Series LWB compare to rivals?
It rides slightly better than the current E-Class and feels more spacious than the Audi A6, while the 3 Series Gran Limousine sits a clear segment below.
What is the boot space of BMW 5 Series LWB?
The petrol 530Li offers around 500 litres of boot space with a full-size spare. The i5 M60 has a smaller boot due to battery packaging and no front frunk.
Is BMW 5 Series LWB safe?
It gets seven to eight airbags, ISOFIX mounts, ESC, TPMS, 360-degree camera, parking assistant and Level 1 ADAS with lane keep and collision warning as standard.
