BMW 7 Series: The Jury's Verdict

BUY
8.2
Jury Score / 10

For chauffeur-driven luxury buyers who also enjoy occasional driving stints, the 7 Series and i7 deliver flagship tech, lounge-grade rear comfort and unexpectedly nimble dynamics.

By The Car Jury Editorial Published 20 May 2026 Synthesis of 5 independent sources 1,327 words · 6 min read

The 2026 BMW 7 Series is the brand's most tech-laden flagship ever, offered as the petrol 740i at Rs 1.7 crore and the electric i7 at around Rs 2 crore (ex-showroom). With a 31.3-inch theatre screen, automatic doors, lounge-style rear seats and genuine BMW driver engagement despite a 5-metre footprint, it targets the Mercedes EQS and S-Class head-on.

What Works

  • Lounge-mode rear seat with 31.3-inch theatre screen and Bowers & Wilkins-grade audio
  • i7 delivers 500-550 km real-world range with 240 kW DC fast charging support
  • Driver-focused dynamics atypical for a 5-metre limousine
  • Automatic doors, remote parking and augmented reality navigation feel genuinely futuristic
  • Adaptive air suspension auto-lowers at speed for stability

Watch Out For

  • Pricing of Rs 1.7-2.06 crore (ex-showroom) makes it the costliest BMW sedan ever in India
  • Ground clearance remains a concern on broken roads despite raisable air suspension
  • Some chrome detailing at the rear feels overdone for a flagship
  • Front passenger seat misses the massage function offered to other occupants

Design

The new 7 Series doubles down on polarising presence: split LED headlamps with the laser-light signature, an enlarged illuminated kidney grille and a slab-sided silhouette stretching beyond five metres. The i7 wears 21-inch M alloys while the 740i petrol gets 19-inch wheels with 245/45 front and 275/40 rear Pirellis. Faisal Khan notes the chrome line connecting the tail lamps and the M Sport-specific badging give the rear a busier look than the outgoing model. A front-facing camera with its own washer, flush door handles with soft-close, and BMW's Iconic Glow grille lighting reinforce the flagship intent. Opinions on the chrome quantum split the jury, but the overall stance, particularly in darker shades and at night, is undeniably commanding.

Interior & Features

Inside is where the 7 Series rewrites the segment script. The dashboard layers wood, brushed aluminium, crystalline glass switchgear and ambient lighting into one of the most theatrical cabins on sale. The curved display pairs a 12.3-inch cluster with a 14.9-inch touchscreen, while the rear gets a 31.3-inch 8K ultra-wide screen that drops from the roof and streams Amazon Fire TV, Netflix and YouTube with sun blinds closing automatically in Theatre mode. Each rear door houses a 5.5-inch touch tablet to control seats, massage, climate and even open the doors. Faisal Khan flags that the front passenger seat misses massage, and the front sunroof opens but the rear panoramic section is fixed. Material quality and switch tactility are flagship-grade throughout.

Performance & Powertrain

The petrol 740i runs a 3.0-litre inline-six with 48V mild-hybrid making 381 hp and 520 Nm, claiming 0-100 km/h in around 5.4 seconds. The i7 xDrive60 is the headline act: dual motors, 544 hp combined, a 101.7 kWh battery, 0-100 km/h in 4.56 seconds and a claimed 500-550 km real-world range. A Boost mode button delivers a sports-car-grade shove despite the 2,600 kg kerb weight. DC fast charging at 240 kW takes the i7 from low to full in under an hour, while the 22 kW AC home charger needs 5-7 hours. As MotorOctane puts it, the powertrain stays whisper-quiet yet thumps hard when prodded, making this one of the quickest luxury sedans on sale.

Ride Quality & Handling

The two-axle adaptive air suspension is the centrepiece, automatically lowering at speed for aerodynamics and stability and lifting on demand for broken sections. Gagan Choudhary notes that ride quality is genuinely plush at city speeds with most road texture filtered out, though sharper expansion joints and deeper potholes still transmit a muted thud into the cabin. Ground clearance remains the practical concern despite the lift function, and drivers will need to read approaches carefully on Indian roads. Handling, remarkably for a 5,391 mm long sedan, is composed: the steering is light but accurate, body roll is well contained, and the car shrinks around the driver in traffic. Few luxury limousines this size feel this willing to be hustled.

Build Quality & Technology

Build feels resolutely flagship: soft-close doors that can also open and close automatically via sensors or the screen, frameless door glass with double glazing, and crystalline elements on the gear selector, iDrive controller and seat controls. Six airbags, ABS with dynamic brake lights, parking distance control, surround view, crash-active protection and BMW's connected services suite are standard. The i7 is locally assembled at the Chennai plant alongside the 740i petrol, helping containing pricing. Niggles exist: the screen does not fully cover the windshield reflection, the vanity mirror coverage is incomplete and certain rear-control gestures need familiarisation. Overall fit, finish and switch damping are arguably the best BMW India has retailed, and clearly aimed at S-Class buyers.

Price & Value

At Rs 1.70 crore for the 740i petrol and roughly Rs 2.06 crore for the i7 (ex-showroom), this is the most expensive BMW sedan sold in India. The 740i ships with a 3-year/40,000 km service inclusive package starting around Rs 75,000. Direct rivals are the Mercedes-Benz S-Class and EQS, plus the Audi A8 L. The i7 in particular justifies its premium with genuinely path-breaking tech, while the petrol's case rests more on driver engagement than outright opulence. Namaste Car points out that local assembly helps keep pricing competitive with CBU rivals. For buyers wanting both a chauffeur-driven sanctum and a car they will personally enjoy driving on weekends, the value equation is uniquely justifiable.

What India's Reviewers Agree On

Consensus

  • The 31.3-inch rear theatre screen with Amazon Fire TV is a genuine first-in-segment feature
  • Automatic doors with sensor-based obstacle detection are a standout party trick
  • The i7's 0-100 km/h in 4.56 seconds feels sports-car quick despite the 2,600 kg kerb weight
  • Despite being over 5 metres long, the car feels surprisingly nimble and easy to drive in city traffic
  • Cabin quality, crystalline switchgear and material selection are flagship-grade

Points of Disagreement

  • Ride quality: some find the air suspension perfectly plush, others feel sharper bumps still transfer to the cabin and ground clearance needs care
  • Rear seat comfort: most rate it lounge-grade, but at least one reviewer feels the Mercedes S-Class is still more focused on rear-seat luxury

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I buy the BMW 7 Series?
Yes, if you want a chauffeur-driven flagship you will also enjoy driving. The i7 electric is the standout pick for tech-first buyers.
What is the BMW 7 Series price in India?
The 740i petrol starts at Rs 1.70 crore and the i7 electric is priced at around Rs 2.06 crore, ex-showroom.
What are the main problems with the BMW 7 Series?
Ground clearance on broken roads, front passenger misses massage, busy rear chrome, and an Rs 2 crore price tag positioning it above rivals.
How is the BMW 7 Series mileage?
The 740i petrol with mild-hybrid returns roughly 12 km/l. The i7 electric delivers a real-world range of 500-550 km from its 101.7 kWh battery.
Is BMW 7 Series good for highway driving?
Yes. Cruising at 150 km/h feels effortless thanks to air suspension, ADAS-grade cruise control and the i7's instant electric torque.
How does BMW 7 Series compare to rivals?
It directly takes on the Mercedes-Benz S-Class, EQS and Audi A8 L. The i7 leads on cabin tech; S-Class still edges it on rear comfort.
What is the boot space of BMW 7 Series?
Boot space is sufficient for four occupants' road-trip luggage. The 740i petrol has a 74-litre fuel tank; the i7 stores cables under the floor.
Is BMW 7 Series safe?
Yes. Six airbags, ABS with dynamic brake lights, surround view, crash-active protection, attention assist and connected SOS calling are standard.
