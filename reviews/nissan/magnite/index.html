Nissan Magnite: The Jury's Verdict

BUY
7.5
Jury Score / 10

The facelifted Magnite delivers exceptional value, genuine SUV space, 4-star safety and a 360-degree camera at a price no rival matches, provided you can live with Nissan's smaller service network.

By The Car Jury Editorial Published 13 May 2026 Synthesis of 7 independent sources 1,424 words · 6 min read

The 2025 Nissan Magnite is India's most sensible sub-4-metre SUV for value-conscious buyers, packing a 360-degree camera, six airbags as standard and a 4-star Global NCAP rating into a sharp price bracket. The facelift adds leatherette trim, better insulation and a calmer turbo-CVT, though the design changes split opinion and the touchscreen feels dated. Priced from around Rs 6 lakh to Rs 11.5 lakh ex-showroom, it remains the value benchmark in its segment.

What Works

  • Aggressive pricing and low running costs, with claimed maintenance at 39 paise per km
  • Feature-rich: 360-degree camera, wireless Android Auto/CarPlay, ambient lighting, auto-dimming IRVM
  • 4-star Global and ASEAN NCAP rating with six airbags and three-point belts for all
  • Spacious cabin and 336-litre boot with 60:40 split rear seats
  • Strong turbo CVT combination with dedicated L mode for engine braking

Watch Out For

  • 9-inch touchscreen looks dated, low-resolution 360 camera feed and occasional lag
  • 1.0L NA engine struggles with three to four occupants and on inclines
  • Minor rattles, average panel gaps and some hard plastics persist
  • Smaller dealer and service network than Maruti, Hyundai or Tata

Design

The Magnite retains its sub-4-metre SUV stance with a 1.6-metre height, 205 mm ground clearance and 16-inch dual-tone diamond-cut alloys. The facelift brings a larger honeycomb grille, redesigned LED bi-projector headlamps with L-shaped DRLs, new LED tail lamps and a floating skid plate. Opinion is split on the cosmetic update: while it adds a more premium, SUV-like presence, the front end now looks busier than the cleaner pre-facelift car. Panel gaps are wide but consistent, and 67 percent of the body uses high-tensile steel. Functional roof rails take 50 kg, and shark-fin antenna plus body-coloured ORVMs with integrated indicators round off the kit. Six colours including Sun Copper Orange are offered, with dual-tone black roof as an option.

Interior & Features

The facelifted cabin is the Magnite's biggest leap forward. Leatherette-wrapped dashboard, ambient lighting, auto-dimming IRVM, a 9-inch floating touchscreen with wireless Android Auto and Apple CarPlay, six-speaker Arkamys 3D audio, a 7-inch digital cluster and automatic AC with PM 2.5 filter elevate the perceived quality. Space is genuinely segment-leading: three adults fit across the rear bench, rear AC vents and a USB port are standard, and the 336-litre boot expands to 690 litres with the 60:40 split folded. Niggles remain: the touchscreen UI feels a generation behind, the 360-camera resolution is low, lower-dashboard plastics are scratchy and there is no ventilated seat or wireless charger as standard. Gagan Choudhary flagged that the IRVM placement limits rear visibility for shorter drivers.

Performance & Powertrain

Two 1.0-litre three-cylinder petrols are offered: a 71 hp/96 Nm naturally aspirated unit with a 5-speed manual, and a 98 hp/160 Nm turbo paired with a 5-speed manual or CVT (152 Nm on CVT). The turbo-CVT is the pick: MotorOctane found it smooth, easy in traffic and confident on highways, with the CVT's L mode delivering strong engine braking in the ghats. The NA engine is adequate solo in the city but feels strained with three or four passengers and on inclines, and lacks refinement compared to Maruti and Hyundai equivalents. Real-world fuel efficiency is 12-15 kmpl in the city and up to 21 kmpl on the highway for the NA, while the turbo CVT returns 9-11 kmpl city and 16-18 kmpl highway.

Ride Quality & Handling

Suspension is MacPherson strut up front and twin-tube telescopic at the rear, both with stabiliser bars and double-acting shock absorbers. The facelift's softer tune handles 80 percent of Indian roads with composure, and the tall 195/60 R16 sidewalls plus 205 mm ground clearance soak up potholes that would unsettle stiffer rivals. Sharp expansion joints can feel clunky at low speeds. NVH has improved through better insulation and glass, with the cabin staying quiet to around 80-85 kmph before wind noise filters in. The steering is light for city use but slow, the brakes lack initial bite and the foot well is cramped for larger feet. Vehicle Dynamic Control, traction control, hill-start assist and hydraulic brake assist are standard on the turbo.

Build Quality & Technology

Built at Nissan's Chennai plant and exported to over 65 countries, the Magnite uses 67 percent high-tensile steel and earned 4 stars from both Global NCAP and ASEAN NCAP. Six airbags, ABS with EBD, ESP, traction control, hill-start assist, TPMS, ISOFIX and three-point belts for all are standard. The facelift's leatherette trim, tighter panel fitment and improved damping noticeably lift cabin ambience over the pre-facelift car, as the Unknown Reviewer noted. Long-term niggles remain: minor rattles appear over time, wipers are noisy, fog lamps are weak, headlamps falter in heavy rain and there is no IR-cut glass. Nissan offers a 3-year/1-lakh-km warranty, four free labour services up to 20,000 km and claimed running cost of 39 paise per km.

Price & Value

Value is the Magnite's defining trump card. The range spans roughly Rs 6 lakh to Rs 11.5 lakh ex-showroom, with the top Tekna Plus turbo CVT at Rs 11.5 lakh. MotorBeam underlined that no other sub-4-metre SUV bundles a 360 camera, six airbags, wireless connectivity, ambient lighting and a 4-star NCAP rating at this price. Running costs are exceptional: an annual service comes in under Rs 2,000 thanks to free labour, and claimed maintenance is 39 paise per km. The catch is Nissan India's smaller dealer footprint and lingering uncertainty around Nissan's global alliance plans, which could affect resale confidence. For buyers who prioritise features, safety and space per rupee, nothing in the segment matches it.

What India's Reviewers Agree On

Consensus

  • Best-in-segment value with a 360-degree camera, six airbags and 4-star Global NCAP safety as standard
  • Genuine cabin space: three adults fit at the rear and the 336-litre boot is usable
  • Turbo petrol with CVT is smooth, refined and a strong highway cruiser
  • Ground clearance of 205 mm and tall sidewalls handle Indian potholes confidently
  • LED headlamps offer an excellent throw and spread for the price point

Points of Disagreement

  • Facelift styling: some reviewers find the new grille and bumper busy, others call it more premium
  • 1.0L naturally aspirated engine: acceptable city runabout for some, underpowered and unrefined for others

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I buy the Nissan Magnite?
Yes, if you want maximum features, safety and space per rupee in the sub-4-metre SUV segment. The turbo CVT is the variant to pick.
What is the Nissan Magnite price in India?
The 2025 Magnite is priced from roughly Rs 6 lakh to Rs 11.5 lakh ex-showroom, with the top Tekna Plus turbo CVT at Rs 11.5 lakh.
What are the main problems with the Nissan Magnite?
Dated touchscreen, weak fog lamps, headlamps struggle in heavy rain, minor rattles develop over time, and Nissan's smaller service network compared to mainstream rivals.
How is the Nissan Magnite mileage?
The NA petrol returns 12-15 kmpl in city and up to 21 kmpl on highway. The turbo CVT delivers 9-11 kmpl city and 16-18 kmpl highway.
Is Nissan Magnite good for highway driving?
Yes, especially the turbo CVT. It cruises easily, overtakes confidently above 2,500 rpm, and the L mode provides strong engine braking in ghats.
How does Nissan Magnite compare to rivals?
It undercuts Brezza, Venue, Sonet and Nexon significantly while matching them on space and safety, though rivals offer better refinement and stronger service networks.
What is the boot space of Nissan Magnite?
The Magnite offers 336 litres of boot space, expandable to 690 litres with the 60:40 split rear seats folded down. A 14-inch spare wheel sits underneath.
Is Nissan Magnite safe?
Yes. The Magnite has a 4-star Global NCAP and ASEAN NCAP rating, with six airbags, ESP, traction control, hill-start assist and three-point belts for all as standard.
