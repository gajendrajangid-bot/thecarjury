Audi Q8: The Jury's Verdict

BUY
8.2
Jury Score / 10

The RSQ8 Performance offers Lamborghini Urus mechanicals and Nurburgring-record pace at a substantial discount, wrapped in understated Audi elegance.

By The Car Jury Editorial Published 22 May 2026 Synthesis of 6 independent sources 1,347 words · 6 min read

The 2026 Audi Q8 facelift arrives in India in two distinct flavours: a refined 3.0 V6 petrol flagship coupe-SUV and the bonkers RSQ8 Performance, which packs a 640 hp twin-turbo V8 borrowed from the Lamborghini Urus. It is understated, ferociously fast, and far cheaper than its Italian cousin.

What Works

  • Brutal yet usable performance with launch control and minimal turbo lag thanks to 48V mild-hybrid
  • Carbon-ceramic brakes (420 mm front, 370 mm rear) standard on the Performance variant
  • Compliant ride despite 23-inch wheels and low-profile tyres, with adaptive air suspension
  • Understated design that flies under the radar versus a flashy Urus
  • Bang & Olufsen 17-speaker 730W 3D audio system as standard

Watch Out For

  • Dashboard design is now visibly older than rivals from Mercedes and BMW
  • No ADAS suite offered in India, only basic lane-keep assist
  • No spare wheel, only a repair kit
  • Fake exhaust elements and design flourishes feel unnecessary on a car this expensive

Design

The facelift is gentle: revised front and rear bumpers, a flatter 2D four-ring logo, new HD Matrix LED headlamps with customisable laser light signatures, and OLED tail lamps with welcome animations. The 23-inch wheels on the RSQ8 Performance now wear a light golden finish that contrasts well with the Chilli Red paint, though Faisal Khan prefers the older wheel design. At five metres long with a near three-metre wheelbase, the silhouette remains an understated coupe-SUV, free of the flamboyance of an Urus or even the latest BMW XM. Audi pulls off the sloping coupe roofline more elegantly than most. The fake vents and trim flourishes do feel unnecessary at this price point, and the basic shape is now seven years old.

Interior & Features

The cabin is solidly built with Alcantara on the steering, doors and centre armrest, red contrast stitching including on the seat belts, and proper leather across the dashboard. The triple-screen layout (12.3-inch cluster, 10.1-inch infotainment, 8.6-inch climate) with haptic feedback works well, but the architecture is now visibly older than rivals. Wireless Apple CarPlay and Android Auto are still missing despite the screens supporting them via software update. Four-zone climate, panoramic sunroof (optional), heads-up display, soft-close doors, powered sunblinds, and 30-colour ambient lighting are present. Rear seat space is genuinely good for two adults up to six feet, with reclining and sliding functions. Boot capacity is 605 litres, but there is no spare wheel.

Performance & Powertrain

This is where the RSQ8 Performance justifies its existence. The 4.0-litre twin-turbo V8 now produces 640 hp and 850 Nm (up 40 hp and 50 Nm), achieved via a new sports exhaust, revised catalytic converter and higher boost pressure. The 48V mild-hybrid masks turbo lag and enables 40-second engine-off coasting. Audi claims 0-100 km/h in 3.6 seconds; Gagan Choudhary and other testers consistently beat that, recording 3.47 seconds. Top speed is 305 km/h with the limiter raised. The 8-speed torque converter shifts seamlessly, the quattro sport differential can send up to 85 percent torque rearward, and active roll stabilisation eliminates body roll. The regular Q8 55 TFSI's 3.0 V6 making 340 hp and 500 Nm feels adequate but unremarkable in comparison.

Ride Quality & Handling

Despite 23-inch wheels wrapped in 295/35 rubber and a stiff sporting brief, the adaptive air suspension keeps the RSQ8 surprisingly compliant in Comfort mode. There are six drive modes plus two customisable RS modes, each allowing independent tweaks to drivetrain, suspension, steering, exhaust and differential. Off-road mode raises ride height by 90 mm. The steering is direct with genuine feedback, and the carbon-ceramic brakes (standard on the Performance) deliver enormous stopping power. The car feels glued through corners and direction changes belie its 2,300 kg mass, behaving more like a hot hatch than an SUV. The regular Q8's lower-spec wheels and softer setup will likely deliver a more pliant ride better suited to broken Indian roads.

Build Quality & Technology

Fit, finish and material quality are flagship-grade throughout, with Alcantara, real leather, soft-touch surfaces and proper switchgear. The 17-speaker, 730W Bang & Olufsen 3D system is standard and excellent. Safety covers six airbags on the Performance, 360-degree camera with 3D view and lens-cleaning function, park assist, electronic stability and tyre pressure monitoring. Euro NCAP rates the standard Q8 five stars. Notable omissions for India include a full ADAS suite (only basic lane-keep assist), wireless Android Auto and CarPlay, and ventilated rear seats. Frameless doors with soft-close, four-zone climate with ionizer, wireless charging, powered tailgate with electric parcel shelf, name projection from the wing mirrors, and an automatic self-parking function round out a generous feature list.

Price & Value

On-road Mumbai pricing for the RSQ8 Performance lands at around Rs 2.95 crore, including Rs 33.5 lakh registration and Rs 10 lakh first-year insurance. That is roughly Rs 2.4 crore less than a Lamborghini Urus Performante, which shares the same platform and base V8. The Porsche Cayenne GTS sits about Rs 60 lakh below. Audi bundles a 10-year roadside assistance package, and optional service plans keep running costs predictable. The regular Q8 55 TFSI is expected to be priced around Rs 1.40-1.50 crore ex-showroom. Resale on the V8 will depend heavily on fuel regulations, but as a way of buying Urus mechanicals with Audi understatement and lower running costs, the RSQ8 Performance offers genuine value in this rarefied segment.

What India's Reviewers Agree On

Consensus

  • The RSQ8 Performance's 4.0 twin-turbo V8 makes 640 hp and 850 Nm, hitting 0-100 km/h in a claimed 3.6 seconds
  • Shares platform and engine with the Lamborghini Urus and Porsche Cayenne, but costs roughly Rs 2.4 crore less than the Urus
  • Cabin design is dated compared to newer rivals, though material quality and fit-finish remain flagship-grade
  • Quattro AWD with sport differential and active roll stabilisation deliver near-flat cornering despite 2,300 kg kerb weight
  • Coupe roofline does not significantly compromise rear headroom for occupants up to around 6 feet

Points of Disagreement

  • Whether the dated dashboard architecture is acceptable at this price, or feels overdue for a full redesign
  • Whether the absence of ADAS is a deal-breaker or actually a welcome omission for Indian driving conditions

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I buy the Audi Q8?
Yes if you want understated luxury with Urus-level performance for far less; the RSQ8 Performance is the sweet spot in this lineup.
What is the Audi Q8 price in India?
The Q8 55 TFSI is around Rs 1.17-1.50 crore ex-showroom, while the RSQ8 Performance is roughly Rs 2.95 crore on-road Mumbai.
What are the main problems with the Audi Q8?
Dated dashboard architecture, no full ADAS suite in India, no spare wheel, and wired Apple CarPlay or Android Auto despite the hardware supporting wireless.
How is the Audi Q8 mileage?
The 3.0 V6 petrol delivers around 7-8 kmpl in mixed driving; the RSQ8's 4.0 V8 will be significantly thirstier under spirited use.
Is Audi Q8 good for highway driving?
Excellent. High-speed stability, quattro AWD, adaptive air suspension and strong brakes make it a confident long-distance tourer, especially in Comfort mode.
How does Audi Q8 compare to rivals?
Shares its platform with the Porsche Cayenne and Lamborghini Urus. The RSQ8 Performance undercuts the Urus by Rs 2.4 crore while delivering similar pace.
What is the boot space of Audi Q8?
The Q8 offers 605 litres with the rear seats up, expanding to 1,755 litres with the 40-20-40 rear bench folded. No spare wheel is provided.
Is Audi Q8 safe?
Yes. Euro NCAP rates the Q8 five stars, with six to eight airbags, 360-degree camera, ESC, tyre pressure monitoring and ISOFIX child seat mounts standard.
