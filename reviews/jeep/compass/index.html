Jeep Compass: The Jury's Verdict

BUY
7.5
Jury Score / 10

A premium, rugged SUV that excels on highways and rough roads, but its high price and city-unfriendly nature make it a niche pick.

By The Car Jury Editorial Published 13 May 2026 Synthesis of 6 independent sources 1,356 words · 6 min read

The 2025 Jeep Compass remains one of India's most authentic SUVs, blending genuine off-road DNA with a properly premium cabin. The 2.0-litre diesel and frequency-selective damping shine on highways and broken roads, but high pricing and underwhelming city manners limit its mass appeal.

What Works

  • Genuine off-road capability with 4x4 Low, Terrain Select, and 170mm ground clearance
  • Outstanding highway ride and high-speed stability thanks to FSD suspension
  • Premium cabin materials, ventilated seats, panoramic sunroof, and Alpine audio
  • Solid build quality and robust safety structure with up to six airbags and 360-degree camera
  • Strong, refined 2.0-litre diesel with 170 HP and 350 Nm

Watch Out For

  • Expensive: top variants cross 30 lakh, well above Seltos and Harrier
  • Cramped for three at the rear; tight headroom for taller passengers
  • Front bumper flap scrapes on speed breakers and mall ramps
  • Patchy after-sales service network compared to mainstream brands

Design

The Compass still wears its Jeep heritage with conviction: the seven-slot grille, trapezoidal wheel arches, and squared-off stance announce intent without resorting to gimmickry. The facelift adds LED projector headlamps, sharper DRLs, a refreshed lower bumper with a front parking camera, and new alloy designs in 17 and 18-inch sizes. As MotorBeam notes, the chrome strip linking the mirrors and the sloping roofline give it genuine SUV proportions rather than the crossover silhouette dominating the segment. At 4.4 metres long with 170mm ground clearance, it looks compact yet purposeful. Seven exterior colours including Brilliant Black and Magnesio are on offer. The design has aged remarkably well; eight years on, it still looks more premium than several newer rivals.

Interior & Features

Inside, the Compass feels like a properly engineered SUV rather than a feature-stuffed crossover. The dashboard uses soft-touch materials across the upper panels, a new flat-bottom steering wheel, and a 10.25-inch touchscreen paired with a 10.1-inch digital instrument cluster. Ventilated and power-adjustable front seats, dual-pane panoramic sunroof, wireless charging, and a nine-speaker Alpine system with subwoofer are standard on the top trim. The seats themselves are firm but beautifully shaped for long drives, with electric lumbar and seat-base angle adjustment. Rear knee-room is adequate, but the upright backrest, lack of recline, missing rear sunshades, and narrow width make three-abreast seating tight. MotorOctane found the rear cushioning comfortable for two adults but acknowledged headroom is borderline for anyone above 5'10".

Performance & Powertrain

Two engines are offered: a 1.4-litre MultiAir turbo-petrol making 163 HP, and the standout 2.0-litre Multijet-II diesel producing 170 HP and 350 Nm, paired with either a six-speed manual or a nine-speed automatic. The diesel is the heart of the Compass. It pulls effortlessly from 1,600 RPM, masking speed so well that 120 km/h feels like 90. Below 2,000 RPM there's noticeable turbo lag and some diesel clatter at idle, but the mid-range is muscular and the nine-speed auto shuffles ratios unobtrusively. The petrol-DCT combination is smoother but less rewarding for enthusiasts. Highway fuel efficiency settles between 16-18 km/l for the diesel; city economy drops to 8-10 km/l. Refinement at cruising speeds is excellent, though hard acceleration brings the diesel rumble back into the cabin.

Ride Quality & Handling

Ride quality is where the Compass justifies its premium. The Frequency Selective Damping suspension, unique in this price bracket, soaks up broken tarmac and expansion joints with genuine composure. High-speed stability is exceptional; it stays planted through fast sweepers with minimal body roll for an SUV of its height. The steering, as MotorBeam highlights, offers proper feel and even includes pull-and-drift compensation, a rarity in this segment. Off-road, the Active Drive 4x4 system with Auto, Snow, Sand, and Mud modes plus a 4WD Low setting makes it genuinely capable. The catches: the turning radius is large, low-speed ride is firm over sharp bumps, and the front bumper flap scrapes on steep ramps. It is unmistakably built for highways and trails rather than congested city commutes.

Build Quality & Technology

Build quality is the Compass's strongest argument. The body uses 70% high-strength steel, the doors shut with a reassuring thunk, and panel gaps are consistent. The chassis is known to be robust, and safety kit includes six airbags, ESP, traction control, hill-hold, electronic roll mitigation, tyre pressure monitoring, and a 360-degree camera on the top variant. ABS with EBD, ISOFIX, and rear parking sensors are standard. Notable omissions: ADAS is not offered, and TPMS plus the 360-camera are restricted to the top S trim. The Namaste Car walkaround highlighted touches like protective brake-disc wipers, solar-control glass, and connected-car tech with remote disable via phone. Cabin insulation is good at highway speeds, though some plastic rattles emerge over very rough surfaces.

Price & Value

Value is where the Compass stumbles. Prices for the 2025 lineup range from around 20 lakh for entry variants to over 30 lakh for the top diesel automatic 4x4. That places it roughly 10-11 lakh above a comparably equipped Seltos and 5-6 lakh above the Tata Harrier, which shares the same Multijet diesel. In the American market, the gap to a Seltos-equivalent is about 3 lakh, so Indian pricing feels stiff. After-sales network density also lags behind Hyundai, Toyota, and Tata. The case for the Compass rests on genuine off-road ability, segment-leading build, and a uniquely premium cabin feel that none of the cheaper rivals match. For buyers prioritising those traits, it remains the only honest choice in the price band.

What India's Reviewers Agree On

Consensus

  • Build quality is tank-like and class-leading, with a solid chassis and 70% high-strength steel structure
  • The 2.0-litre Multijet diesel feels effortless on the highway with strong mid-range torque
  • Frequency Selective Damping delivers ride quality that absorbs broken roads brilliantly
  • Interior quality and materials punch above the segment with rich plastics and well-shaped seats
  • Pricing is steep compared to Seltos and Harrier, and after-sales network remains a concern

Points of Disagreement

  • Rear seat comfort: some find legroom and headroom adequate for three, others say six-footers and three-abreast seating feel cramped
  • Infotainment usability: newer reviews praise the 10.1-inch screen, while earlier impressions criticised laggy inputs requiring double-taps

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I buy the Jeep Compass?
Yes, if you want genuine off-road ability, premium build, and excellent highway ride. Skip it if your driving is mostly urban or budget is tight.
What is the Jeep Compass price in India?
The 2025 Compass range starts around 20 lakh for the base petrol and crosses 30 lakh for the top diesel automatic 4x4 variant, ex-showroom.
What are the main problems with the Jeep Compass?
High pricing, tight three-abreast rear seating, scraping front bumper flap, large turning radius, and a patchy after-sales service network compared to mainstream rivals.
How is the Jeep Compass mileage?
The 2.0-litre diesel delivers 8-10 km/l in city and 16-18 km/l on highways. The petrol returns slightly lower figures, especially in stop-go traffic.
Is Jeep Compass good for highway driving?
Exceptional. The diesel masks speed effortlessly, FSD suspension delivers superb stability, and 100 km/h sits at just 1,600 RPM in ninth gear.
How does Jeep Compass compare to rivals?
It is pricier than Seltos and Harrier but offers superior build, genuine 4x4 ability, and a more premium cabin. Rivals win on space and features-per-rupee.
What is the boot space of Jeep Compass?
Boot capacity is marginally larger than a Kia Seltos and notably taller, accommodating three medium-to-large suitcases comfortably with rear seats up.
Is Jeep Compass safe?
Yes. It offers up to six airbags, ESP, traction control, hill-hold, electronic roll mitigation, 360-degree camera, and a 70% high-strength steel body structure.
