Jeep Compass: The Jury's Verdict

BUY
7.5
Jury Score / 10

A genuinely capable, premium-feeling SUV that rewards highway and off-road use, provided you can stomach the price and Jeep's after-sales reputation.

By The Car Jury Editorial Published 13 May 2026 Synthesis of 7 independent sources 1,365 words · 6 min read

The 2025 Jeep Compass remains one of the most solidly built and capable SUVs in its segment, with a 170hp 2.0L diesel, optional 4x4 and Frequency Selective Damping suspension that shine on highways and broken roads. The facelift adds sharper screens, richer materials and a panoramic sunroof, but city manners, after-sales reach and a top-spec price near Rs 30 lakh demand careful thought.

What Works

  • Solid build and safe, rigid chassis with 70% hot-stamped steel
  • Capable 4x4 system with Auto, Snow, Sand and Mud modes
  • Refined highway manners and strong diesel mid-range
  • Premium cabin with 10.1-inch touchscreen and 10.25-inch digital cluster
  • Frequency Selective Damping suspension absorbs broken roads well

Watch Out For

  • Pricey at the top end versus Harrier and Seltos
  • Cramped for three at the rear and tight on rear headroom
  • Low front bumper flap scrapes on ramps and speed breakers
  • Patchy after-sales network compared to mainstream rivals

Design

The facelifted Compass keeps the trademark seven-slot grille, squared wheel arches and sloping roofline that mark it as a proper Jeep, while LED projector headlamps, new alloy designs and fresh colours like Brilliant Black and Magnesio modernise the stance. At 4.4 metres long with 170mm to 180mm of ground clearance, it sits between compact and midsize SUVs. MotorBeam notes it is the most aerodynamic Jeep ever produced, with a shark-fin antenna, chrome strip linking the mirrors and subtle Jeep Easter eggs across the body. Side cladding, roof rails and 18-inch wheels with 225/55 tyres give it visual heft without overstyling. Seven years on, the design has aged better than most rivals and still photographs like a premium European SUV rather than a mass-market crossover.

Interior & Features

Inside, the Compass finally matches its price. A 10.1-inch touchscreen with wireless Android Auto and Apple CarPlay sits beside a 10.25-inch digital cluster that even shows gearbox oil temperature for the geeky owner. Materials feel rich on the dashboard top, doors and armrests, with a panoramic sunroof, ventilated and powered front seats, wireless charging, a nine-speaker Alpine system with subwoofer and a 360-degree camera on the top trim. The seats are firm but beautifully shaped for long drives, and adjustable lumbar plus seat-base tilt help dial in posture. Namaste Car highlights the depth of stitched detailing and Jeep motifs scattered through the cabin. The catch: rear headroom is tight for six-footers, three abreast feels narrow, and there are no rear sunshades or seatback recline.

Performance & Powertrain

Two engines are on offer: a 1.4L turbo petrol making 163hp paired with a 6-speed manual or 7-speed DCT, and the 2.0L Multijet-II diesel producing 170hp and 350Nm, mated to a 6-speed manual or 9-speed automatic with 4x4. The diesel is the one to have. There is mild turbo lag below 2,000rpm and an audible clatter at idle, but the mid-range is muscular and the engine masks speed effortlessly, cruising at 100kmph at around 1,600rpm in ninth gear. MotorOctane found the response linear rather than explosive, better suited to relaxed driving than hard launches. The 9-speed auto is smooth but can feel ordinary; ARAI claims 16-17kmpl, real-world delivers 8-9kmpl in the city and 16-18kmpl on highways.

Ride Quality & Handling

This is where the Compass earns its premium. The Frequency Selective Damping suspension, unique in this price band, delivers a ride that stays composed over broken tarmac at speed while remaining tied down through corners. The chassis feels agile despite the weight, body roll is well controlled and high-speed stability is excellent. Steering is light at parking speeds but weighs up nicely on the move, though feedback through the chunky wheel is modest and the Bridgestone tyres get vocal when pushed. City life is the weak spot: the turning radius is large, low-speed ride is firm over sharp bumps, and engine vibrations intrude at idle. MotoWagon rates the high-speed composure as a clear step above mainstream rivals, justifying the suspension hardware.

Build Quality & Technology

The Compass continues to feel like the most solidly engineered vehicle in its class. The body uses 70% hot-stamped steel, doors shut with a vault-like thunk and the cabin is largely free of rattles. Safety kit includes six airbags, ESP, traction control, electronic roll mitigation, hill hold, hill descent control, ABS with EBD, tyre pressure monitoring and a 360-degree camera on the top S variant. The Unknown Reviewer points out that ADAS is still missing, which is a notable omission at this price in 2025. Connected car tech via the Jeep Life app covers remote tracking, geofencing and even remote disable. Build, safety hardware and chassis robustness collectively form the strongest argument for choosing the Compass over newer, flashier rivals.

Price & Value

Pricing runs from roughly Rs 20 lakh to Rs 30 lakh ex-showroom, with the top diesel 4x4 automatic landing close to Rs 30 lakh on-road. That puts it 5-6 lakh above the Tata Harrier, with which it shares the diesel engine, and around 10-11 lakh above a comparable Kia Seltos. Arun Panwar's long-term ownership view suggests the Compass holds up well over time, but the after-sales network remains thinner than Hyundai, Toyota or Mahindra, and parts can be pricey. Buyers prioritising build, off-road ability and highway refinement get genuine value; those chasing features-per-rupee, three-row practicality or low running costs will find better deals elsewhere. The Compass asks a premium and largely justifies it, but only for the right buyer.

What India's Reviewers Agree On

Consensus

  • Build quality and chassis feel genuinely tank-like and a clear segment benchmark.
  • The 2.0L Multijet diesel with 170hp/350Nm is effortless on highways once past mild turbo lag.
  • Ride quality from the Frequency Selective Damping suspension is excellent over broken roads.
  • Cabin materials, dual screens and feature list now feel premium and class-leading.
  • Rear seat is best for two; three adults and tall passengers find space tight.

Points of Disagreement

  • City usability: some find the steering and gearbox manageable, others call the Compass clumsy, noisy and tiring in traffic.
  • Value: opinions split between calling it a complete near-luxury package versus overpriced against the Harrier and Seltos.

TeamBHP's Take

TeamBHP

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I buy the Jeep Compass?
Yes, if you value build quality, highway refinement and occasional off-roading, and can absorb the higher price and Jeep's limited service network.
What is the Jeep Compass price in India?
The 2025 Compass ranges from around Rs 20 lakh to Rs 30 lakh ex-showroom, with the top diesel 4x4 automatic being the priciest variant.
What are the main problems with the Jeep Compass?
Firm low-speed ride, tight rear headroom for tall passengers, low front bumper flap scraping on ramps, no ADAS, and patchy after-sales support.
How is the Jeep Compass mileage?
The 2.0L diesel returns 8-9kmpl in city and 16-18kmpl on highways. ARAI rates it 16-17kmpl. Petrol is less efficient overall.
Is Jeep Compass good for highway driving?
Yes, the Compass excels on highways with strong diesel mid-range, composed Frequency Selective Damping suspension, low cabin noise at speed and confident high-speed stability.
How does Jeep Compass compare to rivals?
It out-builds the Harrier and Seltos and beats them on ride quality, but is pricier, smaller inside and has weaker service reach.
What is the boot space of Jeep Compass?
Boot is slightly larger and taller than the Kia Seltos, easily swallowing three suitcases, and rear seats fold 60:40 for extra flexibility.
Is Jeep Compass safe?
Yes, six airbags, ESP, electronic roll mitigation, hill hold, 360-degree camera and a 70% hot-stamped steel body make it among the safest SUVs here.
