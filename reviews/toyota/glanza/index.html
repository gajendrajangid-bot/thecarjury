Toyota Glanza: The Jury's Verdict

WAIT
7.1
Jury Score / 10

A rebadged Baleno with Toyota's longer warranty and proven reliability makes the Glanza the safest premium hatchback pick under Rs 10 lakh.

By The Car Jury Editorial 4 July 2026 Synthesis of 4 independent sources 5 min read

The 2025 Toyota Glanza is a Maruti Baleno with a Toyota badge, a longer 3-year/1-lakh-km warranty, and access to Toyota's service network. It gets the proven 1.2L K-Series petrol with manual, AMT and CNG options, a 9-inch touchscreen, six airbags and a 360-degree camera, all wrapped in a sub-4-metre body priced from Rs 6.90 lakh ex-showroom.

What Works

  • Longer 3-year/1-lakh-km warranty extendable to 5 years/2.2 lakh km
  • Proven 88 hp K-Series petrol with 22 kmpl ARAI, real-world 14.9 kmpl average
  • Six airbags, 360-degree camera, ESP and hill-hold standard on top variants
  • Sub-4-metre footprint makes city driving and parking effortless
  • CNG option delivers a claimed 30 km/kg for high-usage buyers

Watch Out For

  • Low ground clearance bottoms out on speed breakers when loaded
  • Hard plastics and fabric upholstery feel a step behind the Hyundai i20
  • AMT lag noticeable during quick overtakes and aggressive driving
  • AC recirculation lets outside air seep in even when switched on

Design

The Glanza sticks close to the Baleno silhouette but distinguishes itself with a chrome-finished grille, LED projector headlamps with DRLs, LED fog lamps and a carbon-fibre-texture bumper insert. At 4 metres long, 1.7 metres wide and 1.5 metres tall, it slots neatly into sub-4-metre tax benefits while riding on 16-inch polished alloys wrapped in 195/55 R16 rubber. The LED combination tail lamps, rear spoiler and shark-fin antenna complete a design that Namaste Car notes stays understated rather than shouty. It is a mature-looking hatchback that will not turn heads but will not date quickly either. The Sport Red, along with grey, silver, blue and white options, keeps the palette conservative.

Interior & Features

Inside, the Glanza runs a dual-tone black and beige cabin with a piano-black dashboard insert and orange backlighting on the switches. The 9-inch SmartPlayCast infotainment supports wireless Android Auto and Apple CarPlay, and pairs with a head-up display, a 4.2-inch TFT multi-info display and automatic climate control. Space is genuinely good for the segment: rear knee room and headroom accommodate six-footers, though the missing middle-passenger headrest is a basic-safety miss. The bigger issue is material quality. Hard plastics dominate touch points, and the fabric upholstery feels downmarket next to the leatherette-equipped i20 and Altroz. Boot space stands at 318 litres with 60:40 split-folding rear seats extending practicality.

Build Quality & Technology

Build feels solid where it matters. The high-tensile-steel TECT body, six airbags, ABS with EBD, electronic stability control, hill-hold, ISOFIX mounts and a 360-degree camera bring the safety package up to segment expectations. Toyota's 3-year/1-lakh-km warranty, extendable to 5 years/2.2 lakh km, plus 24/7 roadside assistance, is a meaningful upgrade over the Baleno's standard 2-year/40,000-km cover on the same production line. Panel gaps are consistent and doors thud shut with reassuring weight. The AC recirculation letting outside air seep in even when engaged is the only recurring niggle flagged after three months of ownership. Reliability, as expected from a Maruti-Toyota co-produced car, has been faultless.

Performance & Powertrain

The 1.2-litre K-Series Dual Jet, Dual VVT petrol makes 88 hp and 113 Nm, with idle start-stop and a claimed 22.35 kmpl. MotorBeam's three-month long-term test returned 13.6 kmpl worst, 16.2 kmpl best and 14.9 kmpl average in Mumbai traffic, translating to roughly Rs 7.24 per km. The engine has enough punch to cruise at 120-140 kmph and remains refined throughout. The 5-speed manual is the enthusiast pick with a light hydraulic clutch, while the 5-speed AMT costs Rs 60,000 more and suits stop-go traffic despite its trademark head-nod between shifts. A CNG variant with 76 hp, 98 Nm and a claimed 30.61 km/kg is offered for high-running buyers.

Ride Quality & Handling

Ride quality is tuned for comfort over sportiness. The MacPherson front and torsion-beam rear soak up broken tarmac reasonably well at city speeds, and the 4.8-metre turning radius makes tight U-turns easy. The steering is light and offers little feedback, which suits urban commuters but disappoints enthusiasts. The real concern, as Arun Panwar's drive underlines, is low ground clearance: with four adults and luggage, the underbody scrapes on tall speed breakers and broken patches. High-speed stability is acceptable but not planted; crosswind sensitivity is noticeable above 120 kmph. On the plus side, the LED projector headlamps throw a long, sharp beam that inspires confidence on unlit highways.

Price & Value

Priced from Rs 6.90 lakh ex-showroom for the base E to around Rs 10 lakh for the top V variant, the Glanza's real value lies in its warranty and Toyota's service reputation rather than any mechanical edge over the Baleno. MotoWagon rates the S trim as the sweet spot for feature-to-price ratio, while long-term buyers should stretch to the V for the 360-degree camera, HUD and 9-inch screen. The AMT and CNG each command a Rs 60,000 premium. Against the Maruti Swift, Maruti Baleno and Hyundai i20, the Glanza undercuts the i20 on price while matching it on features, and beats the Tata Altroz on resale and service peace of mind.

What India's Reviewers Agree On

Consensus

  • Mechanically identical to the Maruti Baleno but with a superior warranty package from Toyota
  • 1.2L K-Series petrol is refined, fuel-efficient and delivers real-world 14-16 kmpl in city use
  • Feature-rich cabin with 9-inch touchscreen, HUD, 360-degree camera and six airbags
  • Low ground clearance is a real concern on broken Indian roads, especially when fully loaded
  • AMT is convenient in traffic but laggy compared to a torque converter

Points of Disagreement

  • Interior quality: some rate it acceptable for the segment, others flag hard plastics as a clear weakness versus the i20
  • Manual vs AMT preference: purists prefer the MT for engagement, commuters find the AMT's Rs 60,000 premium worthwhile

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I buy the Toyota Glanza?
Yes, if you want Baleno mechanicals with a longer warranty and Toyota service. Choose the S variant for value or V for features.
What is the Toyota Glanza price in India?
The 2025 Toyota Glanza starts at Rs 6.90 lakh ex-showroom for the E variant and tops out around Rs 10 lakh for the V variant.
What are the main problems with the Toyota Glanza?
Low ground clearance that scrapes when loaded, hard plastic interiors, AMT gearshift lag, and AC recirculation allowing some outside air ingress.
How is the Toyota Glanza mileage?
ARAI-claimed 22.35 kmpl for petrol and 30.61 km/kg for CNG. Real-world city returns 13.6 to 16.2 kmpl, averaging 14.9 kmpl.
Is Toyota Glanza good for highway driving?
Yes for cruising at 100-120 kmph with good refinement and headlamp throw, but light steering and low ground clearance limit spirited driving.
How does Toyota Glanza compare to rivals?
Same car as Baleno with better warranty. Undercuts the pricier i20, beats the Altroz on reliability, matches the Swift on efficiency.
What is the boot space of Toyota Glanza?
318 litres with rear seats up, expandable via 60:40 split-folding rear seats for larger luggage or weekend trip loads.
Is Toyota Glanza safe?
The top variants get six airbags, ABS with EBD, ESC, hill-hold, ISOFIX mounts and a 360-degree camera on a high-tensile-steel TECT body.
