Volvo XC60: The Jury's Verdict

BUY
7.8
Jury Score / 10

The XC60 is the safest, most understated luxury SUV in its segment with a beautifully finished cabin and excellent ride, even if it lacks the badge appeal of German rivals.

By The Car Jury Editorial Published 13 May 2026 Synthesis of 5 independent sources 1,360 words · 6 min read

The 2025 Volvo XC60 facelift is a Swedish luxury SUV that prioritises safety, comfort, and understated design over outright sportiness or badge appeal. Powered by a 2.0L turbo-petrol mild-hybrid making 246 hp and 360 Nm, it competes with the Mercedes GLC, BMW X3, and Audi Q5 at around Rs 75-85 lakh ex-showroom. It is the thinking buyer's luxury SUV.

What Works

  • Outstanding safety package with intrusive but effective ADAS
  • Phenomenal Bowers & Wilkins audio with Gothenburg Concert Hall mode
  • Air suspension and well-tuned ride absorb Indian roads with composure
  • Premium cabin materials, cruelty-free leather, and superb finish
  • Loaded with features: 360 camera, heads-up display, four-zone climate, panoramic sunroof

Watch Out For

  • Lacks the road presence and badge value of Mercedes, BMW, Audi rivals
  • No sport mode or paddle shifters; performance is adequate, not engaging
  • Touchscreen-dependent controls can be distracting and need a learning curve
  • Boot space at 483-505 litres is smaller than segment expectations

Design

Penned by Thomas Ingenlath, the XC60 facelift looks like a scaled-down XC90 and that is a compliment. The hammerhead Thor's Hammer LED DRLs, vertical slatted grille, and clean Scandinavian surfacing give it understated presence rather than aggression. As MotorOctane notes, the proportions read more crossover than full SUV, which works for urban Indian buyers who want luxury without ostentation. The 19-inch diamond-cut alloys wear Michelin Latitude Sport tyres co-developed for Volvo, while subtle chrome inserts, integrated dual exhausts, and a roof spoiler finish the rear cleanly. Available in Crystal White, black, blue, grey, and Bright Dusk Gold, the XC60 is for buyers who reject Mercedes-BMW-Audi flash. If you want stealth wealth, this is the segment's most elegant suit.

Interior & Features

The cabin is the XC60's strongest pitch. Tan leather, open-pore wood inlays, brushed metal speaker grilles, and Orrefors Swedish crystal gear knob all read genuinely premium. Faisal Khan highlights the orthopaedically designed front seats with power cushion extension, lumbar adjustment, side bolster control, massage, heating, and ventilation. The four-zone climate, panoramic sunroof, heads-up display, and 12.3-inch driver display are all present. Rear seat space is decent for two adults with heated seats, dedicated AC vents, and two Type-C ports, though the transmission tunnel hurts the middle passenger and thigh support could be longer. Door bins swallow one-litre bottles and storage is well-thought-out. The 9-inch Google-powered touchscreen handles nearly every function, which simplifies the dashboard but demands a learning curve.

Performance & Powertrain

The B5 Ultra runs a 2.0-litre turbo-petrol four-cylinder with 48V mild-hybrid, producing 246 hp and 360 Nm, paired to an 8-speed Aisin automatic and all-wheel drive. Zero to 100 kmph comes up in 6.9 seconds with a limited 180 kmph top speed. Volvo has deliberately stepped away from six-cylinder engines and high-output tunes; the philosophy is effortless overtaking and city refinement, not autobahn theatrics. There is no sport mode and no paddle shifters, which will disappoint enthusiasts but suits the brand's relaxed-luxury intent. Five drive modes (Eco, Comfort, Off-Road, Dynamic, Individual) allow tweaking of throttle, steering, and suspension. Claimed fuel economy is around 12 kmpl from a 71-litre tank. Performance is brisk and refined rather than thrilling.

Ride Quality & Handling

Ride quality is a clear highlight, helped by the optional air suspension that can also lower the boot for loading. Over broken Indian surfaces the XC60 stays composed, isolating occupants from sharp edges while keeping body movements in check at speed. Handling is secure and predictable rather than sporty; the steering is accurate and light, which suits urban use better than enthusiastic backroad work. Gagan Choudhary notes the suspension feels a touch firm at very low speeds but glides beautifully once loaded or at pace. NVH is excellent thanks to laminated side and rear glass and thorough sound insulation. The 11-metre turning circle is manageable, ground clearance is sensible for Indian conditions, and AWD with Instant Traction adds wet-weather confidence without serious off-road pretensions.

Build Quality & Technology

Build quality is where the XC60 quietly outpoints its German rivals. Doors shut with a heavy, vault-like thunk, panel gaps are uniform, and there is no plastic that feels out of place. Namaste Car points to the ultra-high-strength boron steel safety cage, foam-cored bonnet for pedestrian protection, whiplash-protection front seats, and laminated glass. The feature list is genuinely exhaustive: 360-degree camera, pilot assist, adaptive cruise, blind-spot with steer assist, run-off-road mitigation, oncoming lane mitigation, cross-traffic alert with auto-brake, four-zone climate with PM 2.5 sensor, wireless charging, and a 1410-watt 15-speaker Bowers & Wilkins system with a Gothenburg Concert Hall mode. Six airbags, ISOFIX, hill descent control, and Euro NCAP 5-star certification are standard. Few cars at any price feel this thoroughly engineered.

Price & Value

At an ex-showroom price of around Rs 75-85 lakh for the B5 Ultra petrol, the XC60 sits squarely against the Mercedes GLC, BMW X3, and Audi Q5. It does not beat them on outright performance, badge cachet, or rear-seat opulence, and the touchscreen feels behind the curve on size. What it offers instead is unmatched safety engineering, superior build quality, the segment's best audio system, and a cabin that ages gracefully. For buyers who value substance over status and want to avoid the German cliche, the XC60 is genuinely compelling. Those chasing performance numbers, the latest screen real estate, or chauffeur-grade rear comfort should look elsewhere. Service network and resale remain weaker than the Germans, which buyers must factor into long-term ownership cost.

What India's Reviewers Agree On

Consensus

  • Class-leading safety with full ADAS suite, six airbags, and 5-star Euro NCAP rating
  • Exceptional Bowers & Wilkins 15-speaker audio system is among the best in any car
  • Build quality, fit-finish, and material choices feel genuinely premium throughout
  • Comfortable orthopaedically designed seats with massage, ventilation, and cushion extension
  • Refined four-cylinder turbo-petrol with mild-hybrid delivers adequate, not exciting, performance

Points of Disagreement

  • Touchscreen size and usability: some find the 9-inch unit dated and fiddly versus rivals' larger screens, others appreciate the Google-powered ease
  • Rear seat comfort: opinions split on whether kneeroom, thigh support, and the lack of cooled rear seats are acceptable at this price

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I buy the Volvo XC60?
Yes, if you value safety, build quality, ride comfort, and understated design over outright performance or German badge appeal. It is the thinking buyer's luxury SUV.
What is the Volvo XC60 price in India?
The B5 Ultra petrol variant is priced at approximately Rs 75-85 lakh ex-showroom, with on-road prices varying by state and registration costs.
What are the main problems with the Volvo XC60?
Touchscreen-dependent controls have a learning curve, the 9-inch screen feels small versus rivals, boot space is modest, and dealer network trails the Germans.
How is the Volvo XC60 mileage?
Claimed fuel economy is around 12 kmpl from the 2.0L turbo-petrol mild-hybrid with a 71-litre tank, translating to roughly 9-11 kmpl in real-world Indian conditions.
Is Volvo XC60 good for highway driving?
Yes, excellent. Refined cruising, composed air suspension, pilot assist adaptive cruise, AWD stability, and superb NVH make it a relaxed long-distance highway companion.
How does Volvo XC60 compare to rivals?
Versus Mercedes GLC, BMW X3, and Audi Q5, the XC60 wins on safety, build, and audio but loses on badge value, performance, and rear-seat opulence.
What is the boot space of Volvo XC60?
Boot space is 483-505 litres, expandable to 1410 litres with rear seats folded. Air suspension can lower the loading bay at the press of a button.
Is Volvo XC60 safe?
Exceptionally. Six airbags, full ADAS suite, boron steel safety cage, whiplash-protection seats, 360 camera, and Euro NCAP 5-star rating make it the segment's safest pick.
