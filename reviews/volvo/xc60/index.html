Volvo XC60: The Jury's Verdict

BUY
7.8
Jury Score / 10

A genuinely safe, comfortable, understated luxury SUV that rewards buyers who value substance over status badges.

By The Car Jury Editorial Published 13 May 2026 Synthesis of 5 independent sources 1,336 words · 6 min read

The 2025 Volvo XC60 is a Scandinavian luxury SUV that prioritises safety, comfort and understated design over German-style road presence. Powered by a 2.0L four-cylinder mild-hybrid petrol making 246hp, it offers a Bowers & Wilkins audio system, orthopaedic seats and a full ADAS suite. Priced around Rs 75 lakh ex-showroom, it competes with the Mercedes GLC, BMW X3 and Audi Q5.

What Works

  • Outstanding safety package with full ADAS and six airbags
  • Class-leading Bowers & Wilkins 15-speaker audio
  • Supremely comfortable front seats with massage and ventilation
  • Solid build quality and premium cabin finish
  • Four-zone climate, air suspension and panoramic sunroof come standard

Watch Out For

  • No diesel or higher-output petrol option; only one 2.0L mild-hybrid variant
  • Touchscreen handles nearly every function, making basic tasks distracting while driving
  • Boot space at 483-505 litres is modest for the segment
  • Lacks the badge value and road presence of Mercedes, BMW and Audi

Design

The XC60 wears Volvo's restrained Scandinavian design language with confidence. The Thor's Hammer LED headlamps with active bending function, vertical grille with the iron mark and concealed fog lamps give the front a clean, premium face. In profile, the 19-inch diamond-cut Michelin-shod wheels and chrome window line work well, though Faisal Khan notes the rear feels more evolutionary than revolutionary. At 4.7 metres long and 1.65 metres tall, it reads more crossover than tall SUV. The crystal white, thunder grey and gold finishes all suit the shape, but the car deliberately avoids drama. Buyers who want their luxury SUV to shout will find the XC60 too quiet; buyers who want taste over flash will find it just right.

Interior & Features

The cabin is where the XC60 makes its strongest case. The dashboard mirrors the XC90 with open-pore wood inlays, perforated Nappa leather, brushed metal speaker grilles and the signature Orrefors crystal gearknob. The four-zone climate control, air-quality sensor, panoramic sunroof and ambient lighting feel genuinely premium. Front seats, designed with orthopaedic input, offer 16-way power adjustment, extendable thigh support, ventilation, heating and a five-mode massage function. Rear-seat space is adequate rather than generous: kneeroom and headroom are decent, but underthigh support is modest and the transmission tunnel intrudes on the middle passenger. Namaste Car highlights the rear gets only heated seats, not ventilated, which feels like an oversight for Indian summers despite the otherwise thoughtful cabin.

Performance & Powertrain

Power comes from a 2.0-litre turbocharged four-cylinder petrol with 48-volt mild-hybrid assistance, producing 246hp and 360Nm, paired to an eight-speed Aisin automatic and all-wheel drive. Zero to 100kmph takes 6.9 seconds and top speed is limited to 180kmph. Volvo has deliberately walked away from six-cylinder engines and the obsession with outright performance. As MotorOctane observes, the brief here is effortless overtakes and relaxed cruising rather than autobahn theatrics. There is no sport mode and no paddle shifters. The engine is smooth and quiet, though throttle response has a slight delay before delivering a linear surge. Mileage hovers around 12kmpl from the 71-litre tank. Enthusiast drivers may want more drama, but most buyers will find the powertrain perfectly judged.

Ride Quality & Handling

Ride and handling reveal the XC60's split personality. The standard air suspension absorbs sharp bumps and broken patches quietly at speed, and the cabin stays composed through expressway undulations. At low city speeds, however, the suspension feels firmer than expected, and the TeamBHP long-term owner notes a 'springy' busy quality on highways that softer-suspended German rivals avoid. Body roll is well controlled but the car does not encourage corner attacks; the steering is accurate and light rather than communicative. The all-wheel-drive system sends power to the front in normal conditions and shifts up to 50 percent rearward when needed. ADAS calibration is generally mature, though MotorOctane flags it as slightly intrusive for Indian driving styles. This is a relaxed long-distance cruiser, not a back-road weapon.

Build Quality & Technology

Build quality is arguably the XC60's strongest suit alongside safety. Doors shut with a reassuring heavy thud, panel gaps are tight, and there are no rattles or squeaks reported even in long-term ownership. The foam-filled bonnet is engineered to reduce pedestrian head impact, whiplash-protective headrests are standard, and the structure uses ultra-high-strength boron steel. Safety kit includes six airbags, City Safety auto-braking for vehicles, pedestrians, cyclists and large animals, oncoming lane mitigation, run-off-road protection, blind-spot monitoring with steer assist, 360-degree camera and pilot assist for semi-autonomous lane-centred cruising. Euro NCAP awarded five stars and the IIHS gave it the highest Good rating. Gagan Choudhary points out the touchscreen-heavy interface demands a learning curve, but the underlying hardware feels built to last.

Price & Value

At roughly Rs 75-84 lakh ex-showroom, the XC60 undercuts equivalent Mercedes GLC, BMW X3 and Audi Q5 variants while bundling features those rivals charge extra for: ventilated and massaging front seats, four-zone climate, air suspension, Bowers & Wilkins audio, full ADAS and a panoramic sunroof come as standard. The trade-off is real: thinner service network, weaker badge recall and lower resale than the Germans. There is no diesel and no higher-output engine option, which limits choice. Volvo's customer service, based on TeamBHP ownership accounts, has been notably empathetic, including a full car replacement when an early unit developed a fault. For buyers who prize substance, safety and equipment over badge prestige, the XC60 offers genuine value.

What India's Reviewers Agree On

Consensus

  • Safety credentials are class-leading with a full ADAS suite, six airbags and a five-star Euro NCAP rating
  • The 15-speaker Bowers & Wilkins audio system is among the best in any car at any price
  • Front seats are exceptionally comfortable with massage, ventilation, heating and 16-way power adjustment
  • Build quality and cabin material finish feel genuinely premium and rattle-free
  • Styling is understated and lacks the road presence of the German rivals

Points of Disagreement

  • Ride quality verdict splits between 'comfortable and absorbent' and 'springy with busy suspension on highways'
  • Rear seat space is rated decent by some and merely adequate by others, especially on thigh support

TeamBHP's Take

TeamBHP

TeamBHP's long-term owner praises the XC60's tank-like build, class-leading audio, supremely comfortable seats and Volvo India's exemplary customer service after a faulty unit was replaced within 30 days. Key gripes: a springy highway ride versus the BMW X1, weak Google Automotive connectivity and thin service network.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I buy the Volvo XC60?
Yes, if you value safety, comfort and equipment over badge prestige. Skip it if road presence and a strong service network matter most to you.
What is the Volvo XC60 price in India?
The 2025 XC60 B5 Ultra petrol mild-hybrid is priced from approximately Rs 75 lakh ex-showroom, with on-road pricing expected to rise by Rs 1-3 lakh.
What are the main problems with the Volvo XC60?
Touchscreen-heavy controls, modest boot space, no diesel option, slightly springy highway ride, thinner service network and weaker resale value than the German rivals.
How is the Volvo XC60 mileage?
The 2.0L mild-hybrid petrol delivers around 12kmpl from its 71-litre tank in mixed driving, helped by 48-volt mild-hybrid assistance and engine stop-start.
Is Volvo XC60 good for highway driving?
Yes. It cruises silently with strong NVH insulation, supportive front seats, pilot assist for lane-centred cruising and effortless overtakes, though the ride feels slightly busy.
How does Volvo XC60 compare to rivals?
Versus Mercedes GLC, BMW X3 and Audi Q5, the XC60 offers more standard features and stronger safety but less performance, badge value and road presence.
What is the boot space of Volvo XC60?
Boot space is 483-505 litres, expandable to 1410 litres with rear seats folded. Air suspension lets you lower the loading bay at the press of a button.
Is Volvo XC60 safe?
Yes, extremely. It has a five-star Euro NCAP rating, IIHS Good rating, six airbags, full ADAS suite, City Safety auto-braking and whiplash-protective seats.
