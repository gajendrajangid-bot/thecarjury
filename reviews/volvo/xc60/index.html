Volvo XC60: The Jury's Verdict

BUY
7.8
Jury Score / 10

A safety-first, beautifully built understated luxury SUV that rewards buyers seeking substance over status.

By The Car Jury Editorial Published 13 May 2026 Synthesis of 5 independent sources 1,220 words · 5 min read

The 2025 Volvo XC60 is the thinking buyer's alternative to the German trio, leading with Scandinavian design, segment-best safety and genuinely premium cabin quality. A 2.0-litre turbo-petrol mild hybrid delivers relaxed performance over outright drama, while air suspension and a feature-packed cabin make it a serious luxury SUV at around 75-84 lakh ex-showroom.

What Works

  • Five-star Euro NCAP safety with full ADAS suite as standard
  • Air suspension delivers excellent ride comfort
  • Bowers & Wilkins 15-speaker audio is reference-grade
  • Genuinely premium cabin with cruelty-free leather and recycled materials
  • Understated, classy design that ages well

Watch Out For

  • Boot space of 483-505 litres trails segment rivals
  • No sport mode or paddle shifters; performance is relaxed rather than thrilling
  • Touchscreen-heavy interface buries climate and key functions
  • Rear seat gets heated function but no ventilation, and no rear sunshade

Design

Thomas Ingenlath's design language carries over with subtle facelift tweaks, and the XC60 still looks like a smaller XC90. Hammerhead full-LED headlamps with active bending, a vertical-slat grille, 19-inch diamond-cut Michelin-shod alloys and the signature T-shaped DRLs lend a confident but understated stance. The 4.7-metre length, 1.9-metre width and 1.6-metre height give it crossover proportions rather than a tall-SUV silhouette. Faisal Khan notes the rear is more evolutionary, with the trademark vertical tail-lamps intact. Dual exhausts, chrome inscriptions and a roof spoiler with integrated stop-lamp round it off. For Indian buyers tired of bling, this is a luxury SUV that whispers rather than shouts, and that is precisely its appeal.

Interior & Features

The cabin sets the segment benchmark for tasteful minimalism. Nappa leather, real wood inlays, brushed metal and a clean dashboard layout from the XC90 deliver genuine Scandinavian luxury. Orthopaedic-surgeon-designed front seats get power adjustment, memory, massage, ventilation, heating, four-way lumbar and cushion extension. The four-zone climate control with PM 2.5 sensor, panoramic sunroof, heated steering and 15-speaker 1410W Bowers & Wilkins system with Gothenburg Concert Hall mode are highlights. MotorOctane points out the leather is cruelty-free, a Volvo signature. Rear passengers get heated seats, AC vents, two Type-C ports and a ski-hatch, but space is merely adequate: kneeroom is decent, under-thigh support could be better, and the large transmission tunnel compromises the middle occupant.

Performance & Powertrain

Powering the B5 Ultra is a 2.0-litre four-cylinder turbo-petrol with 48V mild-hybrid assistance, producing 246 hp and 360 Nm, paired to an eight-speed Aisin automatic and intelligent all-wheel drive. The 0-100 km/h sprint takes 6.9 seconds with top speed limited to 180 km/h. Volvo's philosophy is deliberate: enough performance for confident overtakes and triple-digit cruising, but no sport mode and no paddle shifters. Five drive modes including Eco, Comfort, Off-Road, Dynamic and Individual let owners tweak steering, throttle and suspension. Buyers chasing the drama of a BMW X3 M40i or Mercedes-AMG GLC will find this car deliberately under-stated. As an effortless everyday luxury SUV, however, the powertrain is well-calibrated and refined.

Ride Quality & Handling

Air suspension is the XC60's secret weapon on Indian roads, soaking up broken patches and expansion joints with composure. Ride height is adjustable, the boot can be lowered for loading, and Off-Road mode raises the car for rougher terrain. Handling is secure and predictable rather than playful: the steering is accurate but light, and there is mild body roll when pushed. NVH levels are excellent thanks to laminated side and rear glass, and the cabin stays hushed at highway speeds. Namaste Car highlights touring-grade composure as a key trait. ADAS calibration, including Pilot Assist and adaptive cruise, works well but MotorOctane warns it can feel intrusive in dense Indian traffic, requiring occasional deactivation.

Build Quality & Technology

This is where the XC60 walks away from rivals. Door thunks are vault-like, panel gaps are tight and every touchpoint feels engineered to last. The Orrefors crystal gear knob, brushed aluminium speaker grilles and matte wood inlays elevate perceived quality beyond price. Safety kit is exhaustive: six airbags, City Safety with pedestrian, cyclist and large-animal detection, oncoming-lane mitigation, run-off-road protection, BLIS with cross-traffic alert, 360-degree camera, hill descent control and a five-star Euro NCAP rating. Gagan Choudhary notes Volvo's bonnet uses foam under the cover to reduce pedestrian head impact, a detail no rival bothers with. The Sensus 9-inch touchscreen with Google built-in, wireless CarPlay and four-year free connectivity rounds out a comprehensive package.

Price & Value

At an ex-showroom price of around 75-84 lakh for the B5 Ultra petrol, the XC60 sits squarely against the Mercedes GLC, BMW X3 and Audi Q5. It does not offer dramatically more equipment than the Germans, and it lacks their road presence and badge cachet. What it offers instead is unmatched safety credentials, a more characterful cabin, superior audio and a quieter sense of luxury. For buyers who prioritise substance over show, who want the safest car in the segment and who appreciate Scandinavian restraint, the value equation works. For those buying a luxury SUV partly as a status statement, the German alternatives will feel more rewarding on the driveway.

What India's Reviewers Agree On

Consensus

  • Safety remains Volvo's headline strength, with ADAS, six airbags and class-leading occupant protection standard.
  • Build quality, fit and finish, and the Bowers & Wilkins audio system are best-in-class.
  • The 2.0-litre four-cylinder petrol is punchy enough for Indian conditions but tuned for comfort, not sport.
  • Air suspension delivers a comfortable, plush ride and lets the boot floor lower for easy loading.
  • Front seats designed by orthopaedic surgeons are exceptionally comfortable for long drives.

Points of Disagreement

  • Rear-seat space: some find it decent for tall passengers, others flag tight under-thigh support and a large transmission tunnel.
  • Touchscreen usability: praised for clean integration, but criticised for burying functions and being distracting on the move.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I buy the Volvo XC60?
Yes, if you prioritise safety, build quality and understated luxury over the badge appeal of Mercedes, BMW or Audi rivals.
What is the Volvo XC60 price in India?
The 2025 XC60 B5 Ultra petrol is priced around 75-84 lakh ex-showroom, with the single variant currently on sale.
What are the main problems with the Volvo XC60?
Touchscreen-dependent controls, modest boot space, tight rear under-thigh support, no rear ventilated seats or sunshade, and intrusive ADAS in city traffic.
How is the Volvo XC60 mileage?
Expect around 12 km/l from the 2.0-litre mild-hybrid petrol with a 71-litre fuel tank, helped by 48V regen and AWD efficiency.
Is Volvo XC60 good for highway driving?
Yes, with air suspension, laminated glass, Pilot Assist, adaptive cruise and 246 hp on tap, it is an excellent long-distance cruiser.
How does Volvo XC60 compare to rivals?
It beats the Mercedes GLC, BMW X3 and Audi Q5 on safety, build and audio, but trails on badge presence and outright performance.
What is the boot space of Volvo XC60?
Boot capacity is 483-505 litres, expanding to 1410 litres with rear seats folded, and the air suspension lowers the floor for loading.
Is Volvo XC60 safe?
Extremely. It has six airbags, full ADAS suite, five-star Euro NCAP rating, IIHS Top Safety Pick Plus and Volvo's pedestrian-protection engineering.
