Volvo XC60: The Jury's Verdict

BUY
7.8
Jury Score / 10

An understated, supremely safe and feature-rich luxury SUV that rewards buyers who value substance and comfort over German badge appeal.

By The Car Jury Editorial Published 13 May 2026 Synthesis of 5 independent sources 1,276 words · 5 min read

The 2025 Volvo XC60 is a Scandinavian luxury SUV that trades flashy road presence for class-leading safety, a tank-like build and one of the finest cabins in its segment. Powered by a 2.0L turbo-petrol mild-hybrid making 246 hp with AWD, it rivals the Mercedes GLC, BMW X3 and Audi Q5 at around Rs 75-84 lakh ex-showroom.

What Works

  • Phenomenal build quality and cabin material richness
  • Comprehensive ADAS and six-airbag safety package
  • Reference-grade Bowers and Wilkins audio system
  • Air suspension with boot loading-height adjustment
  • Four-zone climate, ventilated and massaging front seats

Watch Out For

  • No diesel or plug-in hybrid option in India
  • Rear seat space is merely adequate, not class-leading
  • Heated rear seats are pointless for most Indian buyers
  • Thin dealer and service network compared to Germans

Design

The XC60 reads like a scaled-down XC90, and that is a compliment. Thomas Ingenlath's clean Scandinavian language survives the 2025 facelift with hammerhead Thor LED headlamps featuring active bending, a vertical-slat grille with integrated camera, and the signature wraparound tail-lamps that have defined Volvo for a decade. At 4.7 metres long with 19-inch diamond-cut alloys wrapped in Volvo-branded Michelin rubber, it looks understated rather than aggressive. As Namaste Car notes, the proportions are crossover-low rather than SUV-tall, which works for highway poise but costs road presence next to a GLC. There is a headlamp washer, integrated wiper jets, retractable mirrors and dual exhausts. The crystal-white shade flatters the design; darker colours hide the detailing.

Interior & Features

Step inside and the XC60 immediately justifies its price. The dashboard mirrors the XC90 and S90 with tan Nappa leather, real wood inlays, drilled metal speaker grilles and the Orrefors crystal gearknob that glows softly at night. Faisal Khan highlights that the front seats were developed with orthopaedic surgeons, and it shows: 16-way power adjustment, extendable thigh support, five massage programmes, ventilation and heating. The four-zone climate control with PM 2.5 air purifier is genuinely useful in Indian cities. The panoramic sunroof is huge. Rear space is the weak link: kneeroom and headroom are decent for 5'10", but the transmission tunnel and middle hump make it strictly a four-adult car. Rear gets heated seats, which most Indian owners will never use.

Performance & Powertrain

Volvo has retired its six-cylinder and diesel engines globally, so India gets only the 2.0-litre four-cylinder turbo-petrol with a 48-volt mild-hybrid system, producing 246 hp and 360 Nm, paired to an eight-speed Aisin automatic and AWD. The 0-100 km/h sprint takes 6.9 seconds and top speed is electronically limited to 180 km/h. MotorOctane sums up the character well: this is a relaxed, refined cruiser rather than a corner-carver. There are no paddle shifters and no sport mode, just five drive profiles. Throttle response is linear; floor it from rest and there is a brief pause before a clean surge. Overtakes between 80 and 140 km/h are effortless. Volvo's philosophy is enough performance, not excess, and it suits the car's mature brief.

Ride Quality & Handling

This is where opinions split. The air suspension delivers a generally absorbent, quiet ride that flattens broken city tarmac and stays composed at three-digit speeds. The cabin is exceptionally well-insulated; tyre and wind noise are negligible until 120 km/h. However, the TeamBHP long-term owner reports a persistent springy, busy quality on undulating highways that BMW X3 owners may find unfamiliar. Handling is secure and predictable rather than engaging: the steering is accurate but light, body roll is well-controlled, and the AWD system sends power rearward only when slip is detected. Pilot Assist and adaptive cruise work brilliantly on highways but can feel intrusive in Indian city traffic, requiring the driver to dial down sensitivity. The 11-metre turning circle is reasonable for the size.

Build Quality & Technology

Build quality is arguably the XC60's strongest suit. Doors shut with a deep, German-grade thunk, panel gaps are tight, and the boron-steel safety cage underpinning the SPA platform is reassuring. Safety kit is comprehensive: six airbags, full ADAS with collision avoidance, run-off-road mitigation, oncoming-lane mitigation, BLIS blind-spot monitoring, 360-degree camera, hill descent and hill hold. Euro NCAP awarded the previous-generation car five stars. Features are stuffed in without segmentation games: heads-up display, wireless charging, four-zone climate, ventilated massaging front seats, panoramic sunroof, power tailgate with foot-flick fold-down rear seats, and air suspension that lowers for easier boot loading. The 9-inch Google-powered touchscreen handles almost everything; MotorOctane flags that this tap-heavy approach can be distracting on the move.

Price & Value

At an ex-showroom price band of Rs 75 lakh for the B5 Ultra petrol and around Rs 84 lakh for the AWD Inscription, the XC60 sits squarely against the Mercedes GLC, BMW X3 and Audi Q5. Feature-for-feature it offers more standard kit than any German rival at the same price: full ADAS, air suspension, B&W audio, four-zone climate, massaging ventilated seats and orthopaedic-designed front chairs are either optional or unavailable on rivals. The trade-offs are real: weaker badge equity, a thinner service network and only one petrol powertrain with no diesel or PHEV option. Gagan Choudhary notes Volvo's brand pull remains the obstacle, not the product. For buyers who prioritise substance, safety and a quieter kind of luxury, it is genuinely strong value.

What India's Reviewers Agree On

Consensus

  • Best-in-class safety credentials with full ADAS suite as standard
  • Bowers and Wilkins 15-speaker audio is genuinely concert-grade
  • Front seats are orthopaedically designed and exceptionally comfortable on long drives
  • Understated design lacks the road presence of German rivals
  • Cabin fit, finish and material quality feel a clear step above the price

Points of Disagreement

  • Ride quality: some find it plush and well-damped, others call the suspension busy and springy
  • Touchscreen-led UX: praised for cleanliness by some, criticised as distracting and tap-heavy by others

TeamBHP's Take

TeamBHP

A Volvo that won a customer for life: the product is brilliant, but it is the after-sales handling of a faulty delivery that truly stood out.

What owners praise
  • Ventilated front seats were a lifesaver during 45-degree Delhi summer test drives and long highway runs
  • Cabin stays genuinely silent up to 120 km/h with negligible tyre and wind noise
  • 1100W 14-speaker Bowers and Wilkins outperformed the owner's home Bose system within five seconds
  • Volvo India replaced the entire defective car within 30 days without any escalation or harsh emails
What owners flag
  • Suspension feels springy and busy on highway undulations, unlike the stiffer X1 it replaced
  • Google Automotive frequently loses GPS signal or internet connection for maps mid-drive
  • Throttle has a one-second lag from rest before power surges in non-linearly
  • Service network is very thin, and there is no diesel or higher powertrain option

Long-term reality: 2,500 km in 60 days revealed a major snag in the first delivery that forced a tow-back, but Volvo India and Scandia Motors replaced the car entirely within 30 days. The owner says Volvo has won them for life.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I buy the Volvo XC60?
Yes, if you value safety, comfort and understated luxury over badge appeal. It beats the GLC, X3 and Q5 on standard features.
What is the Volvo XC60 price in India?
The 2025 XC60 starts at around Rs 75 lakh ex-showroom for the B5 Ultra petrol, with the AWD Inscription closer to Rs 84 lakh.
What are the main problems with the Volvo XC60?
Thin service network, no diesel or PHEV option, weaker badge value than Germans, tap-heavy touchscreen UX, and a somewhat busy ride on undulating highways.
How is the Volvo XC60 mileage?
Owners report around 12 km/l from the 2.0L turbo-petrol mild-hybrid, with a 71-litre tank giving comfortable highway range for long road trips.
Is Volvo XC60 good for highway driving?
Excellent. The cabin is silent up to 120 km/h, ADAS and Pilot Assist work seamlessly, and the AWD with air suspension is composed.
How does Volvo XC60 compare to rivals?
It offers more standard features than the GLC, X3 and Q5 at similar prices, but has weaker road presence and a thinner dealer network.
What is the boot space of Volvo XC60?
The boot offers 483 to 505 litres depending on configuration, expanding to 1,410 litres with the rear seats folded electrically via boot buttons.
Is Volvo XC60 safe?
Among the safest SUVs sold in India, with six airbags, full ADAS, boron-steel safety cage, BLIS, 360-camera and five-star Euro NCAP rating.
