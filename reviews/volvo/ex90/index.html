The Volvo EX90 carries forward Volvo's understated Scandinavian design language with a heavy focus on safety, minimalism and a Google-powered cabin. It targets buyers who want a luxury seven-seater without the German road-presence theatrics, but its tech-heavy interface and India pricing demand careful consideration.

What Works

  • Class-leading safety suite with full ADAS, blind-spot intervention and pilot assist
  • Bowers and Wilkins audio system is genuinely concert-grade
  • Air suspension and well-insulated cabin deliver excellent ride comfort
  • Solid build, premium materials and sustainable interior choices
  • Google-native infotainment with five-year digital services package

Watch Out For

  • Touchscreen-dependent controls bury basic functions and frustrate non-tech-savvy users
  • Understated styling lacks the road presence of German rivals at this price
  • Third-row and rear-seat practicality is tighter than the exterior footprint suggests
  • ADAS calibration can feel intrusive in dense Indian traffic

Design

The EX90 follows Volvo's familiar Scandinavian template: T-shaped Thor's Hammer LED matrix headlights, a closed-off EV-style nose, clean shoulder lines and a tall but not imposing stance. At roughly 4.9 metres long and 2 metres wide on 20-inch diamond-cut alloys, it has presence without shouting. Namaste Car notes the new bumper, integrated cornering lights and welcome-farewell light sequences. Compared to a BMW X7 it looks noticeably smaller and lower, more crossover than full-size SUV. The understated styling will not turn heads like a GLE or X5, and that is precisely the point: this is luxury for buyers who actively want to avoid attention. Colour choices include Onyx Black, Crystal White Pearl, Denim Blue, Dusk Gold, Red and Grey.

Interior & Features

Inside, the EX90 commits hard to minimalism. A large portrait touchscreen runs Google's native system with built-in Maps, Assistant, Play Store and a five-year digital services package, paired with a 12.3-inch driver display and graphical head-up display. The Bowers and Wilkins 19-speaker, 1410-watt setup with ventilated subwoofer is the cabin's showpiece and is genuinely outstanding. Materials feel premium throughout: fine Nappa leather, ash wood inlays and tactile metal switches. The flip side, as Gagan Choudhary highlights, is that almost every function lives inside the screen, which can frustrate older buyers. Four-zone climate, air purifier with PM 2.5 sensor, panoramic sunroof, power-folding rear headrests and massaging front seats are all present. Third-row access is reasonable but space is best suited to children.

Performance & Powertrain

India gets the B5 mild-hybrid petrol as the headline XC90 powertrain: a 2.0-litre four-cylinder making 250 hp and 360 Nm, paired with a 48V lithium-ion system and an eight-speed Geartronic automatic driving all four wheels. Zero to 100 km/h takes 7.7 seconds, top speed is 180 km/h, and claimed efficiency is around 12 km/l from a 71-litre tank. The philosophy, as MotorOctane explains, is deliberate: Volvo is not chasing 500 hp numbers, only the power you actually need to overtake confidently. Throttle response is smooth, the mild-hybrid layer smooths low-speed crawling, and the gearbox is unobtrusive. There are no paddle shifters and no aggressive Sport mode, but Dynamic, Comfort, Eco, Individual and Off-Road drive modes meaningfully change character.

Ride Quality & Handling

Ride is where the EX90 platform shines. The four-corner air suspension monitors road conditions 500 times per second and adjusts continuously, while the Active Chassis system tunes damping for the selected drive mode. The result is a cabin that glides over broken tarmac and isolates occupants from typical Indian road imperfections, helped further by laminated side and rear glass. Body control on twisty sections is composed rather than sporty, and the steering is light, accurate and predictable rather than feelsome. Turning radius at around 12 metres is manageable for the footprint. ADAS intervention is well-judged on highways but, as noted in driving tests, can feel intrusive in dense city traffic, where some assists are best toggled off for a more natural rhythm.

Build Quality & Technology

Build quality is arguably the EX90's strongest suit after safety. Doors close with a deep, solid thunk, panel gaps are consistent, and every touchpoint, including recycled and sustainable materials, feels properly engineered rather than merely decorative. Seven airbags, whiplash-protection headrests, side-impact protection, pilot assist, 360-degree camera, blind-spot intervention with autonomous braking, oncoming-lane mitigation and a double-locking anti-theft system are standard. Euro NCAP and NHTSA five-star ratings and IIHS Top Safety Pick status back up Volvo's safety promise. The Volvo Cars app adds remote pre-conditioning, status checks and door control. The only real fit-and-finish niggle, flagged in one review, is the slightly rough stitching visible in the 360-degree camera UI rendering of the car itself.

Price & Value

At an ex-showroom price of around Rs 1 crore for the B5 Ultra petrol, the EX90's value depends entirely on what you prioritise. You will not get the badge cachet or road presence of a Mercedes GLE, BMW X5 or Audi Q7 for the money. What you get instead is arguably the safest luxury seven-seater on sale in India, a genuinely premium cabin, an outstanding audio system, and Volvo's distinct anti-flashy character. Volvo's brand pull is weaker than the German trio, which means resale and showroom theatre will lag. For buyers who view a car as a tool for safe, comfortable family transport rather than a status statement, the value equation works. For everyone else, the badge premium will sting.

What India's Reviewers Agree On

Consensus

  • Safety is the single strongest reason to consider a Volvo, with radar, camera and ADAS suites that rivals do not match at this price
  • Design is deliberately understated, more crossover-elegant than aggressive SUV
  • Build quality and material finish feel genuinely premium, with solid door thunks and cruelty-free leather
  • The Google-integrated infotainment with built-in Maps, Assistant and Play Store is a clear strength
  • Ride is comfortable and absorbent, tuned for relaxed luxury rather than sportiness

Points of Disagreement

  • Reviewers differ on whether Volvo's understated styling is a virtue or a weakness against Mercedes, BMW and Audi road presence
  • Opinions split on the heavy reliance on touchscreen controls: some find it clean and modern, others call it confusing for older buyers

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I buy the Volvo EX90?
Buy it if you want the safest luxury seven-seater and prefer understated styling over German flash. Otherwise wait for clearer India EV pricing.
What is the Volvo EX90 price in India?
The related XC90 B5 Ultra petrol is priced at around Rs 1 crore ex-showroom. EX90 India pricing has not yet been officially confirmed.
What are the main problems with the Volvo EX90?
Touchscreen-dependent controls, weaker road presence than German rivals, tight third-row practicality, and ADAS intervention that can feel intrusive in Indian city traffic.
How is the Volvo EX90 mileage?
The XC90 B5 petrol mild-hybrid returns roughly 12 km/l from a 71-litre tank. EX90 EV range will depend on the India-spec battery configuration.
Is Volvo EX90 good for highway driving?
Yes. Air suspension, strong sound insulation, pilot assist and adaptive cruise make it an excellent long-distance highway cruiser with composed ride quality.
How does Volvo EX90 compare to rivals?
Versus Mercedes GLE, BMW X5 and Audi Q7, it trades road presence and badge value for superior safety, cleaner design and a calmer cabin.
What is the boot space of Volvo EX90?
The XC90 platform offers 680 litres with the third row folded and 1874 litres with the second row folded, plus hooks, straps and underfloor storage.
Is Volvo EX90 safe?
Yes. Seven airbags, full ADAS suite, blind-spot intervention, pilot assist, 360-degree camera and Volvo's five-star Euro NCAP and NHTSA pedigree make it class-leading.
