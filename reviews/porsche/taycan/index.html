Porsche Taycan: The Jury's Verdict

BUY
8.4
Jury Score / 10

The Taycan Turbo S is the most engaging electric car on sale in India, with sports-car dynamics that justify its eye-watering price for enthusiasts.

By The Car Jury Editorial Published 22 May 2026 Synthesis of 5 independent sources 1,380 words · 6 min read
Image: Porsche press kit

The 2026 Porsche Taycan is Porsche's first series-production EV and India's fastest-accelerating car, hitting 100 km/h in 2.8 seconds. Priced from Rs 1.69 crore ex-showroom to Rs 2.66 crore on-road for the Turbo S, it pairs brutal acceleration with the precision and feedback Porsche enthusiasts demand. Range and rear space are the compromises.

What Works

  • Blistering 0-100 km/h in 2.8 seconds, fastest-accelerating car in India
  • Precise steering, flat cornering and brilliant brake pedal feel for an EV
  • Genuinely premium cabin with curved 16.8-inch display and excellent NVH from double-glazed windows
  • 800-volt architecture supports 270 kW DC fast charging, 5 to 80 percent in 22 minutes
  • Dual charging ports, adaptive air suspension with lift function for Indian speed breakers

Watch Out For

  • Cramped rear seat, tight headroom and difficult ingress due to sloping roof
  • Only 366-400 litres of boot space, much of it eaten by the charging cable and spare wheel
  • Real-world range of 360-400 km is poor versus the claim of 420-626 km
  • Touch-only climate vent direction control and absurd optional pricing for basic features

Design

The Taycan is unmistakably a Porsche, with the four-point LED matrix headlamps, sloping coupe roofline and a continuous LED tail-light bar that draws constant attention on Indian roads. At nearly five metres long with a 2.9-metre wheelbase and 126 mm of ground clearance, it sits low and wide. Frameless doors, retractable rear spoiler that deploys at speed, pop-out door handles and active aero flaps up front contribute to a low drag coefficient. The carbon exterior package and Neptune Blue paint draw heavily on Porsche's customisation catalogue, where even matching-colour keys and illuminated door sills are paid extras. MotorBeam notes the Cayenne Coupe sibling shares the same sporty silhouette philosophy, but the Taycan looks lower, meaner and more purposeful in the metal.

Interior & Features

The cabin is a tech showcase: a 16.8-inch curved driver display, a 10.9-inch central touchscreen, an 8.4-inch haptic climate panel and an optional 10.9-inch passenger screen that goes blank to the driver's eye. Materials, double stitching, Alcantara and the analogue Porsche clock feel genuinely special, and the Bose 14-speaker 710-watt system sounds excellent. The flaws are practical: AC vent direction must be set through the touchscreen rather than physically moved, the panoramic roof has no blind (only a switchable liquid-crystal film), rear headroom is tight under the sloping roof, ingress is awkward, and the centre rear seat is largely unusable due to the battery hump. Front seats are firm but supportive, with 18-way adjustment, memory and optional massage.

Performance & Powertrain

The Turbo S puts out 751 PS (in overboost) and 1,050 Nm from a dual-motor setup fed by a 93.4 kWh battery, doing 0-100 km/h in 2.8 seconds, 0-200 km/h in 9.6 seconds and topping out at an electronically limited 260 km/h. A two-speed gearbox on the rear axle shifts around 68 km/h, adding a hint of mechanical drama. Faisal Khan confirms launches are repeatable without battery heat-soak, which is rare for an EV. The fake electric sport sound divides opinion: some find it enhances engagement, others switch it off. Range is the weakness: claimed around 420 km, real-world running yields 360 to 400 km, and hard driving drops that further. The base rear-wheel-drive Taycan with 429 PS is claimed at 651 km.

Ride Quality & Handling

This is where the Taycan separates itself from every other EV on sale. Adaptive air suspension, electromechanical anti-roll bars (the optional PDCC Sport), rear-wheel steering that turns up to 2.8 degrees, and torque vectoring on the rear axle combine to hide the 2,295 kg kerb weight remarkably well. Body roll is virtually absent in Sport Plus, the steering is direct and properly weighted with genuine feedback, and brake pedal feel is the best in the EV segment because regen blends seamlessly with the mechanical carbon-ceramic brakes. Ride quality on 21-inch wheels with 30-profile rear tyres is surprisingly compliant on broken Indian roads, and the nose-lift function raises the front by 20 mm under 30 km/h to clear speed breakers. Gagan Choudhary calls it the closest an EV gets to a proper Porsche driving feel.

Build Quality & Technology

Fit, finish and material quality are exceptional, as expected at this price. Panel gaps are tight, the paint finish on the Neptune Blue test car drew specific praise, and the double-glazed windows deliver class-leading NVH with almost no wind or tyre roar entering the cabin. Switchgear has proper haptic feedback rather than mushy capacitive taps, and the curved driver display is sharp and legible. The Taycan is 37 percent aluminium and rides on the J1 platform shared with the Audi e-tron GT. Five-star Euro NCAP rating, ten airbags, an eight-year or 1.6 lakh km battery warranty and a comprehensive suite of driver assists including night vision (Rs 3.27 lakh optional), lane change assist and 3D surround-view cameras round out the package.

Price & Value

Value is the hardest case to make. The range starts at Rs 1.69 crore ex-showroom for the rear-wheel-drive base Taycan and climbs to Rs 2.66 crore on-road Mumbai for the Turbo S, with the test car carrying Rs 37.6 lakh of optional kit. Individual options are absurd: Rs 7.46 lakh for the switchable glass roof, Rs 6.75 lakh for the carbon package, Rs 4.69 lakh for 21-inch wheels, Rs 2 lakh for the 360-degree camera, and Rs 33,000 just for the model name on the sill. The Mercedes-AMG EQS 53 at Rs 2.58 crore offers more luxury and range but is 0.6 seconds slower to 100 km/h. Within Porsche's own range, the Macan EV is the more sensible buy. The Taycan is for buyers who prioritise driving dynamics above everything else.

What India's Reviewers Agree On

Consensus

  • Acceleration is brutal and class-leading, with the Turbo S hitting 100 km/h in 2.8 seconds
  • Handling, steering feel and chassis composure are the best among electric cars on sale in India
  • Build quality, materials and the curved 16.8-inch driver display feel genuinely premium
  • Optional extras are absurdly expensive and easily add Rs 30-40 lakh to the on-road price
  • Real-world range of around 360-400 km is modest for a car this expensive

Points of Disagreement

  • Whether the artificial electric sport sound enhances engagement or feels gimmicky
  • Whether the all-wheel-drive Turbo S or the lighter rear-wheel-drive base Taycan is the better driver's car

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I buy the Porsche Taycan?
Yes, if driving dynamics matter more than range or rear-seat space. The Turbo S is India's fastest car and the most engaging EV on sale.
What is the Porsche Taycan price in India?
The range starts at Rs 1.69 crore ex-showroom for the rear-wheel-drive base, and the Turbo S costs Rs 2.66 crore on-road Mumbai before options.
What are the main problems with the Porsche Taycan?
Real-world range of 360-400 km, cramped rear seats, small 366-400 litre boot, touch-only climate vent control and Rs 30 lakh-plus in typical optional extras.
How is the Porsche Taycan mileage?
Claimed range is 420-651 km depending on variant. Real-world running yields 360-400 km on the Turbo S, less if driven hard.
Is Porsche Taycan good for highway driving?
Excellent. Double-glazed windows deliver class-leading NVH, the air suspension lowers automatically for stability, and brake feel is the best in the EV segment.
How does Porsche Taycan compare to rivals?
It is 0.6 seconds quicker to 100 km/h than the Mercedes-AMG EQS 53 but offers less luxury and range. The Tesla Model S Plaid is quicker still.
What is the boot space of Porsche Taycan?
Around 366-400 litres at the rear plus 81-90 litres in the front frunk, though the rear charging cable and spare wheel eat into usable space.
Is Porsche Taycan safe?
Yes. Five-star Euro NCAP rating, ten airbags, ABS, ESC, active bonnet, lane change assist, optional night vision and 3D surround-view cameras come together.
