Mahindra BE6 — The Jury's Verdict

BUY

A genuinely futuristic, fun-to-drive electric SUV with class-leading tech and range at a price that undercuts rivals by lakhs — provided you can live with a firm ride and Mahindra's patchy after-sales.

8.0
Jury Score / 10

The Mahindra BE6 is India's first truly concept-car-like electric SUV, packing 282 BHP, a 79 kWh battery with 450-500 km real-world range, and premium-segment features like semi-active dampers, 16-speaker Harman Kardon audio and AR head-up display — all starting at Rs 18.90 lakh ex-showroom. It drives like a sports car thanks to rear-wheel drive and a low centre of gravity, but trades rear-seat comfort and ride pliancy for its sporty ambitions. For enthusiasts and tech-lovers, it's a genuine game-changer; for chauffeur-driven families, the XEV 9e makes more sense.

What Works

  • Futuristic, genuinely unique design that stands out in every segment
  • 282 BHP rear-wheel-drive powertrain with 6.7-second 0-100 km/h sprint
  • Real-world range of 450-500 km with 79 kWh battery and 175 kW DC fast charging (20-80% in 20 minutes)
  • Lifetime battery warranty for first owner — a strong EV assurance
  • Loaded with segment-first features: semi-active dampers, AR head-up display, 16-speaker Harman Kardon with Dolby Atmos, auto-park, digital key

Watch Out For

  • Cramped rear seat with high floor, knees-up seating, tight headroom due to sloping roofline and difficult ingress/egress
  • Firm suspension setup — liveable but not comfort-biased; small bumps felt in the cabin
  • Excessive piano black on both exterior and interior attracts scratches and is hard to maintain
  • Mahindra's after-sales service remains a gamble, and early owners may face software niggles on such a complex car

Design

Every reviewer agrees the BE6 looks like nothing else on Indian roads. MotorOctane notes the car 'grabs attention like a supercar' with strangers constantly stopping to ask questions, while Manish Bhardwaj's owner was mistaken for a supercar driver in Rajasthan. RushLane highlights the coupe-SUV stance with massive C-shaped LED DRLs, sequential indicators, flush door handles, 20-inch alloys with 245-section tyres and an aggressive rear with diffuser and roof spoiler. Faisal Khan calls it 'straight out of 2050'. The BE6 measures 4,371 mm long, 1,907 mm wide, 1,627 mm tall with a 2,775 mm wheelbase and 207 mm ground clearance (battery pack 222 mm). TeamBHP appreciates the wide front and rear track that gives it a planted stance, but flags the heavy use of piano black exterior cladding as a maintenance nightmare. A Batman Edition (999 units, sold out in 135 seconds) adds satin matte black paint, Batman decals on doors, wheels and sunroof, and a unique V8-like sonic exhaust sound for Rs 90,000 over the standard Pack 3.

Interior & Features

The cabin is as radical as the exterior. RushLane calls it a 'race-ready cockpit' with twin 10.25-inch freestanding screens, an aircraft-style thrust-lever drive selector, roof-mounted toggle switches and strap-style door handles inspired by the Porsche GT3RS. Features include wireless charging, ventilated front seats, cooled glovebox, panoramic Infinity fixed glass roof with interactive lighting, ambient lights synced to music (Groove Me), driver-facing selfie camera, 16-speaker Harman Kardon with Dolby Atmos tuned by A.R. Rahman, and PM 2.5 filter. However, TeamBHP and MotorOctane highlight real drawbacks: the rear seat feels claustrophobic due to an all-dark cabin, small windows and sloping roofline; the high floor forces a knees-up seating position; under-thigh support is lacking; and ingress/egress is awkward. TeamBHP also flags AC controls buried in the touchscreen, excessive piano black that scratches easily, and a small 455-litre boot with a high loading lip (plus a 45-litre frunk). The 12.3-inch MID with AR navigation in the HUD is praised for clarity.

Performance & Powertrain

Powering the top-spec is a 79 kWh LFP battery (BYD blade cells, assembled in Chakan) feeding a single rear-axle motor producing 282 BHP and 380 Nm. 0-100 km/h takes 6.7 seconds and top speed is electronically limited to 202 km/h — TeamBHP confirmed it hits 200 km/h on Mahindra's test track, faster than the heavier XEV 9e. A smaller 59 kWh pack with 228 BHP is also offered. Three drive modes — Range, Everyday and Race — alter throttle response and steering weight, with a 10-second Boost button on the steering for instant overtakes. MotorOctane emphasises that even Korean EVs costing Rs 50+ lakh (Ioniq 5, EV6 RWD) are slower. Regeneration has three levels plus one-pedal drive, and brake-by-wire blends regen and mechanical braking seamlessly. RushLane and Faisal Khan both call it 'a sports car for the masses'. Charging: 175 kW DC fast charges 20-80% in 20 minutes; 11 kW AC wall charger does 0-100% in 8 hours. Lifetime battery warranty applies to the first private owner.

Ride Quality & Handling

This is where opinions split. RushLane calls the ride 'nothing short of exceptional' even on 20-inch low-profile tyres, crediting the semi-active dampers that adjust damping force in real-time via IMU sensors. Faisal Khan agrees, saying on the worst roads 'you don't have to slow down one bit'. TeamBHP, however, is more measured — calling the setup distinctly firmness-biased, with small potholes registering sharply in the cabin, noticeable side-to-side movement on bad patches, and a constantly 'busy' ride due to the heavy battery, stiff chassis and big wheels. Handling, though, is unanimously praised: wide track, short overhangs, low centre of gravity and a rear-biased weight distribution make it sharp into corners and stable at high speeds. The Variable Gear Ratio steering is direct and weighs up nicely in Race mode, and the 10-metre turning radius is excellent for city use. All-wheel disc brakes offer progressive, confident stopping. NVH is well controlled thanks to acoustic glass and foam-filled Goodyear ElectricDrive tyres.

Build Quality & Technology

TeamBHP notes the BE6 feels solid on the outside with satisfactory paint quality, and the cabin uses generous soft-touch materials on the dashboard and door pads. However, some cabin plastics don't feel premium given the otherwise loaded SUV, and the heavy use of piano black is a lint/scratch magnet. RevLimits calls the interior quality 'so good and premium'. The bigger concern is reliability and after-sales: TeamBHP explicitly warns about 'niggles and bugs in a freshly baked Mahindra, especially early batches' and questions whether Mahindra's service network can handle such a complex car. Manish Bhardwaj's owner interview echoes this — while the BE6 itself has been trouble-free over 6,000 km, the owner's XUV700 has had repeated software glitches, sudden engine shutdowns at 120 km/h and fuel injector leaks over 3+ years, with Mahindra's technicians struggling to diagnose issues. Safety is a strong point: up to 7 airbags, Level 2+ ADAS with 5 radars and 1 camera, all-wheel disc brakes, ESP, 360-degree camera and TPMS.

Price & Value

At Rs 18.90 lakh ex-showroom for the Pack 1 (59 kWh, 228 BHP), the BE6 is aggressively priced — roughly Rs 1.2 lakh more than the cheapest Creta AT but with EV running costs and vastly more power. The top-spec Pack 3 with the 79 kWh battery is estimated around Rs 27 lakh ex-showroom (Batman Edition adds Rs 90,000). MotorOctane argues it's the biggest steal in the segment: it matches or exceeds the real-world range of a Hyundai Ioniq 5 or Kia EV6 (both Rs 50+ lakh) and out-accelerates their RWD variants. Features like semi-active dampers (a 'Rs 20+ lakh BMW M340i feature'), 16-speaker Harman Kardon, AR HUD and auto-park are otherwise found in Rs 60+ lakh luxury cars. The lifetime battery warranty sweetens the deal further. The 999-unit Batman Edition sold out in 135 seconds, and waiting periods are currently around 2 months. For enthusiasts and early-adopters, this is genuinely one of India's most value-packed cars; for pure family-hauling, the XEV 9e costs roughly Rs 3 lakh more and is more practical.

What India's Reviewers Agree On

Consensus

  • Real-world range of 450-500 km on the 79 kWh pack is excellent, rivalling EVs costing double
  • 0-100 km/h in 6.7 seconds and rear-wheel-drive dynamics make it genuinely fun to drive
  • Loaded with premium features (semi-active dampers, 1400W Harman Kardon, AR HUD, auto-park) at a disruptive price point
  • Rear seat is cramped with high floor, limited headroom and a claustrophobic feel due to dark interior and small windows

Points of Disagreement

  • Ride quality: RushLane and Faisal Khan call the semi-active damper setup 'exceptional' even on 20-inch wheels, while TeamBHP finds it distinctly firm with small potholes registering sharply in the cabin
  • Mahindra after-sales: most YouTube reviewers don't address it, but Manish Bhardwaj's owner interview and TeamBHP flag service quality as a serious concern, especially for such a complex, tech-heavy car

TeamBHP's Take

TeamBHP

TeamBHP, after a detailed hands-on review with expert Graaja, calls the BE6 a legitimate head-turner with a 'great handling package' and standout tech, but explicitly warns buyers about three things: the stiff ride (liveable but not comfort-oriented), the cramped and awkward rear seat due to the high floor and sloping roofline, and Mahindra's hit-or-miss after-sales — a real concern on such a complex, tech-heavy car, especially in early batches. Community responses on the forum add that the XEV 9e is more practical for families (better headroom, knee room, bigger boot) for just Rs 3 lakh more, while the BE6 is ideal for young couples or bachelors who'll rarely use the rear seat.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I buy the Mahindra BE6?
Yes, if you're an enthusiast or early-adopter who wants a genuinely fun, futuristic EV with 450-500 km real-world range and premium features at a disruptive price. Skip it if you primarily need rear-seat comfort for chauffeur-driven family use — the XEV 9e is the better choice for that at roughly Rs 3 lakh more.
What is the Mahindra BE6 price in India?
The BE6 starts at Rs 18.90 lakh ex-showroom for the Pack 1 with the 59 kWh battery (228 BHP). The top-spec Pack 3 with the 79 kWh battery and 282 BHP is estimated around Rs 27 lakh ex-showroom. The limited Batman Edition (999 units, sold out) added Rs 90,000 over Pack 3.
What are the main problems with the Mahindra BE6?
The biggest issues are a cramped rear seat with high floor and knees-up seating, a firm ride that sends small potholes into the cabin, excessive scratch-prone piano black inside and out, AC controls buried in the touchscreen, and concerns over Mahindra's inconsistent after-sales service for such a complex, tech-heavy car.
How is the Mahindra BE6 mileage?
The 79 kWh variant has an ARAI range of 682 km and a WLTP-tested 550 km. MotorOctane consistently achieved 450-495 km in real-world expressway driving at 100 km/h. Owner Harry (Manish Bhardwaj's interview) reports 550 km in the city and 480-500 km on highways. Mahindra guarantees over 500 km real-world with AC on.
Is Mahindra BE6 good for highway driving?
Yes — it's very stable at high speeds thanks to a low centre of gravity and wide track, hits an electronically limited 202 km/h top speed, and offers effortless overtakes even in Range mode. Adaptive cruise control with Level 2+ ADAS, brake-by-wire and the semi-active dampers make it a capable long-distance cruiser with 450+ km highway range.
How does Mahindra BE6 compare to rivals?
Priced against ICE SUVs like the Creta, Seltos and Grand Vitara while delivering EV performance that outruns Rs 50+ lakh Korean EVs like the Hyundai Ioniq 5 and Kia EV6 RWD (both slower than the BE6's 6.7-second 0-100 km/h). Against the Tata Curvv EV, the BE6 offers far more power, features, range and a more premium drive — the Curvv's rear space is even tighter. The MG Windsor EV has better rear-seat space but is nowhere near as sporty or feature-loaded.
What is the boot space of Mahindra BE6?
The BE6 offers 455 litres of boot space (VDA-ISO V211) plus a 45-litre frunk under the bonnet. The rear seats fold down for additional cargo space, though the loading lip is high. A skinny 135 mm space-saver spare is stored below the boot floor (which caps cruising speed to 80 km/h if used).
Is Mahindra BE6 safe?
Yes — it's loaded with up to 7 airbags, Level 2+ ADAS with 5 radars and 1 camera (adaptive cruise, blind spot detection, lane keep assist, rear cross-traffic alert), all-wheel disc brakes, ESP, 360-degree camera, hill hold, hill descent, TPMS, front parking sensors, driver fatigue monitoring via an in-cabin camera, and Sentry mode for parked-car surveillance.
What is the waiting period for Mahindra BE6?
Based on owner feedback in Manish Bhardwaj's review, the waiting period is around 2 months after booking. The limited Batman Edition's 999 units sold out in just 135 seconds. Early buyers also get a lifetime battery warranty (first owner only; 10 years / 2,00,000 km if transferred).
Which variant of Mahindra BE6 should I buy?
If budget permits, go for the Pack 3 with the 79 kWh battery (282 BHP) — it unlocks the full 450-500 km real-world range, 20-inch alloys, Harman Kardon 16-speaker audio, panoramic Infinity roof, semi-active dampers and full ADAS suite. The Pack 1 at Rs 18.90 lakh makes sense only if you're happy with the 59 kWh pack's ~400 km range and don't need the premium features.
