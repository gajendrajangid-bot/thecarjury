Range Rover Sport: The Jury's Verdict

BUY
8.2
Jury Score / 10

A genuinely luxurious, capable and engaging SUV for self-drivers, provided you accept CBU pricing and Land Rover's known software niggles.

By The Car Jury Editorial Published 20 May 2026 Synthesis of 6 independent sources 1,240 words · 5 min read
Image: Land-rover press kit

The third-generation Range Rover Sport (L461) lands in India between Rs 1.47 crore and Rs 3 crore ex-showroom, with diesel, petrol mild-hybrid, PHEV and a 4.4L BMW-sourced V8. It blends Range Rover luxury with a sharper, more driver-focused setup, though it remains a CBU-priced indulgence with Land Rover's familiar software quirks.

What Works

  • Strong road presence with clean, restrained Range Rover design language
  • Class-leading air suspension with 900mm wading and 281mm max ground clearance
  • Powerful, refined engine lineup including a 635hp V8
  • Massage, heated/cooled seats, fridge and Meridian 1430W audio as standard luxuries
  • Genuinely capable off-road thanks to Terrain Response 2

Watch Out For

  • CBU pricing pushes the top variant past Rs 2.2 crore on-road
  • Land Rover's known software glitches and electrical niggles
  • Touch-heavy controls and a manual rear sunblind feel out of place
  • Turning radius is large without optional rear-wheel steering

Design

The Sport carries forward the minimalist Range Rover design philosophy under Gerry McGovern, with hidden wipers, flush pop-out door handles, a floating roof and clamshell bonnet. At nearly 5 metres long with a 3-metre wheelbase, it sits 20mm lower and is tuned 35% stiffer than the full-size Range Rover. Faisal Khan notes the fake side vents and bonnet louvres are non-functional, retained purely as design heritage. Lighting is full LED with digital HD Matrix headlamps and animated indicators at the rear. Standard wheels are 22-inch with 21 and 23-inch options. The SV editions add forged carbon visual packs and SV-stamped 23-inch alloys with massive red callipers. It is unmistakably a Range Rover, just sharper.

Interior & Features

The cabin is where the Sport justifies its sticker. The 13.1-inch curved Pivi Pro touchscreen, 13.7-inch driver display and head-up display dominate the dashboard, with a four-zone climate system, PM 2.5 cabin filtration and 30-colour ambient lighting. Front seats offer up to 22-way adjustment with heating, ventilation, massage and Body and Soul audio-haptic seats on SV trims. There are twin glove boxes, a refrigerated centre console, wireless charging, a 1430W 29-speaker Meridian Signature setup and a clear-sight camera mirror. Gagan Choudhary highlights deployable picnic seating from the boot. Rear seat space is generous, though executive-style chairs from the long-wheelbase Range Rover are not offered, and the rear sunblind remains manual at this price.

Performance & Powertrain

India gets four powertrains: a 3.0L inline-six diesel (D350) making 346hp/700Nm, a 3.0L inline-six petrol mild-hybrid, a PHEV on Autobiography trims, and a 4.4L BMW-sourced twin-turbo V8 producing 626hp/750Nm with a 3.9-second 0-100 and 290kmph top speed. The d350 is the sweet spot: linear, refined, and free of diesel clatter, paired to a ZF 8-speed automatic that is smooth if a touch slow in regular mode. Faisal Khan rates the V8 as the bomb for outright pace, while the diesel feels brisker than the petrol thanks to torque. Seven drive modes, launch control and paddle shifters keep enthusiasts engaged. Refinement and active noise cancellation make it serene at cruising speeds.

Ride Quality & Handling

Six-D Dynamics air suspension with switchable-volume air springs lets ride height swing between 205mm and 294mm, with 900mm wading capacity in off-road mode. The Sport is firmer than the regular Range Rover by design, so low-speed ride over sharp expansion joints can thump, particularly on the 22-inch wheels. At highway speeds it settles beautifully. Body roll is well contained for a 2,400kg SUV, the steering is responsive with strong feedback, and lane changes feel sure-footed. Optional all-wheel steering sharpens low-speed agility and high-speed stability, though without it the turning radius is large in tight city use. Terrain Response 2 with auto-detect, adaptive off-road cruise control and electronic active differential make it genuinely capable off-road too.

Build Quality & Technology

Material quality inside is excellent: soft-close doors, semi-aniline leather, forged carbon trims, and tactile metal switchgear. The clear-sight camera mirror, illuminated seatbelt buckles, deployable side steps and head-up display all feel premium. However, Ashwin Singh Takiar's two-year, 13,000km ownership flags Land Rover's reliability reputation: occasional software glitches, random error messages, and one instance where 4-Low refused to engage off-road. The hardware itself feels bulletproof, but electrical and software issues are a known Land Rover trait. Touch-based AC controls and infotainment require learning. Six airbags, Euro NCAP five stars, 360-degree cameras, transparent bonnet view, lane-keep assist and adaptive cruise round out a comprehensive safety and assistance suite.

Price & Value

Prices start at Rs 1.47 crore ex-showroom for the base diesel Dynamic SE and climb to Rs 2.98 crore for the SV Edition Two. The Sport arrives via CBU, which is why it commands a premium over locally assembled BMW X5 and Mercedes GLE. The P425 5.0L supercharged V8 at roughly Rs 1.7 crore ex-showroom is the value pick for enthusiasts, swapping some features for two extra cylinders. The Autobiography PHEV and full-fat 626hp V8 sit at the top. Faisal Khan's view: if you can stretch to Rs 2.2 crore, the full-size Range Rover at Rs 3.5 crore offers more luxury. The Sport is the better choice if you actually plan to drive yourself.

What India's Reviewers Agree On

Consensus

  • Cabin and feature loadout rival cars costing significantly more
  • Diesel d350 and V8 petrol are the standout powertrains
  • Air suspension delivers excellent ride height versatility on and off road
  • Sharper, more involving to drive than the regular Range Rover
  • CBU pricing makes it materially more expensive than locally assembled German rivals

Points of Disagreement

  • Whether to pick the Sport or step up to the full-size Range Rover for chauffeur use
  • Whether the older 5.0L supercharged V8 (P425) is preferable to the newer 4.4L twin-turbo unit at this price

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I buy the Range Rover Sport?
Yes, if you want a driver-focused luxury SUV and can absorb CBU pricing. Self-drivers will prefer it over the full-size Range Rover.
What is the Range Rover Sport price in India?
It starts at Rs 1.47 crore ex-showroom for the base diesel and goes up to Rs 2.98 crore for the SV Edition Two.
What are the main problems with the Range Rover Sport?
Owners report occasional software glitches and random electrical errors. Touch controls and a manual rear sunblind feel out of step at this price.
How is the Range Rover Sport mileage?
Real-world economy for the inline-six petrol is around 3-4 kmpl in the city and 6.5-7 kmpl with calm highway driving.
Is Range Rover Sport good for highway driving?
Yes. Air suspension, active noise cancellation, adaptive cruise and strong powertrains make it an effortless, refined long-distance cruiser.
How does Range Rover Sport compare to rivals?
It is pricier than the BMW X5 and Mercedes GLE due to CBU import, but offers superior luxury, off-road capability and brand cachet.
What is the boot space of Range Rover Sport?
Boot space is 647 litres with rear seats up, expanding to 1,491 litres with the 60:40 split rear seats folded down.
Is Range Rover Sport safe?
Euro NCAP awarded it five stars. It gets six airbags, 360-degree cameras, lane-keep assist, adaptive cruise and a full ADAS suite.
