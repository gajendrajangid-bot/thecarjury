Land Rover Range Rover: The Jury's Verdict

BUY
8.4
Jury Score / 10

For buyers with the budget, no other SUV blends presence, off-road capability and rear-seat luxury this completely.

By The Car Jury Editorial Published 22 May 2026 Synthesis of 6 independent sources 1,344 words · 6 min read

The fifth-generation Range Rover (L460) is a 5.2-metre statement of British luxury that drives like a magic carpet and pampers rear passengers like a private jet. Prices start around Rs 2.36 crore ex-showroom, with the long-wheelbase Autobiography P400 stretching to roughly Rs 4.6 crore. It is rivalled only by the Mercedes S-Class for sheer chauffeur-driven indulgence.

What Works

  • Reclining rear suite with massage, footrest and powered ottoman
  • Air suspension delivers exceptional ride comfort and 294mm max ground clearance
  • Split tailgate 'event suite' with cushions, speakers and lighting
  • Twin chilled compartments, four-zone climate and 35-speaker Meridian system
  • Genuine off-road capability with 900mm wading and terrain response
  • Strong road presence and unmistakable Range Rover design language

Watch Out For

  • Most physical controls have moved into the touchscreen, hurting usability while driving
  • JLR service network is thin outside major metros
  • Single-digit fuel economy on the petrol; diesel returns only 10-13 kmpl
  • Pricing escalates sharply with options, and resale depends heavily on variant

Design

The L460 evolves the Range Rover silhouette rather than reinventing it: flush deployable door handles, hidden tail lamps that only reveal themselves when lit, the signature floating roof and a clean 0.30 drag coefficient. At 5.2 metres long with a 3.0-metre wheelbase on the LWB, it matches an S-Class or A8L in footprint but towers over them. 22-inch wheels, Matrix LED headlamps with animated indicators and a boat-tail rear give it presence without shouting. Namaste Car counts 31 exterior colours plus the SV Bespoke programme for full personalisation. The split tailgate, a Range Rover signature since 1969, remains, and the dual shark-fin antennae, one housing the ClearSight rear camera, are a neat detail. It is design by confident refinement, not reinvention.

Interior & Features

The cabin is where the Range Rover earns its price. Up front, a 13.1-inch Pivi Pro touchscreen, a 13.7-inch digital cluster and a heads-up display anchor a minimalist dashboard, while the centre console hides a refrigerated compartment between the front seats. Rear passengers in the LWB Autobiography get the real theatre: 24-way powered Executive Class seats that recline fully, deploy a powered footrest, fold the front passenger seat forward and offer hot-stone massage, heating, ventilation and four-way lumbar. A second chilled compartment with two SV-branded glasses sits between them, alongside 11.4-inch rear touchscreens, wireless headphones and powered cupholders. As Gagan Choudhary demonstrates, electric-release dual gloveboxes, a ClearSight digital IRVM and four-zone climate complete a cabin that feels closer to a private jet than a car.

Performance & Powertrain

India gets a 3.0-litre inline-six in two flavours: the P400 petrol with 394 bhp and 550 Nm, doing 0-100 kmph in 5.7 seconds, and the D350 diesel with 345 bhp and 700 Nm, doing the same run in 6.1 seconds. Both use 48V mild-hybrid assistance and a ZF 8-speed automatic. Top speed is 242 kmph for the petrol, 234 kmph for the diesel. The diesel is the more sensible choice for Indian conditions, returning a claimed 13 kmpl against the petrol's roughly 10 kmpl. Performance is effortless rather than explosive; this is a 2.5-tonne car tuned for relaxed cruising, not stoplight drags. A plug-in hybrid is offered globally but is not the volume seller in India.

Ride Quality & Handling

This is the Range Rover's defining quality. Electronic air suspension with Adaptive Dynamics monitoring body and steering 500 times a second delivers what Rolls-Royce calls a magic-carpet ride, soaking up Indian expansion joints and broken tarmac without disturbing the cabin. The 50 percent stiffer body shell over the previous generation sharpens responses, and all-wheel steering with up to 7 degrees of rear movement gives an 11.5-metre turning circle, making this 5.2-metre SUV genuinely manageable in city traffic. Body roll exists but is well controlled for the height. Off-road, ride height adjusts from 205mm to 294mm, wading capacity goes up to 900mm, and Terrain Response 2 handles surface selection automatically. It is not a sports SUV; it is a luxury liner that happens to be unstoppable.

Build Quality & Technology

Cabin materials are excellent: semi-aniline leather, real wood, brushed metal and acoustic laminated glass front and rear keep the cabin library-quiet. Soft-close doors, the powered split tailgate with the picnic-style Tailgate Event Suite (cushions, speakers, lighting), and the sheer number of motorised functions, electric gloveboxes, deploying cupholders, powered rear console table, speak to obsessive engineering. Feature highlights include a 35-speaker 1,600W Meridian Signature system, dual sun visors with vanity mirrors, 30-colour ambient lighting, a panoramic sunroof with electric blind and a five-star Euro NCAP rating. The flip side, flagged by MotorOctane, is that electronics reliability is the one area where JLR still has work to do, and the dealer footprint is genuinely thin outside the top metros. Buyers in tier-two cities should factor that in.

Price & Value

Pricing starts at roughly Rs 2.36 crore ex-showroom and the LWB Autobiography P400 on test is Rs 4.60 crore ex-showroom, with the SV variant pushing higher still. At this level the only direct rival is the Mercedes-Benz S-Class, which is a sedan with very different priorities. For buyers who want the highest seating position, genuine off-road ability and the most pampering rear seat in the segment, nothing else delivers the package. Resale is reportedly strong, particularly for the SV. Faisal Khan notes the smaller Range Rover Evoke starts around Rs 82 lakh on-road for those who want the badge on a tighter budget. Within the brand, the Range Rover Sport at our 8.2 rating remains the keener-driving alternative.

What India's Reviewers Agree On

Consensus

  • Cabin luxury, especially the reclining rear 'suite' seats with massage and footrest, is class-leading
  • Ride quality on air suspension feels genuinely 'magic carpet' smooth at all speeds
  • The Meridian Signature 1,600W 35-speaker system is exceptional
  • Commanding driving position and effortless cruising make it ideal for highways
  • Off-road hardware (294mm max ride height, 900mm wading, terrain response) is genuinely capable
  • JLR's limited dealer network and electronics reliability remain real concerns

Points of Disagreement

  • Whether the screen-only HVAC and mode controls are acceptable or a usability step backwards
  • Whether the LWB Autobiography is worth the premium over the standard wheelbase for Indian buyers

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I buy the Land Rover Range Rover?
Yes, if you want the most luxurious rear seat in an SUV and can live with JLR's limited service network outside major Indian metros.
What is the Land Rover Range Rover price in India?
Prices start at around Rs 2.36 crore ex-showroom, with the LWB Autobiography P400 tested at Rs 4.60 crore ex-showroom.
What are the main problems with the Land Rover Range Rover?
Limited JLR dealer network outside metros, electronics reliability concerns, and screen-only HVAC controls that distract while driving.
How is the Land Rover Range Rover mileage?
The D350 diesel returns a claimed 13 kmpl, real-world 10-12. The P400 petrol manages around 10 kmpl claimed, single digits in city.
Is Land Rover Range Rover good for highway driving?
Exceptional. The air suspension, commanding seating position and effortless inline-six engines make it one of the best long-distance cruisers sold in India.
How does Land Rover Range Rover compare to rivals?
Its only true rival is the Mercedes S-Class. The Range Rover wins on SUV stance and off-road ability; the S-Class on sedan refinement.
What is the boot space of Land Rover Range Rover?
818 litres with all seats up, expanding to 2,176 litres with the second row folded. The signature split tailgate doubles as a picnic seat.
Is Land Rover Range Rover safe?
Yes. The Range Rover carries a five-star Euro NCAP rating with six airbags, 3D surround cameras, ADAS features and a high-strength body shell.
