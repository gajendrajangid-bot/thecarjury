# Interlinking Benchmark — The Car Jury vs CarDekho vs CarWale
**Audit date:** May 2026 | **Method:** Live HTML crawl of model, review, compare, and news pages

---

## Methodology

Pages audited per site (equivalent page types):
- Model/overview page for Hyundai Creta (the highest-traffic test car)
- Expert review / verdict page
- Head-to-head compare page (Creta vs Seltos)
- News article referencing the car

All internal link counts exclude navigation, footer, and utility links (privacy, careers, etc.) unless they represent a meaningful signal difference.

## Where CarJury Is Meaningfully Behind

### 1. Homepage link density — the largest gap

CarDekho's homepage carries 40+ followed links to model pages, compare pages, news articles, and editorial features. CarWale's homepage carries 30+. The Car Jury's homepage carries **zero**. This is not a small gap — this is the single most impactful structural difference and the one to fix first.

**What to do:** Already captured in the strategy (Part 6). Minimum 8 followed links from homepage. Both benchmarks suggest 15–20 would be competitive.

---

### 2. Compare links from model/review pages

**CarDekho** on the Creta model page: links to 7 specific compare pages (Creta vs Seltos, Creta vs E-Vitara, Creta vs Brezza, Creta vs Grand Vitara, Creta vs Hyryder, etc.). On the Creta vs Seltos compare page: links to 13 related compare pages — some featuring cars not even on the main page.

**CarWale** on the Creta model page: zero compare links. Their compare section is its own silo. This is a significant weakness in CarWale's structure that CarJury should not replicate.

**CarJury current:** 1–3 compare links (some reviews have 0). Already worse than CarDekho, though better than CarWale's silo approach.

**What to do:** The Type C compare links in the strategy are already the right fix. Target: every review links to every compare page that features that car. The current Nexon EV page (3 compare links) is already the benchmark to match across all reviews.

---

### 3. Rival links from news articles

**CarDekho** news articles carry 5–14 model page links, 2–5 compare page links, and 5–7 links to related news articles — all within a single article. A news piece about Creta vs Elevate links directly to model pages for both cars plus four other comparison pages for similar matchups.

**CarWale** news articles carry 4–6 news links and 3–5 model links.

**CarJury** has no news section yet. This is fine — but the moment news launches, every article must carry mandatory links to the review page(s) of the cars mentioned, plus relevant compare pages. This is already in the strategy (Part 9.3). The benchmark confirms it: CarDekho treats this as a hard rule.

**The delta:** When news launches on CarJury, the minimum standard should be: 2 review page links + 1–2 compare links per article. CarDekho's 115-link news articles are overdone (significant link equity dilution) — CarJury should aim for 6–10 internal links per news article, not 100+.

---

### 4. Review listing page — rivals linked

**CarWale** does something interesting on their `/reviews/` listing page for a given car: they link directly to the reviews listing pages for rival cars. The Creta reviews page links to Duster reviews, Harrier reviews, Taigun reviews, Hyryder reviews. This creates a lateral navigation path across competitors at the reviews-index level.

**CarDekho** does this less explicitly but achieves it via the compare blocks.

**CarJury current:** The `/reviews/` index page links to all individual reviews but doesn't surface rival groupings or segment clusters.

**What to do:** This is not in the current strategy and is worth adding. The `/reviews/` index page (and any segment-specific pages you build) should explicitly group cars by segment and link across the cluster — not just list everything alphabetically. This is a relatively easy UX and SEO win.

---

### 5. Variant-level pages (CarDekho only — do NOT copy)

CarDekho has 86 /overview/ sub-pages from a single model page: price in Delhi, price in Pune, price in Hyderabad, price in Bangalore, specs, colours, videos, EMI calculator, dealer locator, etc. Each of these is a page.

This explains the 180-link count on their model page. The majority of those links are to variant/city-price pages — not to content CarJury should create.

**What CarJury should NOT do:** Build thin city-price pages, variant detail pages, or other spec-sheet duplicates. These dilute your editorial identity. CarJury's advantage is depth of verdict — that's where the content investment should go.

## Where CarJury Is Already Ahead or Equal

### 1. Compare page cross-linking

CarJury's compare pages already link to 5–6 related compare pages in a "You might also be comparing" block. CarDekho does 8–13 (more, but noisier). **CarWale does essentially nothing** — their compare pages are isolated. CarJury's current compare interlinking is better than CarWale and competitive with CarDekho.

**The only gap:** CarJury's review pages don't link back to compare pages reliably. CarDekho does. Fix: Type C links in the strategy.

---

### 2. Verdict structure — significant advantage

Neither CarDekho nor CarWale has a structured verdict. CarDekho's "expert review" pages are long-form text with no score, no BUY/WAIT/SKIP, no consensus/disagreement breakdown. CarWale has ratings but they are user-aggregated, not editorial.

The Car Jury's BUY/WAIT/SKIP + jury score + pros/cons + consensus/disagreement breakdown is a genuine structural differentiator. It creates highly citable, answer-first content for AI search engines (GEO) and makes the "Also in the running" component uniquely useful — a reader can scan scores across rivals at a glance. Neither competitor offers this.

**This is the moat.** Protect and deepen it as the content grows.

---

### 3. Influencer attribution

Both CarDekho and CarWale cite creators in their content without linking to reviewer profiles. CarJury has 37 dedicated influencer profile pages, each with their own interlinking. This is ahead of both benchmarks and creates a content cluster that the competitors lack entirely.

**The gap to close:** Influencer profile pages don't yet link to all reviews they appear in. The existing interlinking audit (Part 1) showed this is partially done. Make it complete — every influencer profile should link to every review where their content was synthesised.

---

### 4. Schema.org / GEO structured data

CarDekho uses some structured data but it's inconsistently applied. CarWale's pages are heavily JavaScript-rendered (their title tag returns "Loading..." on server-side fetch), which suggests poor Googlebot and AI-crawler accessibility. The Car Jury's plain HTML + full Schema.org (Review + Car + FAQ + BreadcrumbList) on every page is structurally superior for both traditional SEO and GEO.

## The Three Benchmarking Findings to Add to the Strategy

These are gaps the benchmark revealed that were not fully captured in the interlinking strategy. They have been added to the relevant sections below.

### Finding 1 — Reviews index should group by segment, not just list

The `/reviews/` index page should display cars grouped by segment (Mid EVs, Premium SUVs, etc.) with cross-links within each group. Both CarDekho and CarWale do some form of this. CarJury's flat alphabetical list misses the opportunity to create the lateral navigation that sends a Creta reader to Seltos and Kushaq without them having to leave the index.

**Add to strategy Rule 3 (hub pages):** The `/reviews/` index page should segment-group all listed cars and each card should link to 1–2 rivals within the same segment grouping.

### Finding 2 — News articles must link to compare pages, not just review pages

CarDekho's news articles consistently link to compare pages alongside model pages. This drives traffic into the compare section from news — a distribution path CarJury's news section should replicate from day one.

**This is already in strategy Part 9.3** but the benchmark confirms the pattern is important. When a news story mentions "Nexon EV pricing update", it should link to both `/reviews/tata/nexon-ev/` AND `/compare/creta-electric-vs-nexon-ev/`. The compare link may be the more useful destination for a buyer who's already decided to shortlist the Nexon EV.

### Finding 3 — Compare pages should link to review pages more explicitly

CarDekho's compare pages link to review pages as well as related comparisons. CarJury's compare pages already link to both review pages featured, but they could go further: link to the review listing pages for both brands (e.g., "See all Tata reviews") and to 1–2 related reviews in the same segment that are not directly compared on this page.

This is currently not in the strategy. **Add to Rule 2 (compare pages):** Each compare page should link to 1–2 additional reviews in the same segment that buyers in this price band commonly also consider — beyond just the two cars being compared.
