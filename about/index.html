About The Car Jury

India's car media has an agenda. We don't.

We find every credible independent review, weigh the evidence, and return one verdict: Buy, Wait, or Skip. Here's exactly how, and why you can trust it.

How It Works

1
Select 5 independent creators

We pick 5 Indian YouTube creators who reviewed the car independently. No CarDekho, AutoCarIndia, or media outlets, only independent voices with no manufacturer relationships.

2
Extract full transcripts

We pull complete video transcripts, often 100,000+ words per car across all 5 reviewers. Every opinion, spec, complaint, and praise is captured.

3
AI synthesis

Claude AI reads all transcripts and maps consensus (what most reviewers agree on), disagreements (genuine splits in opinion), and key facts. Nothing is invented, only what's in the transcripts.

4
Jury score

We score 6 dimensions: Design, Interior, Performance, Ride Quality, Build Quality, and Value for Money. The overall Jury Score is the weighted average.

5
Buy / Wait / Skip verdict

Every review ends with a clear recommendation. Not "it depends", a real answer for a real Indian buyer.

How Cars Are Scored

Every car is evaluated across six dimensions. Each dimension is scored from 1 to 10 based on consensus across all five reviewers, and where reviewers disagree, we surface that too.

Design

Exterior styling, proportions, and kerb appeal, both subjective reaction and market fit.

Interior

Cabin quality, material feel, ergonomics, and feature richness for the price.

Performance

Engine response, gearbox behaviour, in-city driveability, and highway confidence.

Ride Quality

Comfort on Indian roads, broken tarmac, speed breakers, and highway cruising.

Build Quality

Panel fit, material durability, NVH suppression, and long-term reliability signals.

Value for Money

The most weighted dimension. Does the car justify its price against rivals in India?

The overall Jury Score is a weighted average, with Value for Money carrying the highest weight, because that's what matters most to an Indian buyer. The score then maps to one of three verdicts:

Buy Strongly recommended. Earns its price and more. A safe, confident choice. 7.0 +
Wait Decent car with caveats. A better variant, rival, or update may suit you better. 5.0 – 6.9
Skip Not worth your money right now. Alternatives exist or the flaws are too significant. below 5.0

Our Principles

  • No manufacturer relationships, ever
  • No sponsored content or paid placements
  • No test drives arranged by PR agencies
  • All verdicts sourced from publicly available independent reviews
  • Disagreements between reviewers are surfaced, not hidden
  • Indian road conditions, Indian prices, Indian buyer priorities

Why We Built This

Most car reviewers in India get their test drives arranged by manufacturers. Manufacturer PR decides who gets early access, which journalists are invited, and sometimes what can and cannot be said. The result is reviews that read like press releases.

We built The Car Jury because we got tired of it. We don't take manufacturer test drives. We don't accept sponsorships. We have no relationships with automakers, and we never will.

Instead, we find India's best independent creators, people who funded their own access to the car, read every word of their transcripts, weigh their opinions against each other, and give you one clear, honest verdict. No agenda. No conflicts. Just the truth about the car.
