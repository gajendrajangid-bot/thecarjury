Tata Nexon EV vs
Maruti Suzuki e Vitara

A proven, feature-loaded EV you can buy today versus a promising debut still finding its price.

Choose the
Tata Nexon EV
  • You charge at home overnight and want 400+ km of real-world range without overthinking every trip.
  • You have a family of four and need a boot that swallows a weekend bag without a game of Tetris.
  • You want to plug a kettle or power tools into your car on a camping trip, thanks to V2L capability.
  • You are a first-time EV buyer who values Tata's established service network and resale precedent over novelty.
  • You care about active safety and want six airbags, a reinforced side structure, and paddle-shift regen as standard.
  • You want the car in your driveway this month, not after waiting for a pricing announcement.
Choose the
Maruti Suzuki e Vitara
  • You prioritise brand trust above all else and Maruti's dealer reach and after-sales reputation is a non-negotiable comfort.
  • You do mostly urban commutes and the 49 kWh pack's range is more than sufficient for your daily 40-60 km.
  • You want ventilated front seats, a panoramic roof, and a 10-way powered driver seat as standard in an EV at launch.
  • You value a composed, linear driving feel over the punchy, eager throttle response of performance-tuned EVs.
  • You are buying this as a second household car and a 5-star Bharat NCAP rating is your headline safety criterion.
  • You are happy to wait for confirmed pricing and believe Maruti will undercut expectations aggressively enough to justify it.
Where They Diverge
Four situations that tip the decision

Both score 7.8/10. In real life, they are built for different people.

Long weekend highway drive with family

The Nexon EV's 40.5 kWh pack claims 465 km ARAI, and V3Cars rates it as the most capable mass-market long-range option available today. The e Vitara's 61 kWh variant competes on paper, but with no confirmed charging network partnerships or real-world range data yet published, planning a highway trip in it remains guesswork. For a family that does Bangalore to Coorg or Delhi to Jaipur regularly, the Nexon EV is the known quantity.

Edge: Creta
City parking and daily urban commuting

Both cars fit comfortably in standard urban parking bays, but the Nexon EV's stiff low-speed ride makes pothole-riddled city roads noticeably uncomfortable. The e Vitara, according to Faisal Khan, drives smoothly and linearly in stop-start traffic, which suits a buyer whose car never leaves the city. If your commute is under 60 km daily on mixed urban roads, the e Vitara's ride composure is the easier daily companion.

Edge: Duster
Resale value and ownership peace of mind

The Nexon EV has a documented resale history in India, an established Tata EV service network, and a buyer community that has lived with the car through multiple model years. The e Vitara is Maruti's first EV, which means resale trajectories are entirely unproven. Car Blogger notes that Tata's EV ecosystem maturity is a genuine ownership advantage that first-generation Maruti EV buyers simply cannot access yet.

Edge: Creta
Cabin usability on a long drive

The Nexon EV's Arcade.EV touchscreen with Netflix, YouTube, and paddle-shift regen controls keeps passengers and the driver engaged without menu-diving. The e Vitara's dual-screen setup looks premium but buries basic functions like seat ventilation inside sub-menus, a frustration Amit Khare flagged during his review. On a four-hour drive, the Nexon EV's ergonomic logic beats the e Vitara's visual ambition.

Edge: Creta

Design
The Nexon EV's full-width connected LED light bar doubles as a charging-status indicator, a feature Car Blogger called genuinely clever rather than cosmetic. The EV-specific alloys and functional air curtains reinforce that this is a car designed around its powertrain. The facelift silhouette is sharper, though the overall shape remains familiar to existing Nexon buyers.
8.0 / 10
Faisal Khan rates the e Vitara among the best-looking Marutis in years. The Y-shaped DRLs, hidden rear door handles, and active grille shutters create a cohesive modern EV face that feels genuinely fresh for the brand. It looks larger and more planted than its dimensions suggest.
7.5 / 10
Design-first buyerse Vitara reads as a cleaner, more resolved design with fewer busy elements
Interior
The Empowered variant's blue-and-black leather-like dashboard and flat-bottom steering wheel feel premium for the segment. The 12.3-inch Arcade.EV screen is the largest and most content-rich in class, and the bundled Jio dongle makes OTA streaming genuinely usable. Ergonomic quirks with minor controls remain, but the core layout is intuitive.
7.5 / 10
The e Vitara's cabin is Maruti's most premium ever on paper: soft-touch surfaces, 12-colour ambient lighting, and a 10-way powered driver seat. Yet Amit Khare found that basic functions are buried in sub-menus, making the dual-screen system feel more complicated than convenient. The 238-litre boot is a real-world penalty no spec sheet can paper over.
6.5 / 10
Buyers who prioritise usabilityNexon EV's layout rewards daily use without a learning curve
Performance
The Gen-2 motor produces 145 PS and 250 Nm. Despite losing 38 Nm over its predecessor, the 20 kg lighter motor delivers a 0-100 km/h time of 8.9 seconds and a 150 km/h top speed. Sport mode sharpens throttle response noticeably, and paddle-shift regen adds driver engagement that V3Cars rated as a genuine improvement.
7.5 / 10
The 61 kWh e Vitara produces 174 PS and hits 0-100 km/h in 8.7 seconds, marginally quicker on paper. However, Faisal Khan noted the delivery is smooth and linear rather than punchy, which suits comfort-oriented drivers but disappoints those expecting EV urgency. India misses the AWD variant with 307 Nm entirely.
7.0 / 10
Drivers who want EV punchNexon EV's Sport mode delivers more immediate, satisfying throttle response
Ride Quality
At highway speeds the Nexon EV settles into a composed, stable ride. Low-speed ride quality is a known weakness: the suspension is stiff over sharp urban bumps and speed breakers. Gagan noted this is a compromise Tata made for handling stability rather than city comfort.
7.5 / 10
The e Vitara rides smoothly across urban surfaces, with Faisal Khan describing the experience as linear and unflustered in city traffic. Whether the ride holds up on rougher state highways remains unverified in long-term tests. Initial impressions suggest it is the more comfortable daily urban companion.
7.0 / 10
City-first commuterse Vitara's low-speed compliance suits pothole-heavy urban roads better
Build Quality
Tata has reinforced the Nexon EV's side structure for the facelift and added six airbags as standard, which is a meaningful safety upgrade. Panel-gap consistency is still an occasional concern, a point Car Blogger flagged as the one area where fit and finish lags segment expectations. Overall solidity is acceptable for the price.
7.0 / 10
The e Vitara carries a 5-star Bharat NCAP rating and is built on a new dedicated EV platform, which gives it structural credibility from day one. Gagan Choudhary noted the cabin materials feel genuinely premium rather than aspirational. Being a first-generation product, long-term durability data does not yet exist.
7.0 / 10
Safety-conscious familiesBoth earn this, but e Vitara's NCAP rating gives it a certified edge
Value for Money
At ₹14.49 lakh entry pricing, the Nexon EV bundles a 7.2 kW AC charger, six airbags, V2L/V2V, and Arcade.EV as standard. V3Cars concluded it remains India's most feature-rich mass-market electric car per rupee. You are paying for a product with proven ownership costs, established resale, and an active EV service network.
8.0 / 10
The e Vitara's pricing is still unannounced, which makes any value assessment speculative. Its feature list is strong and the Maruti badge commands trust, but until a sticker price is confirmed, buyers cannot evaluate whether the smaller boot and ergonomic compromises are justified by the cost. Waiting for launch pricing is the only rational call.
6.5 / 10
Buyers who need certainty nowNexon EV's known pricing and features make the buying decision straightforward today
Real-World Range and Charging
The 40.5 kWh pack claims 465 km ARAI, with real-world range typically landing in the 320-360 km band. The standard 7.2 kW AC charger is a meaningful inclusion that reduces dependence on public DC fast chargers. V2L and V2V capability add genuine utility for buyers who camp, travel, or want to charge other devices from the car.
The 61 kWh pack is the larger battery in this comparison and claims competitive range on paper, but no independent real-world range test is available yet. Charging infrastructure partnerships and home charger bundling details are unconfirmed at the time of writing. Until the e Vitara is in buyers' hands, its range story is a promise rather than a proven track record.
Long-distance EV travellersNexon EV's proven range data and V2L capability make it the safer long-trip bet today
