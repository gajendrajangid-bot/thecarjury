Most buyers decide here. Read this before anything else.

Choose the
Mahindra XUV 3XO
  • You drive long stretches on expressways and want the most powerful engine in this segment to make overtaking effortless.
  • You have two adults in the front and rarely carry more than one person in the back, so the tight rear isn't a dealbreaker.
  • You work in tech or finance and want a cabin that looks and feels premium enough to impress without crossing into a higher segment.
  • You want a diesel SUV for high-mileage daily commuting, because the 3XO is the only sub-4m option here with one.
  • Safety ratings genuinely influence your purchase, and five stars with six airbags as standard gives you real peace of mind.
  • You find most sub-4m SUVs visually forgettable and want something that polarises opinions rather than blends into traffic.
Choose the
Maruti Brezza
  • You have a family of four with growing kids and need rear-seat space, a usable boot, and a practical daily that accommodates everyone.
  • You live in a city where Maruti service centres are within five kilometres, and low maintenance cost matters as much as purchase price.
  • You plan to sell the car in four to five years and want the strongest resale value in the segment without negotiation.
  • Your household has an older driver or someone less confident, and a smooth, predictable naturally aspirated engine is genuinely reassuring.
  • You cover moderate city distances, the mild hybrid fuel economy keeps running costs low, and performance is not a priority.
  • You are buying your first SUV and want a car that demands nothing from you in terms of learning, maintenance surprises, or brand loyalty.
Where They Diverge
Four situations that tip the decision

Both score 7.6/10. In real life, they are built for different people.

Weekend expressway run with loaded car

The 3XO's 130 hp turbo-GDI with the Aisin torque converter pulls cleanly from 80 kmph without a downshift hunt. The Brezza's 103 PS naturally aspirated unit requires planning ahead on inclines, especially with four occupants. MotorOctane's five-car comparison flagged the Brezza's highway lethargy as its clearest weakness against turbocharged rivals.

Edge: Mahindra XUV 3XO
Daily family school run with three kids

The Brezza's wider rear bench, larger boot and predictable low-speed manners make it a better fit for chaotic school gates. The 3XO's rear seat is the tightest in its class for three abreast, and the polarising front design attracts attention parents may not want. Namaste Car noted the Brezza's practicality as a core reason families repeatedly shortlist it.

Edge: Maruti Brezza
Resale value after four years

Maruti's resale dominance in India is structural, not anecdotal. The Brezza consistently holds 60 to 65 percent of its value at the four-year mark in most markets. The 3XO carries Mahindra's improving but still unproven residuals, and a polarising face can soften used-car demand further.

Edge: Maruti Brezza
Spirited drive on a hilly weekend route

The 3XO was built around a driver-focused brief inherited from the XUV 300, and the chassis remains one of the most composed in the segment on flowing roads. Faisal Khan described the turbo-GDI as the car's strongest argument, and the tight body control makes ghat roads feel rewarding. The Brezza rides comfortably but does not invite the same engagement.

Edge: Mahindra XUV 3XO

Design
Mahindra replaced the XUV 300's anonymous face with connected DRLs, a wide grille and high-mounted fog lamps that divide opinion sharply. The rear and three-quarter view is resolved and genuinely smart. Faisal Khan called the front styling aggressive but acknowledged it stands out in a segment full of safe choices.
7.0 / 10
The Brezza facelift sharpens without reinventing: LED projector headlamps with dual DRL strips, reduced chrome and precision-cut alloys give it a modern but inoffensive character. It reads larger than its footprint from the side. Gagan Choudhary noted it looks like a credible family SUV rather than a statement piece.
7.5 / 10

Interior
The 3XO's dual 10.25-inch screens, dual-zone climate control, Harman Kardon audio and 65W USB-C port are lifted from the XUV 700 and feel genuinely class-leading. The cabin is the widest in segment. MotorBeam rated it a clear leap over the old XUV 300 and ahead of most rivals in perceived quality.
7.5 / 10
The Brezza's refreshed dashboard adds brown inserts, a flat-bottom steering wheel and a 9-inch SmartPlay Pro+ screen with a colour head-up display. The layout is sensible and well-built, though ambience lags the 3XO. Namaste Car called it a well-organised cabin that prioritises function over flair.
7.0 / 10
Tech-first buyers3XO's dual-screen setup and premium audio are a level above
Performance
The 130 hp turbo-GDI is the most powerful engine in this segment, with 250 Nm on overboost and a well-calibrated Aisin six-speed automatic. MotorBeam recorded 12 to 14 km/l on highways. The diesel option adds another dimension for high-mileage buyers.
8.0 / 10
The 1.5-litre K15C with 12V mild hybrid assistance produces 103 PS and 136 Nm. It is refined and frugal in the city but MotorOctane flagged the lack of urgency at highway speeds as the Brezza's clearest limitation. There is no turbo, no diesel and no quick overtaking response on demand.
6.5 / 10

Ride Quality
The 3XO absorbs broken urban roads with composure, and the suspension tuning is firm enough for confident handling without being harsh on passengers. MotorInc noted it handles speed breakers better than its sporty bias suggests.
8.0 / 10
The Brezza's ride is pliant and unflustered across most road surfaces, earning consistent praise from reviewers for its long-distance comfort. Arun Panwar called it one of the most settled sub-4m rides for mixed Indian road conditions.

Build Quality
Panel gaps are acceptable and the cabin materials feel premium at this price, helped by the XUV 700-derived interior components. The 5-star NCAP rating with six standard airbags adds structural confidence. MotorBeam found fit and finish consistent across the range.
7.5 / 10
Maruti's assembly quality is dependable and well-understood by the market. The Brezza's body panels are solid, and long-term reliability data supports its reputation for low ownership stress. Gagan Choudhary noted the Brezza feels built to last without demanding attention.

Value for Money
At Rs 7.49 lakh entry and strong mid-variant pricing, the 3XO packs a 130 hp engine, five-star safety and a premium cabin into the sub-4m budget. MotorBeam flagged the MX3 and AX5 variants as the sweet spot for value. The diesel adds a unique angle no rival in this list offers.
8.0 / 10
The Brezza's sticker price is competitive but the running costs, resale value and service accessibility push its lifetime value proposition strongly. MotorOctane noted that for buyers who calculate total cost of ownership over five years, the Brezza often wins on paper.
7.0 / 10
Upfront feature-per-rupee buyers3XO offers more specification per lakh at comparable trims
Practicality
The 3XO's boot is one of the smallest in segment at around 257 litres, and rear legroom is tight for three adults. It works well for couples or small families but struggles when the whole family plus luggage needs to fit.
The Brezza offers a more usable rear bench, a larger boot and better packaging for four occupants on a long trip. Namaste Car consistently points to this as the reason families default to it over sportier alternatives.
