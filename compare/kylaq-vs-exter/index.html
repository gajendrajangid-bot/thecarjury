A driver's SUV versus a city-smart feature box: choose your priority.

For: This comparison is for first-time SUV buyers choosing between a polished, safety-focused driver's car and a feature-rich urban runabout. If you need a diesel or a panoramic sunroof, look at the Nexon or Brezza instead.

Same price. Different life.

Most buyers decide here. Read this before anything else.

Choose the
Skoda Kylaq
  • You drive long stretches on expressways every weekend and want a car that feels planted, not nervous, at triple-digit speeds.
  • You have a family of four and regularly pack for weekend getaways where boot space decides whether the stroller goes in or stays home.
  • You value build quality the way some people value jewellery: the door thud, the panel gaps and the 5-star NCAP rating matter to you personally.
  • You are buying your second car and want something that holds its value and ages gracefully without busy styling that dates in three years.
  • You live in a city but take mountain roads or broken village tracks several times a year and want the confidence of an electronic differential lock.
  • You are the kind of driver who notices steering weight and chassis response, and you will feel cheated by a car that goes numb the moment you push it.
Choose the
Hyundai Exter
  • You live in a congested metro, your daily commute is under 20 km, and you want a car that is easy to park, easy to insure and easy to love.
  • You want a sunroof, a dashcam and wireless connectivity at a price that leaves room in the budget for a good extended warranty.
  • You are buying your first car and want the reassurance of Hyundai's wide service network and low maintenance reputation across smaller cities.
  • Your co-passengers matter as much as the driver: the Exter's taller roofline, wider door aperture and practical interior layout make every occupant comfortable.
  • You do most of your driving in stop-and-go traffic and the 1.2L petrol's smooth, unstressed character in city conditions suits your pace better than a turbocharged engine.
  • You want a car that looks like an SUV to neighbours and family without paying SUV money, and the Exter's flared arches and H-shaped DRLs deliver exactly that.
Where They Diverge
Four situations that tip the decision

The Kylaq scores 8.4/10, the Exter 6.9/10. In real life, they are built for different people.

Long expressway run with family

The Kylaq's 1.0L TSI pulls cleanly past 100 kmph and the MQB A0 IN platform keeps the body composed under load. The Exter's 1.2L naturally aspirated motor runs out of enthusiasm past 80 kmph, and reviewers at MotorBeam noted the highway posture feels strained on long hauls. Families who spend weekends on National Highways will feel the difference within the first hour.

Edge: Skoda Kylaq
Tight urban parking on a Tuesday morning

Both cars are sub-4m, but the Exter's taller stance and lighter steering make it easier to thread through multi-storey carparks. Faisal Khan highlighted the Exter's effortless low-speed maneuverability as one of its strongest real-world qualities. The Kylaq's heavier, more communicative steering is a feature on highways but asks more effort in congested city blocks.

Edge: Hyundai Exter
Resale value three years from now

Skoda's MQB-platform cars have historically held value better than Grand i10 Nios-based derivatives in the used-car market, and the Kylaq's 5-star safety rating and segment-first positioning strengthen that case. The Exter's broader dealer footprint and Hyundai brand loyalty keep depreciation reasonable, but the Kylaq starts from a stronger premium. Buyers who plan to sell in 2027 should factor this in.

Edge: Skoda Kylaq
Feature checklist conversation at the showroom

The Exter arrives with a sunroof, dashcam, six airbags, wireless charging and a 4.2-inch colour MID at prices where the Kylaq is still in base trim. MotorInc pointed out the Exter punches above its weight on features per rupee at mid-spec variants. The Kylaq catches up on top trims but buyers who want everything at an accessible price point will find the Exter harder to argue against.

Edge: Hyundai Exter
Dimension by Dimension
What the jury said, head-to-head

Scores shown inline. "Best for" tells you who each result matters to.

Axis Skoda Kylaq Hyundai Exter Best for
Design
The Kylaq wears Skoda's new split-headlamp language with clean surfacing, 17-inch alloys and compact tail lamps that Biturbo Media argues will age better than busier rivals. The proportions are mature and restrained, closer to a shrunken Q2 than a typical sub-4m SUV. It is a car that impresses on second look rather than first.
8.0 / 10
The Exter leads with bold H-shaped DRLs, a shimmering grille and flared arches that project genuine SUV stance despite the small footprint. Faisal Khan noted the body cladding lacks perfect symmetry on some panels, but the overall road presence is strong for the segment. It reads louder and younger than the Kylaq from the kerb.
7.5 / 10
Understated style seekersKylaq's restrained design ages better
Interior
The Kylaq offers ventilated front seats with six-way power adjust for both driver and co-passenger, a feature rare in this segment. The 446L boot is class-leading. Rear knee room is adequate for passengers under 5'10" but the cabin prioritises quality over quantity.
7.0 / 10
The Exter's dashboard is lifted from the Grand i10 Nios but gains all-black trim, body-colour accents and a leatherette steering wheel that feel a tier above the price. MotorBeam praised the fit and finish consistency across cabin surfaces. The taller roofline gives rear passengers more headroom than the Kylaq delivers.
7.8 / 10
Feature-first city buyersExter packs more comfort touches at accessible prices
Build & Safety
The Kylaq's MQB A0 IN structure earned a 5-star Bharat NCAP rating with six standard airbags across all variants. Doors shut with what reviewers consistently describe as a German thud, and panel gaps are tight by any segment standard. The electronic differential lock is a genuine engineering addition, not a marketing line.
9.2 / 10
The Exter arrives with six airbags, ESC, VSM, hill-hold and ISOFIX mounts as standard, which is a strong safety baseline for its price band. TeamBHP owners report a well-screwed-together cabin, though scratchy plastics are present as expected at this price point. It has not yet been rated by Bharat NCAP, which leaves a question mark for safety-first buyers.
7.2 / 10
Safety-prioritising familiesKylaq's 5-star rating and tank-like structure set the benchmark
Performance
The 1.0L TSI delivers 115 PS with a broad torque band that makes overtaking on highways feel effortless and unhurried. MotorOctane noted the engine pulls cleanly from low revs, reducing the need to downshift in city traffic. The DSG gearbox on top trims adds quick, polished shifts that match the engine's character.
8.0 / 10
The 1.2L Kappa petrol is smooth, refined and unstressed in city conditions, which is exactly what its buyers need. It runs out of enthusiasm on open roads, and MotorInc confirmed the highway passing game requires planning. For a car that mostly sees traffic signals and school runs, the engine is adequately matched to its mission.
6.8 / 10
Enthusiast or highway commutersKylaq's TSI engine operates in a different performance class
Ride Quality
The Kylaq absorbs sharp urban potholes with composure that surprises given its sporty suspension tuning. MotorBeam highlighted that the balance between ride comfort and handling precision is the best in the sub-4m segment. At highway speeds, the body stays settled and free of the float that affects taller, softer-sprung rivals.
8.5 / 10
The Exter's softer suspension setup prioritises comfort over control, which works well on broken city roads and makes the car forgiving for nervous drivers. At speed, the taller stance introduces more body roll than the Kylaq. For daily city use the ride is perfectly pleasant; it is only when the road opens up that the difference becomes apparent.
7.5 / 10
Mixed road, spirited driversKylaq balances comfort and control more convincingly
Value for Money
The Kylaq starts at Rs 9.15 lakh and the top trim costs significantly more than a loaded Exter. What you get for that premium is a better platform, superior safety certification and stronger resale. It is value for buyers who define value as total cost of ownership, not features per rupee at purchase.
7.5 / 10
The Exter starts at Rs 7.13 lakh and its mid-spec variants deliver sunroof, dashcam and wireless connectivity at prices where the Kylaq is still entry-level. MotorInc rated the Exter's feature-to-price ratio as one of the strongest in the micro-SUV class. For buyers on a strict budget who want a long equipment list, the Exter is genuinely hard to beat.
7.5 / 10
Budget-conscious first buyersExter delivers more features per rupee at accessible trims
Practicality
The Kylaq's 446L boot is the largest in the sub-4m SUV segment and the flat load floor makes loading awkward items straightforward. Ventilated seats for both front passengers and a wireless charger add daily convenience. The single petrol powertrain option limits versatility for buyers who want diesel economy on longer routes.
The Exter's taller body and wider door apertures make it easier to load child seats and get elderly passengers in and out. The roof rails are functional, adding genuine carrying capacity for weekend trips. The absence of a diesel option is shared with the Kylaq, and the smaller boot is the one area where the Exter asks buyers to compromise.
