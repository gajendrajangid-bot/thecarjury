Tata Harrier EV vs

Choose the
Tata Harrier EV
  • You regularly load up the car with family, luggage and occasional camping gear and need genuine off-road clearance to get there.
  • You live in a city where a familiar, imposing SUV silhouette matters at valet parking and school gates.
  • You want a lifetime battery warranty and the peace of mind that Tata's nationwide service network provides.
  • You drive on broken state highways and need a suspension tune that absorbs poor surfaces without drama.
  • You have two drivers at home and need ventilated seats with memory functions on both sides.
  • You occasionally tow or wade through monsoon flooding and need a 600 mm wading depth you can trust.
Choose the
Mahindra BE6
  • You want a car that turns heads at every traffic light and are willing to accept a firmer ride as the trade-off.
  • You commute 60-80 km daily and want a verified 450-500 km real-world range to avoid charge anxiety on long weekends.
  • You are a single buyer or a couple without children, so the tight rear seat and coupe roofline are not daily problems.
  • You care about driving feel: sharp steering, semi-active dampers and rear-wheel-drive balance matter to you more than boot space.
  • Your budget is closer to Rs 19-22 lakh and you want maximum technology and performance per rupee.
  • You want a cabin that feels like a sci-fi film set, with an AR Rahman-tuned infinity roof and a 1400W Harman Kardon system.
Where They Diverge
Four situations that tip the decision

Both score 7.5/10. In real life, they are built for different people.

Weekend highway run above 300 km

The BE6's 450-500 km real-world range means most weekend trips complete on a single charge. The Harrier EV returns 380-420 km in mixed driving, so a short top-up becomes part of the itinerary. On 120 kW DC charging, the Harrier's stops are reasonably brief, but the BE6 simply stops less often.

Edge: Mahindra BE6
Monsoon road with broken surfaces and waterlogging

The Harrier EV's Land Rover-derived platform, 600 mm wading depth and Quad Wheel Drive give it a measurable advantage on degraded roads. MotorBeam found the QWD system tackled Rajmachi's off-road trail without drama. The BE6 handles rain-soaked tarmac confidently, but it is a rear-wheel-drive coupe-SUV built for roads, not trails.

Edge: Tata Harrier EV
Daily urban commute with stop-start traffic

The BE6's lower starting price, longer range and sharp throttle response make it an efficient and engaging city car. The Harrier EV's instant torque from its dual-motor setup is equally quick off the line, but its larger footprint makes tight parking bays more stressful. Faisal Khan notes the BE6's compact coupe proportions help in congested urban blocks.

Edge: Mahindra BE6
Resale value in three to five years

Tata has a longer track record in the Indian EV market, an established service network and the emotional assurance of a lifetime battery warranty, all of which historically support resale confidence. Mahindra's long-term EV resale data is still thin, and RevLimits acknowledges the brand carries lingering question marks around service consistency. Buyers who keep cars for five-plus years will weigh this carefully.

Edge: Tata Harrier EV

What the jury said, head-to-head

Design
The Harrier EV wears its identity carefully: a closed grille, connected LED DRLs with sequential indicators and 19-inch aero alloys separate it from the diesel car, but the silhouette is shared. Gagan Choudhary calls it confident rather than adventurous. MotorOctane's paint gauge recorded an average thickness of 277 microns, which is meaningfully more than the competition.
7.5 / 10
Every reviewer agrees the BE6 looks like nothing else on Indian roads. Faisal Khan calls the design 'straight out of the year 2050,' and Manish Bhardwaj recounts owners in Rajasthan being stunned to learn it was a Mahindra. MotorOctane notes it grabs supercar-level attention at every stop.
9.0 / 10
Statement-seekersBE6 draws crowds the Harrier simply cannot match
Interior
The Harrier EV's 14.53-inch Samsung NeoQLED screen earns genuine praise for UI quality, and the 10-speaker JBL system with Dolby Atmos tuning is well-executed. Namaste Car notes the soft-touch materials feel premium, though piano black trim attracts fingerprints. Ventilated front seats with memory and a four-way powered passenger seat add daily comfort.
7.5 / 10
The BE6 cabin is pure theatre: twin 12.3-inch screens, aeroplane-inspired switchgear and a thrust-lever drive selector create a race-cockpit atmosphere. RushLane praises the sports-car-like seats and generous soft-touch surfaces. The 16-speaker, 1400W Harman Kardon system and AR Rahman-tuned interactive ambient lighting are segment firsts.
7.5 / 10
Tech enthusiastsBE6's cockpit experience is more immersive and theatrical
Performance
The QWD Harrier EV pairs a front induction motor with a rear permanent-magnet motor for 390 hp and 504 Nm, hitting 100 km/h in 6.3 seconds. MotorBeam found the instant torque genuinely useful on the old Mumbai-Pune highway. The AWD traction advantage over slippery or loose surfaces is real and measurable.
8.5 / 10
The BE6 produces 282 hp from a single rear motor and reaches 100 km/h in 6.7 seconds, fractionally slower on paper. What it delivers instead is a sharp, driver-focused experience: semi-active dampers, a low centre of gravity and precise steering that RevLimits calls the most engaging RWD setup in this segment.
8.5 / 10
All-surface driversHarrier's QWD wins on mixed terrain; BE6 wins on driver feel
Ride Quality
The Harrier EV's Land Rover-derived platform absorbs broken surfaces with composure. V3Cars notes the suspension tune prioritises comfort on scarred state highways without becoming floaty at speed. Rear passenger comfort is a genuine strength on long family drives.
8.0 / 10
The BE6's semi-active dampers sharpen cornering but firm up the ride noticeably on city potholes and speed breakers. MotorOctane flagged the stiff setup as a daily-use compromise. Buyers who carry rear passengers over poor roads will feel every imperfection more than they would in the Harrier.
7.0 / 10
Families on long tripsHarrier's comfort tune suits multi-passenger journeys on varied roads
Build Quality
Tata's 277-micron average paint thickness, measured by MotorOctane, signals serious panel investment. The 5-star Bharat NCAP rating and Land Rover-borrowed platform architecture give structural credibility. Cabin plastics are a mixed bag: the touch points are good but deeper in the dashboard materials thin out.
7.5 / 10
The BE6 presents well at the touch points that matter: soft-touch surfaces, quality seat materials and solid switchgear. RushLane found panel gaps consistent across the test car. Long-term durability data is still limited given the model's recent launch, and Mahindra's service quality remains an open question for some reviewers.
7.5 / 10
Long-term ownersTata's track record and thicker paint point to stronger long-term build confidence
Value for Money
At Rs 21.49 lakh to Rs 28.99 lakh ex-showroom, the Harrier EV asks a premium over the BE6 for QWD, a larger battery and the lifetime warranty. V3Cars notes the mid-spec variants offer reasonable value, but the top-end QWD trim climbs close to Rs 29 lakh, where the competition stiffens.
7.5 / 10
Starting at Rs 18.90 lakh ex-showroom, the BE6 delivers 282 hp, 450-500 km real-world range and a theatre-grade cabin at a price the Harrier EV cannot match at entry level. MotorOctane calls it disruptive pricing for a car that punches well above its segment on design and range.
8.5 / 10
Budget-conscious buyersBE6 offers more range and drama per rupee at the entry point
Real-World Range
The 75 kWh battery returns 380-420 km in mixed driving. DC fast charging tops out at 120 kW, which is adequate but not class-leading. For most buyers this range is sufficient; for those planning frequent 400 km-plus highway runs it requires one planned stop.
Reviewers consistently record 450-500 km in real-world mixed use. The combination of a larger usable battery and an aerodynamically efficient coupe body keeps consumption lower at highway speeds. For buyers who want to drive confidently between major cities without a charging plan, the BE6's range buffer is a practical daily advantage.
