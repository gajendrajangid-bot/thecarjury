Maruti Brezza vs
Tata Nexon

Choose between India's most trusted name and its most complete compact SUV package.

Same price. Different life.

Most buyers decide here. Read this before anything else.

Choose the
Maruti Brezza
  • You live in a Tier-2 or Tier-3 city where Maruti's service network means a workshop is never more than 10 kilometres away.
  • You plan to sell the car within four years and want the strongest resale value in the segment, full stop.
  • You cover mostly urban and highway distances and want a smooth, fuss-free automatic that never makes you think about the gearbox.
  • You have ageing parents or young children in the back seat and prioritise a supple, forgiving ride over outright driving excitement.
  • You want a feature-complete cabin, a head-up display and wireless connectivity without paying a turbo premium.
  • You are a first-time car owner who values low anxiety ownership: predictable fuel costs, wide parts availability and a familiar brand.
Choose the
Tata Nexon
  • You regularly carry four adults on long highway runs and want a diesel engine that genuinely rewards overtaking manoeuvres.
  • You live in a metro with CNG infrastructure and want the only turbocharged factory CNG in the segment for running-cost discipline without sacrificing performance.
  • Road safety is non-negotiable for you and a 5-star Global NCAP rating is the first filter you apply before considering any other feature.
  • You drive in hilly terrain or on broken state highways where a torquier, more responsive powertrain makes a real difference to confidence.
  • You want the sharper, more contemporary exterior design and a dual-screen dashboard that reads visually premium to passengers.
  • You are buying your second car and want diesel or DCA gearbox options that the Brezza simply does not offer.
Where They Diverge
Four situations that tip the decision

Both score 7.4/10. In real life, they are built for different people.

Long highway run with four adults

The Nexon's turbo-petrol and diesel both build highway momentum with genuine urgency; MotorBeam noted the diesel's 260 Nm torque makes overtaking feel effortless even at 100 kph. The Brezza's 103 PS naturally aspirated engine gets the job done but requires more planning at high speeds. For family road trips with a full load, the Nexon is the more relaxed long-distance tool.

Edge: Tata Nexon
Daily city commute, ownership anxiety, resale

Namaste Car specifically flagged the Brezza's Maruti badge as a peace-of-mind multiplier in cities without large Tata dealer networks. Resale premiums for Maruti in the used-car market are well documented and buyers typically recover more value at the three-year mark. For buyers who treat their car as a depreciating asset to be managed, the Brezza's ecosystem advantage is real money.

Edge: Maruti Brezza
Safety-first purchase decision

The Nexon carries a 5-star Global NCAP rating, the only car in this price band with that credential, and Gagan Choudhary called it a genuine differentiator for safety-conscious families. The Brezza offers six airbags on top trims, which is commendable, but its NCAP result does not match the Nexon's score. If a crash-test number is your primary filter, the Nexon wins this conversation before it starts.

Edge: Tata Nexon
Running cost discipline over five years

The Brezza's 1.5-litre petrol with mild-hybrid assistance returns competitive fuel economy and its service costs are among the lowest in the segment, as MotorOctane has consistently noted in ownership reviews. The Nexon's turbocharged CNG variant introduces a compelling third option: near-petrol performance with CNG running costs. For pure five-year cost-of-ownership in petrol-only trim, the Brezza holds an edge; the CNG Nexon changes that calculus entirely for metro CNG users.

Edge: Tie
Dimension by Dimension
What the jury said, head-to-head

Scores shown inline. "Best for" tells you who each result matters to.

Axis Maruti Brezza Tata Nexon Best for
Design
The facelifted Brezza adopts a cleaner, less chrome-heavy face with LED projector headlamps and dual DRL strips that read genuinely modern. Arun Panwar noted the redesigned C-pillar and precision-cut alloys make the car appear larger than its footprint. It is a resolved, conservative update that will not polarise.
7.5 / 10
The Nexon's facelifted front, with tri-arrow DRLs and a connected LED tail-lamp signature, is the boldest design statement in the segment. Biturbo Media called it the most resolved-looking Tata yet. The Union Jack tail-lamp motif divides opinion but guarantees the car is noticed.
8.0 / 10
Style-forward buyersNexon's sharper detailing reads more premium at the kerb
Interior
The Brezza's cabin gets a layered dashboard with brown inserts, a flat-bottom steering and a 9-inch SmartPlay Pro+ touchscreen with wireless CarPlay, plus a colour HUD that rivals rarely offer. Namaste Car praised the ambient lighting and sunroof combination for making the cabin feel upmarket. Ergonomics are intuitive and the physical climate controls are a daily-use advantage.
7.0 / 10
The Nexon counters with a 10.25-inch floating touchscreen and a matching 10.25-inch digital cluster, creating a dual-screen layout that looks genuinely contemporary. MotorBeam rated the front seat support and ventilation on long drives as a class highlight. Touch-based climate controls look slick but attract criticism for eyes-off operation.
7.5 / 10
Tech-focused commutersNexon's dual-screen layout sets a higher visual benchmark
Performance
The 1.5-litre K15C with 103 PS and mild-hybrid assistance is refined and smooth but uninspiring when pressed. The new 6-speed torque converter automatic is a clear improvement over the old 4-speed unit. Gagan Choudhary described the engine as perfectly adequate for city use but flagged that it runs out of breath on fast highway stretches.
6.5 / 10
The Nexon's 120 PS turbo-petrol delivers 170 Nm from low in the rev range, and the 1.5 diesel's 260 Nm makes it the performance leader in this segment. Faisal Khan called the diesel the enthusiast's pick for its highway composure and overtaking reserve. The DCA gearbox on petrol trims adds a dual-clutch option the Brezza cannot match.
7.5 / 10
Highway and hilly terrain driversNexon's turbo and diesel options offer decisively more usable power
Ride Quality
The Brezza's suspension tune is one of its strongest suits, absorbing broken urban roads and highway undulations with composure. MotorOctane specifically praised the rear suspension's ability to filter sharp-edged bumps without unsettling passengers. It earns its reputation as a comfortable family car here.
8.0 / 10
The Nexon matches the Brezza closely in ride quality, with its 209 mm ground clearance helping it absorb obstacles confidently. Arun Panwar noted the ride is well-sorted at highway speeds and that the Bridgestone Turanza tyres contribute to a quiet cabin at cruise. Neither car has a meaningful advantage over the other on this axis.
8.0 / 10
Comfort-seeking familiesBoth deliver genuinely good ride quality; choose on other factors
Build Quality
Panel gaps on the facelifted Brezza are consistent and the body feels solid, a standard Maruti has maintained well. The interior plastics on lower trims feel cost-managed, but top trims benefit from the leatherette and soft-touch surfaces. Reviewers agree it feels put-together rather than plush.
7.5 / 10
The Nexon's 5-star NCAP body structure is the most concrete evidence of its build integrity. Biturbo Media noted the door closure sound and panel rigidity feel a step above the Brezza. Interior material quality has improved noticeably with the facelift, though hard plastics still appear in visible lower-dash areas.
7.5 / 10
Safety-conscious buyersNexon's crash-tested body structure provides documented structural confidence
Value for Money
The Brezza's value case rests on low service costs, high resale value and a feature list that includes a HUD and six airbags at competitive price points. MotorBeam noted that total cost of ownership over five years, including depreciation, favours Maruti buyers. It is not the cheapest to buy but among the cheapest to run and sell.
7.0 / 10
The Nexon's 36-variant range means buyers can enter the lineup at Rs. 8 lakh or build a fully loaded diesel automatic near Rs. 15 lakh, covering almost every budget. Namaste Car highlighted the diesel and CNG options as genuine value multipliers for high-mileage users. The breadth of choice makes the Nexon's value argument highly personal and use-case dependent.
7.5 / 10
Resale-focused buyersBrezza's Maruti badge delivers stronger long-term asset value
Practicality
The Brezza's boxy silhouette maximises cabin volume within its sub-4-metre footprint, and rear headroom is generous for a car this size. Boot space is competitive and the 60:40 split rear seat adds flexibility. The wide Maruti service network means the car is practical not just in cabin terms but in ownership terms too.
The Nexon offers a panoramic sunroof on top variants, ventilated front seats and a powertrain range including CNG that adds genuine daily-use flexibility. Ground clearance of 209 mm means it handles bad roads and waterlogged streets with more confidence than most rivals. For buyers who want options, the Nexon's variant depth is its own form of practicality.
Multi-use urban familiesNexon's powertrain variety and features cover more life situations
