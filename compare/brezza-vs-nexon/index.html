Maruti Brezza vs
Tata Nexon

Reliable everyday ownership versus turbocharged performance and class-leading safety credentials.

The Car Jury
9 independent creators
May 2026
For: This comparison is built for first-time SUV buyers in the Rs. 10-15 lakh range choosing between a Maruti badge's long-term peace of mind and a more dynamic, safety-focused alternative. If you want a premium cabin or a sunroof-and-turbo combo above Rs. 15 lakh, look at the Creta or Seltos instead.

Most buyers decide here. Read this before anything else.

Choose the
Maruti Brezza
  • You live in a smaller city where a Maruti service centre is around the corner and downtime genuinely costs you.
  • You plan to sell in three to four years and want the most predictable resale curve in the segment.
  • You drive mostly within city limits and value a smooth, unstressed engine over outright punch.
  • Your household runs on tight fuel budgets and the Brezza's mild-hybrid efficiency matters more than performance figures.
  • You want a feature-loaded cabin — HUD, wireless charging, sunroof — without the complexity of a turbo powertrain.
  • You are buying for a senior family member who finds simpler, lighter controls and a refined NVH environment more comfortable on daily drives.
Choose the
Tata Nexon
  • You regularly drive on highways or hilly terrain and need a powertrain that responds when you ask for it.
  • Child safety is non-negotiable and India's only 5-star Global NCAP rating in this price band settles the question for you.
  • You run high monthly kilometres and want the diesel variant's torque and efficiency on long inter-city hauls.
  • CNG prices make financial sense for your commute but you refuse to compromise on drivability, making the turbocharged i-CNG your natural choice.
  • You value a larger 10.25-inch dual-screen cabin setup and a panoramic sunroof that feel closer to the segment above.
  • You are an enthusiast on a budget who wants a car that rewards spirited driving without stepping up to a more expensive segment.
Where They Diverge
Four situations that tip the decision

Both score 7.4/10. In real life, they are built for different people.

Daily city commute under 30 km

The Brezza's 1.5-litre NA petrol is smooth, refined and entirely unstressed in stop-and-go traffic. The mild-hybrid system trims fuel bills without asking the driver to think about it. MotorOctane noted the new 6-speed automatic makes city crawling genuinely relaxing, which is exactly what most Brezza buyers need.

Edge: Maruti Brezza
Weekend highway run above 120 km/h

The Nexon's 1.2 turbo-petrol delivers 170 Nm from low in the rev range, making overtaking confident and unhurried. Arun Panwar pointed out the Brezza starts feeling breathless above 100 km/h, where the turbo gap becomes noticeable. Buyers who regularly cover long distances will feel the Nexon's composure on fast roads.

Edge: Tata Nexon
Five-year ownership cost and resale

Maruti's service network and spare-parts economy give the Brezza a clear structural advantage over a five-year horizon. Namaste Car highlighted that Brezza resale values consistently outperform segment rivals by a meaningful margin. Nexon ownership costs are manageable, but Tata's service network still trails Maruti in tier-2 and tier-3 towns.

Edge: Maruti Brezza
Driving with young children on board

The Nexon holds India's only 5-star Global NCAP rating in this segment, giving parents a data-backed safety assurance no rival currently matches. Biturbo Media emphasised that the body structure impressed in offset and side-impact tests. The Brezza offers six airbags on top trims, which is creditable, but it has not achieved an equivalent NCAP score.

Edge: Tata Nexon
Dimension by Dimension
What the jury said, head-to-head

Design
The facelift gives the Brezza a cleaner, less chrome-heavy face with LED projector headlamps and dual DRL strips. Precision-cut 16-inch alloys and a redesigned C-pillar add visual bulk without straying from the honest boxy SUV identity. Gagan Choudhary called it a measured improvement that broadens appeal without chasing trends.
7.5 / 10
The facelifted Nexon is the most resolved-looking Tata yet, with slim LED headlamps, tri-arrow DRLs borrowed from the EV line and a connected rear lamp signature. MotorBeam noted the Union Jack tail-lamp motif polarises opinion but the overall stance is genuinely road-commanding for a sub-4m car.
8.0 / 10
Bold-statement seekersNexon carries stronger visual character at the kerb
Interior
Brown inserts, a leather flat-bottom steering and a 9-inch SmartPlay Pro+ touchscreen lift the Brezza's cabin above its predecessor. Wireless CarPlay, a colour HUD and ambient lighting are genuinely useful additions. MotorBeam noted the layout is practical and well-resolved, even if material depth trails a tier above.
7.0 / 10
The Nexon's dual 10.25-inch screens, panoramic sunroof and ventilated front seats create a premium impression that punches above the segment. Faisal Khan highlighted that plastics and leatherette quality have improved considerably with the facelift. Minor ergonomic niggles around the touch climate panel remain an irritant on longer drives.
7.5 / 10
Tech-forward buyersNexon's dual-screen setup feels closer to a segment above
Performance
The 103 PS K15C mild-hybrid is smooth and refined in the city and the new 6-speed automatic is a genuine upgrade over the old unit. Arun Panwar noted NVH is well controlled at urban speeds. On open roads, the absence of turbo boost makes the Brezza feel pedestrian when drivers demand quick overtakes.
6.5 / 10
At 120 PS and 170 Nm, the turbo-petrol gives the Nexon real mid-range authority. The diesel adds 260 Nm and suits buyers who cover long distances regularly. Namaste Car rated the DCA-paired turbo-petrol as the most satisfying gearbox in the segment, with quick responses and minimal turbo lag in everyday use.
7.5 / 10
Driving enthusiastsNexon's turbo gap is decisive on highways and gradients
Ride Quality
The Brezza's suspension tuning is one of its strongest suits, absorbing broken tarmac and speed breakers with composure that surprises first-time drivers. MotorOctane consistently praised its ability to filter urban road chaos without disturbing cabin occupants. It remains among the most forgiving rides in the sub-4m class.
8.0 / 10
The Nexon rides on a well-sorted suspension that handles highway undulations and city potholes with equal confidence. Gagan Choudhary found the 209 mm ground clearance useful on rough roads without introducing excessive body roll on faster bends. Both cars share this axis closely, making ride quality a tie in practical terms.
8.0 / 10
Rear-seat commutersBoth absorb bad roads well; Brezza has a fractional NVH edge
Build Quality
The Brezza's panel gaps and paint quality are consistent with Maruti's manufacturing standards, which Indian buyers have trusted for decades. Door thud and overall tactile solidity feel appropriate for the price. MotorBeam noted the cabin hard plastics are better than Maruti's budget models but stop short of class-leading density.
7.5 / 10
Tata's 5-star Global NCAP body structure signals a step forward in structural integrity, backed by improved soft-touch surfaces inside the facelift cabin. Biturbo Media pointed out the Nexon's doors shut with a reassuring weight. Exterior panel quality has tightened since the pre-facelift generation, narrowing the perception gap with rivals.
7.5 / 10
Safety-conscious familiesNexon's NCAP-validated structure offers measurable reassurance
Value for Money
The Brezza's value case rests on low ownership costs, strong resale and Maruti's service network rather than an aggressive sticker price. Feature loading on top trims is strong, with HUD and six airbags justifying the ask. Namaste Car concluded the total cost of ownership over four years remains the Brezza's most compelling selling point.
7.0 / 10
The Nexon's 36-variant spread means buyers can enter close to Rs. 8 lakh or spec a fully loaded diesel below Rs. 16 lakh, covering almost every budget. Faisal Khan highlighted that the diesel and turbo-CNG variants offer running cost advantages that partially offset the Maruti service-network edge. The sheer spread of choice adds functional value.
7.5 / 10
Total-cost calculatorsBrezza's resale curve lowers long-term cost of ownership
Powertrain Flexibility
The Brezza offers one engine in two gearbox choices. That simplicity works for most buyers but leaves CNG and diesel customers without an option. MotorOctane's CNG comparison noted the Brezza CNG is an accessible alternative, though it lacks the turbocharged edge the Nexon brings to the same fuel type.
Three powertrains, including India's most powerful factory CNG, give the Nexon genuine segment breadth. Diesel buyers, turbo-petrol enthusiasts and CNG commuters all find a variant that fits their specific need. Arun Panwar described the turbocharged i-CNG as a genuine shift for CNG buyers who previously accepted dull performance as a given.
Multi-use householdsNexon's fuel and gearbox spread covers far more buyer profiles
