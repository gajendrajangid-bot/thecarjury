Mahindra BE6 vs

Choose the driver's coupe or the family flagship: same platform, very different lives.

Choose the
Mahindra BE6
  • You drive solo or with one passenger most days and want a car that rewards you for it every single time.
  • You care deeply about how your car looks and want strangers to stop and photograph it in parking lots.
  • You live in a metro, charge at home overnight and treat 450 km of real range as genuinely sufficient.
  • You have a budget closer to Rs 19-22 lakh and want the most performance per rupee in the segment.
  • You are a confident driver who enjoys sharp steering and does not mind trading rear-seat comfort for dynamics.
  • You already own a second car for family duty and this is your personal, expressive daily driver.
Choose the
Mahindra XEV 9e
  • You regularly carry three or four adults and need rear passengers to arrive without complaining.
  • You want the main screen to double as a front-passenger entertainment system on long road trips.
  • You prioritise a settled, composed ride over a sporty, communicative one on broken city roads.
  • You are buying this as the household's only car and need it to handle school runs, highway trips and weekend getaways equally well.
  • You value a slightly more conservative silhouette that reads as premium without drawing a crowd at every stoplight.
  • You can wait six to twelve months for software updates to mature before taking delivery.
Where They Diverge
Four situations that tip the decision

Both score 8.0/10. In real life, they are built for different people.

Mumbai to Pune highway run

The BE6's rear-wheel-drive setup and semi-active dampers make high-speed lane changes feel taut and confidence-inspiring, a trait MotorOctane highlighted as genuinely sports-car-like. The XEV 9e's longer wheelbase and softer damper tune feel more settled at sustained cruising speeds but less involving. Both cars offer similar real-world range, so the choice is whether you want to feel the road or float above it.

Edge: Mahindra BE6
Daily family use with rear passengers

The XEV 9e's longer body and higher roofline give rear occupants noticeably more headroom under the coupe slope than the BE6 offers. Faisal Khan noted the BE6's rear seat feels tight for adults on longer journeys. If your back-seat passengers are adults rather than children, the XEV 9e is the more considerate choice.

Edge: Mahindra XEV 9e
Tech and in-cabin experience

The XEV 9e counters with three 12.3-inch screens, a dedicated passenger display for streaming and Dolby Atmos playback, giving it a genuine edge as a rolling living room. The BE6 responds with a 1400W Harman Kardon system and AR Rahman-tuned ambient lighting, but its twin-screen layout is less generous for front-passenger entertainment. MotorOctane rates the XEV 9e's cabin ambition as a class benchmark.

Edge: Mahindra XEV 9e
First-year ownership risk

Both cars share the same INGLO platform and Mahindra's still-developing EV service network, so neither gets a clean pass here. Reviewers across both tests flagged software niggles and service readiness as open questions. The BE6 has been in owner hands slightly longer, giving it a marginal data advantage, but both buyers should budget for software-update patience in year one.

Edge: Tie
Dimension by Dimension
What the jury said, head-to-head

Design
Faisal Khan called the BE6 'straight out of the year 2050,' and Manish Bhardwaj documented owners in Rajasthan mistaking it for a supercar. The coupe-SUV silhouette, aerodynamic body and concept-car detailing are polarising only in the sense that some people will love it too loudly.
9.0 / 10
The XEV 9e's full-width LED bar, flush handles and active aero curtains give it genuine road presence. The sloping roofline is where reviewer opinions fracture: some read it as sleek, others as a compromise between SUV and coupe that satisfies neither brief completely.
7.0 / 10
Statement seekersBE6 generates supercar-level attention that the XEV 9e simply cannot match
Interior
The BE6 cabin is a race-cockpit exercise: twin 12.3-inch screens, a thrust-lever drive selector, aeroplane-inspired switchgear and a halo around the driver create theatre. RushLane praises the sports seats and generous soft-touch surfaces, and MotorOctane appreciates the 1400W audio system.
7.5 / 10
Three 12.3-inch screens, a dedicated passenger streaming display, Dolby Atmos audio and AR Rahman sound profiles make the XEV 9e the more complete interior for occupants beyond the driver. MotorOctane rates the tech density here as a class benchmark.
8.0 / 10
Families and co-pilotsThree screens and a passenger display make the XEV 9e a proper shared-space cabin
Performance
282 BHP, rear-wheel drive and a 6.7-second 0-100 time sit at the heart of the BE6's identity. Semi-active dampers and sharp steering mean the power is usable and rewarding rather than just impressive on paper. MotorOctane calls the dynamics genuinely sports-car-like for a mass-market EV.
8.5 / 10
The XEV 9e's 281 BHP Pack 3 matches the BE6 almost identically on paper and covers 0-100 in 6.8 seconds. Mahindra has deliberately tuned power delivery to feel linear and smooth across modes rather than violent, which suits family cruising but sacrifices the edge-of-seat quality the BE6 delivers.
8.0 / 10
Driver-focused buyersBE6's rear-drive chassis turns similar power into a more rewarding experience
Ride Quality
The BE6's semi-active dampers are tuned for body control over compliance. Reviewers consistently flag a firm ride on broken urban roads as the clearest everyday trade-off. It is acceptable in Comfort mode but never cosseting.
7.0 / 10
The XEV 9e's longer wheelbase and softer damper calibration absorb urban imperfections more gracefully. At 7.5 against the BE6's 7.0 on the Jury score, the gap is real enough to matter if your daily commute involves rough surfaces and rear passengers.
7.5 / 10
City commuters with passengersXEV 9e's softer tune is noticeably more forgiving on broken roads
Build Quality
Reviewers score the BE6 at 7.5, acknowledging solid panel gaps and good material quality inside, while noting that Mahindra's long-term reliability record on EVs remains an open question. Early production quality is encouraging but unproven over time.
7.5 / 10
The XEV 9e scores 7.0 on build, with reviewers pointing to unfinished software and some interior fit concerns at launch. V3Cars and Namaste Car both suggest waiting for post-launch updates to stabilise the ownership experience before committing.
7.0 / 10
Cautious early adoptersBE6 holds a marginal edge on initial build confidence, though neither car is proven long-term
Value for Money
Starting at Rs 18.90 lakh ex-showroom, the BE6 offers 282 BHP, a 1400W sound system, semi-active dampers and concept-car design at a price that undercuts most comparable EVs globally. The Jury scores it 8.5, the highest single dimension in this comparison.
8.5 / 10
The XEV 9e's three-screen layout, Dolby Atmos audio and flagship tech stack at its price point represent strong value for a tech-led buyer. At 8.0, it scores well, but the BE6's lower entry price and higher performance output give it a sharper value argument.
8.0 / 10
Performance-per-rupee seekersBE6's starting price and hardware list are hard to beat in the segment
Range and Charging
The BE6 delivers 450-500 km of real-world range and supports DC fast charging, making long highway runs genuinely viable. MotorOctane's testing confirmed the range claim holds up in mixed driving conditions. For a daily-driver EV, this removes range anxiety from the equation.
The XEV 9e's 79 kWh Pack 3 offers comparable real-world range with the same INGLO platform charging architecture. The 59 kWh entry variant trades some range for a lower price. Both cars are equally dependent on Mahindra's fast-charging network expansion, which remains the shared wildcard for both buyers.
